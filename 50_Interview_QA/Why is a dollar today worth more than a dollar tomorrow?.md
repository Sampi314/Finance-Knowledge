# Why is a dollar today worth more than a dollar tomorrow?

## The question

Why is a dollar today worth more than a dollar tomorrow?

## Short answer (30 seconds)

A dollar today is worth more because of the time value of money, which is driven by the opportunity cost of capital. If you have a dollar today, you can invest it immediately to earn a return—such as interest or investment yield. Therefore, a dollar received in the future is intrinsically less valuable because you miss out on that earning potential while you wait for it.

## Long answer (2 minutes)

1.  **Opportunity Cost:** The core principle is opportunity cost. Money has earning capacity. If you receive capital today, you can invest it in assets that generate a return over time. If you receive it a year from now, you lose a year's worth of potential returns.
2.  **Risk:** There is always uncertainty about the future. Receiving cash today guarantees you have it, whereas cash promised in the future carries the risk that it may not actually be paid due to default or other unforeseen events. Investors demand compensation for taking on this risk, which is reflected in the discount rate used to value future cash flows.
3.  **Inflation:** While inflation reduces the purchasing power of money over time, it is not the primary reason a dollar today is worth more. Even in an environment with zero inflation, the time value of money exists purely because of the lost opportunity to invest and earn a return.

## Common follow-ups

- Isn't this just because of inflation? (No, inflation plays a role in purchasing power, but the primary driver of the time value of money in finance is the opportunity cost of not being able to invest the capital and earn a return.)
- How do we quantify this difference in valuation models? (We use a discount rate—often representing the expected return on investment for assets of similar risk—to discount future cash flows back to their present value.)
