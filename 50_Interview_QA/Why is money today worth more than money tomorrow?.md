# Why is money today worth more than money tomorrow?

Money today is worth more than the same amount of money tomorrow primarily due to the "opportunity cost" of capital. If you have the money today, you can invest it to earn a return, such as interest. By having to wait for the money, you forgo those potential earnings. Furthermore, you face inflation (which decreases purchasing power) and the risk that the future money may not be paid as promised. Therefore, future cash flows must be discounted to accurately reflect their present value.
