# What is the difference between PIK toggle and full PIK debt?

## The question

What is the difference between PIK toggle and full PIK debt?

## Short answer (30 seconds)

Full PIK debt requires that interest payments be made entirely in-kind, meaning the interest is mandantly added to the principal balance each period without a cash option. In contrast, PIK toggle debt gives the borrower the option to choose, period-by-period, whether to pay the interest in cash or defer it by paying in-kind.

## Long answer (2 minutes)

- **Full PIK (Mandatory PIK)**:
  - Requires all interest to be paid in-kind, rolling the interest expense into the loan's principal balance rather than being paid out in cash.
  - Typically used in subordinated or mezzanine debt tranches.
  - Most appropriate when a borrower genuinely cannot or should not pay cash interest because capital needs to be directed towards operations or growth investments.
- **PIK toggle**:
  - Provides the borrower with the flexibility to elect either cash or PIK interest payments.
  - The PIK option usually comes at a premium; the PIK interest rate is often 100-200 basis points higher than the cash interest rate to compensate lenders for deferred payment and compounding risk.
  - The toggle option can be purely at the borrower's discretion, conditional (e.g., tied to leverage or liquidity thresholds), or time-limited (e.g., available only for the first two years).
