# Why is money today worth more than money tomorrow

## The question

Why is money today worth more than money tomorrow?

## Short answer (30 seconds)

Money today is worth more than money tomorrow because of the opportunity cost associated with time. If you have a dollar today, you can invest it and earn a return, such as interest, so it will grow to more than a dollar tomorrow. Conversely, a dollar received tomorrow does not provide that opportunity to earn a return in the interim.

## Long answer (2 minutes)

The fundamental reason money today is worth more than money tomorrow is the concept of opportunity cost.

- **Investment potential:** Any cash you hold today can be put to work immediately. For instance, you can place it in a savings account, buy bonds, or invest in a business. By doing so, that cash generates a return (interest or dividends) over time. Therefore, $100 today could become $105 in a year.
- **Discounting future cash flows:** Because of this earning potential, we must "discount" money received in the future to understand its value today. We use a discount rate that reflects our expected return on investment (our opportunity cost) to calculate the Present Value of those future cash flows.
- **Inflation is a secondary factor:** While inflation does erode purchasing power, meaning a dollar tomorrow will likely buy less than a dollar today, the primary driver of the time value of money in finance is the opportunity to earn a return. Even in a theoretical world with zero inflation, money today is still worth more due to its investment potential.

## Common follow-ups

- **How do you calculate the present value?** Explain that present value is calculated by dividing the future cash flow by (1 + discount rate) raised to the power of the number of periods.
- **What discount rate should you use?** State that the discount rate should reflect the risk of the cash flows. For a whole company (unlevered free cash flow), you would typically use the Weighted Average Cost of Capital (WACC).
