# Why do you assume that construction loans are refinanced and replaced with permanent loans when the construction finishes?

## The question

Why do you assume that construction loans are refinanced and replaced with permanent loans when the construction finishes?

## Short answer (30 seconds)

Construction loans are riskier and carry higher interest rates, so they attract lenders with a higher risk tolerance. Once a property is built and stabilized, it can be refinanced with a permanent loan that has a lower interest rate. This matches the stabilized property with lenders seeking lower-risk assets and allows equity investors to realize a cash inflow if the property's value has increased.

## Long answer (2 minutes)

1. **Risk and Lender Profile**: Construction loans inherently carry more risk because the property is not yet generating income and faces construction risks. Thus, they have higher interest rates. Permanent loans are meant for stabilized properties and have lower interest rates, attracting a different set of lenders.
2. **Equity Returns**: Real estate loan refinancings are highly favored by equity investors because they can boost returns. When a property stabilizes, its value often increases. The property can then take on additional debt based on this higher value. The difference between the new permanent loan and the old repaid debt is distributed to equity investors as a cash inflow.
