# What is a control premium and why do acquirers pay it?

## The question

What is a control premium and why do acquirers pay it?

## Short answer (30 seconds)

A control premium is the amount an acquirer pays above a target's current trading price to acquire a controlling stake, typically ranging from 25-40% for public companies. Acquirers pay this premium for the right to direct the company's future, allocating cash flow and setting strategy in ways minority shareholders cannot. Furthermore, they pay it to gain access to synergies and strategic benefits that require operational control to capture.

## Long answer (2 minutes)

A control premium is the additional amount an acquirer pays above a target company's current, unaffected market value to acquire a controlling ownership stake. It represents the value of shifting from a passive, minority investor position to a controlling one.

There are three main reasons acquirers pay a control premium:

1. **Control over cash flows and strategy:** A controlling shareholder has the authority to make major decisions that minority shareholders cannot. They can choose to pay dividends, reinvest in the business, pay down debt, alter the cost structure, or change the strategic direction of the company.
2. **Access to synergies:** Buyers often expect to realize synergies from the acquisition, such as eliminating duplicate corporate overhead (cost synergies) or cross-selling products across a combined customer base (revenue synergies). These benefits cannot be achieved without operational control, justifying a premium to unlock them.
3. **Strategic and competitive value:** Ownership control can provide critical competitive advantages, such as preventing a rival from acquiring the target, securing proprietary technology or intellectual property, and gaining immediate entry into new markets.

The premium fundamentally values the right to direct the company's future and extract additional economic value, rather than simply passively holding minority shares.

## Common follow-ups

- **How do you calculate it?** You calculate it as the offer price minus the unaffected share price, divided by the unaffected share price. The key is using the *unaffected* price—the trading price before any takeover speculation inflated the stock.
- **Why do strategic buyers typically pay higher premiums than financial buyers?** Strategic buyers (corporations) can capture operational synergies, meaning the target is genuinely worth more to them combined with their existing operations. Financial buyers (private equity) typically cannot realize these operating synergies and focus primarily on achievable financial returns, limiting what they can pay.
