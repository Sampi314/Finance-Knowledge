# What would you sensitize in a DCF

## The question

What would you sensitize in a DCF?

## Short answer (30 seconds)

In a DCF, the most important variables to sensitize are the Weighted Average Cost of Capital (WACC) against the Terminal Growth Rate (if using the Gordon Growth method) or WACC against the Exit Multiple. This is because the terminal value typically accounts for 60-80% of the total enterprise value, making the model highly sensitive to these assumptions.

## Long answer (2 minutes)

When performing sensitivity analysis on a Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) model, you want to focus on the variables that have the most outsized impact on the implied share price or enterprise value.

The terminal value represents the majority of a company's value in a DCF. Therefore, the classic DCF sensitivity tables pair the discount rate with the terminal value drivers:
- **WACC vs. Terminal Growth Rate:** This shows how the valuation changes across a range of discount rates and long-term growth assumptions. Small changes here (like 50 basis points) can swing the implied value by 10-20%.
- **WACC vs. EBITDA Exit Multiple:** Used when the terminal value is calculated via the multiples method.

Additionally, if the company's value is heavily driven by its operational performance over the forecast period, you might sensitize:
- **Revenue Growth vs. EBITDA Margin:** To show how different operating scenarios impact the cash flows before they are even discounted.

## Common follow-ups

- **What would you sensitize in an LBO?** Entry multiple vs. exit multiple, and leverage vs. exit multiple.
- **What would you sensitize in a merger model?** Purchase price premium vs. synergies realized, or purchase price vs. financing mix (debt/equity).
