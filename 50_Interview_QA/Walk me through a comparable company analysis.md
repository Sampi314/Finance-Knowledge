# Walk me through a comparable company analysis

## The question

"How would you value a company using comparable company analysis? Walk me through the process."

## Short answer (30 seconds)

You first select a peer group of 5-10 comparable public companies based on industry, size, business model, and growth. Then, you gather financial data to calculate their enterprise values and relevant operating metrics. Next, you calculate valuation multiples like EV/EBITDA for both the trailing and forward twelve months. Finally, you analyze the distribution of those multiples to find a median range, and apply that range to the target company to determine its implied valuation.

## Long answer (2 minutes)

Walking through a comparable company analysis involves six main steps:

1. **Select Comparables:** First, identify 5-10 truly comparable public companies. This is based on industry, business model, size (usually 0.5x to 2x the target's revenue), growth profile, and profitability.
2. **Gather Data:** Next, gather market data (stock price, fully diluted shares, net debt) to calculate their equity and enterprise values. Also, pull historical and projected financial metrics like Revenue, EBITDA, and net income from filings and analyst estimates.
3. **Calculate Multiples:** Calculate the relevant valuation multiples for each peer. The most common are enterprise value multiples like EV/EBITDA and EV/Revenue, using both Last Twelve Months (LTM) and Next Twelve Months (NTM) data.
4. **Analyze Distribution:** Calculate summary statistics for the multiples across the peer group, focusing on the median and the interquartile range to exclude extreme outliers. You also look at what drives premium or discount multiples, like higher growth or better margins.
5. **Apply Multiples:** Apply the selected multiple range to the target company's financial metrics to calculate its implied enterprise value. From there, you subtract net debt to bridge to the implied equity value.
6. **Present Results:** Finally, present the analysis in a structured table with clear summary statistics and justifiable conclusions about where the target falls within the valuation range.

## Common follow-ups

- **How do you select the companies on the spot?** Focus on direct competitors in the same subsector, ensuring they have a similar size, business model, and available public data.
- **Why use median over mean?** The median is less affected by extreme outliers in the peer group (e.g., one company trading at 15x while the rest are at 5x).
- **What are the limitations of comps?** It requires truly comparable public companies which aren't always available, it can be distorted by current market bubbles or crashes, and it doesn't capture company-specific strategic value or acquisition premiums (unlike precedent transactions).
