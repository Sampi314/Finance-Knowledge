# What Typically Plugs in an LBO?

## The question

"What Typically Plugs in an LBO?"

## Short answer (30 seconds)

Sponsor equity typically serves as the mathematical plug in an LBO Sources and Uses table. The private equity sponsor first maximizes the available debt financing based on lender commitments and leverage multiples. Whatever funding requirement remains to cover the total uses is then filled by the sponsor's equity contribution.

## Long answer (2 minutes)

In any M&A or LBO transaction, the total sources of capital must exactly equal the total uses of capital as an accounting identity.

- **Step 1:** The "Uses" are calculated first. This includes the equity purchase price, the cost to refinance any of the target's existing debt, transaction fees, and financing fees.
- **Step 2:** The "Sources" are layered in. A private equity firm will maximize the amount of debt (senior, mezzanine, subordinated) it can raise based on the target's cash flow profile to maximize leverage. They will also factor in any rollover equity from existing management.
- **Step 3:** The "Plug." Because the sponsor's goal is to minimize their initial cash outlay to boost returns (like IRR), they solve for their equity contribution last.

The formula is: `Total Uses - All Debt Tranches - Rollover Equity = Sponsor Equity`. By making equity the plug, the model automatically ensures the transaction balances.
