# What are the main property types, and how do they differ from each other?

## The question

What are the main property types, and how do they differ from each other?

## Short answer (30 seconds)

The main categories are office, industrial, retail, and multifamily properties. Office, industrial, and retail properties have businesses as tenants and offer long-term, customized leases of 5-10 years. Multifamily properties have individuals as tenants and offer short-term leases (usually 1 year) with similar terms for all tenants.

## Long answer (2 minutes)

The main real estate asset classes are office, industrial, retail, and multifamily properties:

- **Office, industrial, and retail properties:** These have businesses as tenants and typically offer long-term leases of 5-10 years. The lease terms are highly variable and often include different rental rates, rental escalations, free months of rent, expense reimbursements, and tenant improvements. Because they have fewer tenants with customized leases, their cash flows tend to be "lumpier" with potentially long periods of downtime and significant concessions when securing new tenants.
- **Industrial vs. Office/Retail:** Industrial properties can be built more quickly and cheaply and tend to have fewer tenants, while office and retail properties take more time and money to build and tend to have more tenants.
- **Multifamily properties:** These have individuals as tenants and offer short-term leases (usually 1 year), with very similar terms for all tenants.
- **Other property types:** These include hotels, storage, data centers, healthcare facilities, and condominiums. They differ based on their specific tenants, leases, or ownership structures.
