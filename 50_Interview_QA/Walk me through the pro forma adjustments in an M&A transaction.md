# Walk me through the pro forma adjustments in an M&A transaction.

## The question

"Walk me through the pro forma adjustments in an M&A transaction."

## Short answer (30 seconds)

The key categories of pro forma adjustments include purchase price allocation, financing adjustments, synergy adjustments, and transaction costs. These combine the standalone financials of the acquirer and target while reflecting the deal's structure and economic reality.

## Long answer (2 minutes)

- **Purchase Price Allocation (PPA):** You adjust the target's assets and liabilities to fair value, recognize any new intangible assets (like customer relationships), and record goodwill as the residual excess purchase price. This also creates new depreciation and amortization expenses.
- **Financing Adjustments:** You adjust for how the deal is funded. If funded with debt, you add the new debt balance and the corresponding interest expense. If funded with stock, you increase the share count. If using cash, you reduce the cash balance and account for foregone interest income.
- **Synergy Adjustments:** You factor in expected cost savings (e.g., headcount reductions, facility consolidation) and sometimes revenue enhancements, ensuring these are tax-effected.
- **Transaction Costs:** Advisory fees, legal costs, and financing fees are typically expensed rather than capitalized under current accounting rules.
