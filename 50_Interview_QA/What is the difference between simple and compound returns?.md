# What is the difference between simple and compound returns?

**Simple returns** calculate interest only on the original principal amount, leading to linear growth over time.

**Compound returns** calculate interest on both the original principal and any previously accumulated interest. This "interest on interest" results in exponential growth, generating significantly larger totals over long time horizons compared to simple interest.
