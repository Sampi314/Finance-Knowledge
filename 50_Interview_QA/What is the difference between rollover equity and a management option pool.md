# What is the difference between rollover equity and a management option pool?

## The question

What is the difference between rollover equity and a management option pool?

## Short answer (30 seconds)

Rollover equity represents existing shares that management already owns, which are exchanged for shares in the new company, holding immediate value. In contrast, a management option pool involves new equity grants created at or after the transaction, which have no upfront cost but require vesting and only have value if the equity value increases above the strike price.

## Long answer (2 minutes)

The key differences between rollover equity and a management option pool lie in their source, upfront cost, and value mechanics:

- **Source and Cost:** Rollover equity comes from existing shares that management has already paid for. They exchange these for shares in the new entity (Newco). An option pool, however, consists of fresh equity allocations granted at fair market value with no upfront cost to the management.
- **Immediate Value:** Because rollover equity is an exchange of existing ownership, it has immediate economic value at closing. Options only have value if the post-transaction equity value exceeds their strike price upon exit.
- **Dilution:** Rollover equity does not dilute the sponsor's ownership since it's an exchange rather than a new issuance. Options, when exercised, dilute all shareholders proportionally, including both the sponsor and rollover equity.
- **Tax Treatment:** Rollover equity may qualify for tax-deferred treatment, while options are typically taxed as ordinary income upon exercise or sale.

Most LBOs include both to maintain alignment with existing owners and incentivize the broader management team.
