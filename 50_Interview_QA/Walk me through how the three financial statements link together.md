# Walk me through how the three financial statements link together

## The question

"Walk me through how the three financial statements link together"

## Short answer (30 seconds)

Net Income from the bottom of the Income Statement flows to the top of the Cash Flow Statement. The Cash Flow Statement adjusts Net Income for non-cash items and changes in working capital to arrive at the Net Change in Cash. This net change updates the Cash account on the Balance Sheet, while Net Income flows into Retained Earnings, ensuring Assets equal Liabilities plus Equity.

## Long answer (2 minutes)

1.  **Income Statement to Cash Flow Statement:** The bridge begins with Net Income, which flows from the bottom of the Income Statement to the top of the Cash Flow Statement as the first line item in Cash Flow from Operations.
2.  **Adjustments for Non-Cash Items:** Because Net Income is an accrual number, non-cash charges (such as Depreciation and Amortization) that reduced Net Income are added back.
3.  **Working Capital:** Next, you adjust for changes in working capital. Increases in assets like Accounts Receivable and Inventory are uses of cash (subtract), while increases in liabilities like Accounts Payable are sources of cash (add).
4.  **Investing and Financing:** You then account for Cash Flow from Investing (like CapEx) and Cash Flow from Financing (like debt issuance or dividends paid). The sum of all three sections yields the Net Change in Cash.
5.  **Cash Flow Statement to Balance Sheet:** The Net Change in Cash flows directly to the Balance Sheet to update the Cash account for the new period.
6.  **Income Statement to Balance Sheet:** Net Income from the Income Statement flows directly into Retained Earnings under Shareholders' Equity. Additionally, items from the Cash Flow Statement link directly—for example, CapEx increases PP&E, Depreciation reduces it, and debt transactions update long-term liabilities. Ultimately, these connections ensure the fundamental equation (Assets = Liabilities + Equity) remains perfectly balanced.
