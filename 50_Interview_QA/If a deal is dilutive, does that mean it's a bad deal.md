# If a deal is dilutive, does that mean it's a bad deal?

## The question

"If a deal is dilutive, does that mean it's a bad deal?"

## Short answer (30 seconds)

Not necessarily. Dilution measures only the immediate EPS impact, but doesn't capture strategic value, long-term growth potential, or synergies that take time to realize. A deal might be dilutive in Year 1 but highly accretive by Year 3 once synergies are fully captured and revenue growth accelerates.

## Long answer (2 minutes)

Dilution measures only the immediate EPS impact, but doesn't capture strategic value, long-term growth potential, or synergies that take time to realize.

A deal might be dilutive in Year 1 but highly accretive by Year 3 once synergies are fully captured and revenue growth accelerates. Additionally, strategic considerations like acquiring critical technology, entering new markets, or eliminating competitors may justify short-term dilution.

That said, public company management teams are very sensitive to dilution because investors focus heavily on EPS, so they'll typically structure deals to minimize dilution even if it means taking on more debt or reducing the premium paid.

## Common follow-ups

- "How would you make a dilutive deal accretive?" Increase the cash portion of consideration to reduce share dilution (potentially by raising debt), identify and capture more synergies, negotiate a lower purchase price, structure the deal as an earnout, accelerate synergy realization, or adjust the financing mix to use cheaper debt.
