# Walk me through how you calculate WACC

## The question

Walk me through how you calculate WACC.

## Short answer (30 seconds)

WACC equals the weighted average of cost of equity and after-tax cost of debt. I weight each by the proportion of total capital using market values to arrive at the final blended cost.

## Long answer (2 minutes)

"WACC equals the weighted average of cost of equity and after-tax cost of debt.

- For cost of equity, I use CAPM: risk-free rate plus beta times equity risk premium.
- For cost of debt, I use the yield on existing debt or risk-free rate plus credit spread, then multiply by one minus the tax rate to capture the interest tax shield.
- I weight each by the proportion of total capital using market values."

## Common follow-ups

- **Why do we use after-tax cost of debt?** Interest expense is tax-deductible, so the government effectively subsidizes part of the interest cost. We use after-tax cost to reflect the true economic cost of debt financing.
- **Why do we use market values for WACC weights?** Market values reflect current economic reality and what it would cost to raise capital today. Book values reflect historical costs that may be outdated.
