How to calculate Zakat: A recap of the basic rules
==================================================

*   February 12, 2026

**By Akram Mostafa, Associate Director**

Zakat background & regulations
------------------------------

The Zakat, Tax and Customs Authority (ZATCA) issued updated Zakat Regulations, effective from March 2024. While the new Zakat Regulations came into force in 2024, we still see some financial models being prepared on the old basis. We highlight below some of the key changes we would expect to see in a financial model.

The Zakat base: Year-end balances
---------------------------------

The Zakat base is now calculated using balances from the financial statements at the end of the Zakat year, regardless of when those assets or debts were acquired or incurred. All accounting adjustments made to the financial statements at year-end are generally considered for Zakat purposes unless explicitly excluded under the Regulations. This is a change from using opening balances.

How to determine Zakat
----------------------

### Minimum and maximum Zakat base

It is now a requirement to calculate the minimum and maximum Zakat base, and this is not something that we see in all models.

The minimum Zakat base is determined by comparing the calculated Zakat base with the adjusted net profit. If the calculated base is lower, specific tests apply to determine the appropriate base. In the event that losses are reported and no adjusted net profit exists, no Zakat base arises.

The maximum Zakat base is capped at the equity value reflected in the financial statements at year-end, adjusted with differences between adjusted and book profits and for items reclassified from liabilities to equity.

Transfer pricing to Zakat payers
--------------------------------

ZATCA approved extending the applicability of Transfer Pricing to Zakat payers for years starting on or after 1 January 2024.  Under these new requirements, Zakat payers will need to ensure that their transactions with related parties are priced at arm’s length.

A recap of the basic Zakat rules
--------------------------------

### Zakat base calculation

The Zakat base starts with the difference between book net profit and adjusted net profit, then adding equity and equity-like items, qualifying non-current liabilities, and then deducting allowable assets set out in the Regulations.

Equity and equity-like items include retained earnings, reserves, realised and unrealised gains, unpaid profit distributions, qualifying shareholder credit balances, and certain provisions treated as ownership interests.

### Allowable asset deductions

Deductible assets include qualifying investments, non-available-for-sale materials, raw materials, net fixed assets, non-trading intangible assets, employee housing assets, statutory deposits, deferred tax assets, qualifying sukuk and bonds, investment properties not held for sale, assets under concession arrangements, capital projects under construction not intended for sale, right-of-use assets, and qualifying overdue government receivables.

The value of all items used to adjust the Zakat base is determined in accordance with the Zakat Payer’s financial statements as at the end of the Zakat year. When calculating the total amount of zakatable wealth, debts and liabilities (including any outstanding interest payments) are deducted from the amount of zakatable assets to arrive at the net zakatable wealth figure.

### Attribution of liabilities

The new Regulations introduced an attribution mechanism to reach the Zakat base, allowing liabilities to be added when deductible current assets are offset or when certain non-current assets are excluded from deduction. The attributable amount of liabilities is calculated proportionately based on the ratio of the relevant asset to the total assets of the same category. The attribution is still subject to quantitative limits, and a cap equal to the value of the related asset deduction or exclusion.

### Zakat rate

The standard Zakat rate is 2.5% for a Hijri lunar year. However, if the Zakat payer uses a fiscal year that differs from the Hijri year, the Zakat rate will be proportionate to the actual number of days in the fiscal year, resulting in an approximate Zakat rate of 2.57% for taxpayers who use the Gregorian year.

Our view about Zakat exposures
------------------------------

The updated Zakat Regulations represent a significant shift towards a more technical and efficient Zakat regime in the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia. Zakat payers should reassess their accounting policies, capital structures, and compliance processes to ensure alignment with the Regulations and effectively manage potential Zakat exposures.
