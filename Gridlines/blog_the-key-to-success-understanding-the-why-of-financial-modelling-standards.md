Modelling // The why behind financial modelling standards
---------------------------------------------------------

In the field of [financial modelling,](https://www.gridlines.com/business-modelling-and-forecasting/)
 it is equally important to understand the ‘why’ behind standardised practices as it is to master the ‘how.’ In [this article](https://www.finexmod.com/the-why-behind-financial-modeling-standards/)

Work // Mastering group decision making
---------------------------------------

At Gridlines we are going through a process of implementing a system of extreme role clarity and transparency. The process is based on some of the principles of Holocracy, although we are not implementing Holocracy in full.

As part of this we have been looking at our decision-making processes. At the heart of any organisation’s culture is how we make decisions. This isn’t usually something that’s discussed explicitly. [This article](https://www.corporate-rebels.com/blog/mastering-group-decision-making)
 gives a good introduction to different group decision-making models. [Read more](https://www.corporate-rebels.com/blog/mastering-group-decision-making)

[**All models are wrong. Some are useful.**](https://www.financialmodellinghandbook.org/all-models-are-wrong-some-are-useful/)
 In 1976, British statistician George Box wrote the famous line, “All models are wrong, some are useful.” This is a critical truism to keep in our minds as we build and share financial models. There is only one thing you can say with certainty about your model’s output; it’s not going to be what happens in reality. [Read why this matters for modellers](https://www.financialmodellinghandbook.org/all-models-are-wrong-some-are-useful/)
.

[**Wise people learn from others mistakes.**](https://edbodmer.com/learning-from-bad-models/)
 In [this article](https://edbodmer.com/learning-from-bad-models/)
, Ed Bodmer teaches us what we can learn from horrible models. He uses videos, case studies, and even lets you download these models to see for yourself.
