19 of the best financial modelling tools
----------------------------------------

In the fast-paced world of financial modelling, the right tools can make the difference between a model that drives confident decision-making and one that crashes 20 minutes before the board meeting.

Whether you’re in FP&A, investment banking, private equity, corporate finance, or project finance, 2024–2025 offers an impressive selection of tools built to help you model faster and smarter, with fewer errors.

At Gridlines, we live and breathe financial modelling. We [audit models](https://www.gridlines.com/financial-model-audit/)
, build them, and help teams get the most from Excel and the tools that support it. This guide brings together the best financial modelling tools we think financial modellers should have on their radar right now – from slick Excel add-ins to full-blown planning platforms.

For each one, we’ll explain what it does, how it’s used, and why you might want to check it out.

Excel Add-Ins
-------------

### 1\. Macabacus

_**Best for:**_ Productivity, auditing, PowerPoint integration

[Macabacus](https://macabacus.com/solutions/financial-modeling-auditing?_gl=1*bhxr4x*_up*MQ..*_gs*MQ..&gclid=Cj0KCQjwqv2_BhC0ARIsAFb5Ac_aoGPclyKW0GG3UpnXqpPM__-r8XmzTa_hNeQjXJrsFlYZLkIJU_0aAu6EEALw_wcB)
 is a powerhouse Excel add-in widely used in investment banking, corporate finance, and transaction services. It speeds up model building with over 100 Excel shortcuts, helps with formula navigation, and offers audit tools to catch hardcodes, broken links, and inconsistencies.

Its integration with PowerPoint and Word means tables, charts, and numbers in your deck are always synced with your Excel model. If you’re tired of wasting time reformatting spreadsheets or manually updating slides before a pitch, Macabacus is a no-brainer.

### 2\. UpSlide

_**Best for:**_ Consistent formatting and reporting

[UpSlide](https://upslide.net/)
 is all about making your reporting process smoother and more professional. Like Macabacus, it connects Excel with PowerPoint and Word, but it also enforces company branding automatically and includes a slide-checking feature for presentations.

It’s especially useful in firms where consistency and polish matter (think Big Four, consulting, and banks). Its Excel navigation tools and formula maps help clean up models, and its shared slide libraries keep everyone on-message.

### 3\. Operis OAK

**_Best for:_** Model auditing and risk detection

[OAK (Operis Analysis Kit)](https://www.operisanalysiskit.com/)
 is built by one of the most respected names in model audit. It scans your spreadsheet for structural issues, detects circular references, identifies hardcoded values, and produces a detailed audit trail. This is the tool of choice for anyone involved in project finance, infrastructure, or model audit.

If you need to hand your model over to lenders or external reviewers, OAK gives you confidence that the numbers stack up and nothing’s going to blow up on day one.

### 4\. @RISK by Palisade

**_Best for:_** Monte Carlo simulation and risk analysis

[@RISK](https://lumivero.com/product/risk/)
 brings statistical analysis and uncertainty modelling into Excel. Instead of one outcome, you get a full probability distribution. This is essential for project finance analysts modelling cash flows under uncertain assumptions (think fluctuating demand, energy prices, or interest rates).

With @RISK, you can run thousands of simulations, visualise the distribution of outcomes, and see the probability of breaching covenants or missing targets.

It turns a static model into a powerful decision-making tool.

### 5\. S&P Capital IQ Excel Plugin

**_Best for:_** Pulling market data and financials

CapIQ is a staple in investment banking and equity research. With the Excel plugin, you can pull in historical financials, consensus estimates, peer comparisons, and macroeconomic indicators directly into your model. It saves time, reduces the risk of manual error, and keeps your assumptions up to date.

Modellers use it to quickly build comps tables, DCF models, and trading comparables that reflect the most current data available.

### 6\. Bloomberg Excel Add-In

_**Best for:**_ Live market data and trading models

For those with access to a [Bloomberg Terminal](https://www.bloomberg.com/professional/products/bloomberg-terminal/?utm_source=google&utm_medium=paid_search&utm_campaign=global_fpxaud_bssscorp_2025_ao&utm_content=text_terminal&utm_term=bloomberg+terminal&tactic=953606&gad_source=1&gclid=CjwKCAjw-qi_BhBxEiwAkxvbkGmS-TViFr96wlf6Vfa57wS71tpdCVgqXhwyFPVzCOkwM3Cetn43FRoCJCUQAvD_BwE&gclsrc=aw.ds#overview)
, the Excel add-in is essential. It provides real-time data feeds into Excel, so analysts can link live stock prices, yields, FX rates, and economic indicators directly to their models. It’s especially useful for M&A, treasury, asset management, and trading desks. Modellers can build dashboards that update automatically as markets move, helping with hedging, pricing, and real-time valuation analysis.

### 7\. FactSet Excel Add-In

**_Best for:_** Public market data and advanced analytics

[FactSet’s](https://www.factset.com/)
 Excel plugin offers access to a wide range of financial data for public companies, market indices, commodities, and economic series. It’s often favoured by asset managers and equity analysts who need granular control over how data is pulled into Excel. Its screening tools and custom formulas make it ideal for bespoke investment analysis, peer benchmarking, or portfolio reporting.

Think of it as a highly customisable alternative to CapIQ, with strong modelling chops.

Need support with data integration? 

Learn how our [operational modelling](https://www.gridlines.com/operational-modelling/)
 advisors ensure your model seamlessly interfaces with your management accounting system.

  

### 8\. PitchBook Excel Plugin

_**Best for:**_ Private equity and venture capital models

[PitchBook](https://pitchbook.com/products/excel-plugin)
 brings private market data into Excel. This is gold for PE and VC analysts trying to value companies with no public data. You can access funding histories, valuations, ownership structures, and transaction multiples for private deals. This makes it much easier to build LBOs, compare portfolio performance, or do due diligence on acquisition targets.

If your world involves startups, private equity, or fundraising, this tool is a serious time-saver.

### 9\. Modano

_**Best for:**_ Modular, scalable Excel models

[Modano](https://www.modano.com/)
 is a modelling automation tool that helps you build structured, robust models in Excel without having to reinvent the wheel every time. It provides a suite of pre-built, plug-and-play modules (P&L, balance sheet, cash flow, debt schedule, etc.) that are all fully linked. This ensures consistency across models and makes updates or scenario analysis far less painful. 

Modellers love it for its integrity checks, scenario switching tools, and time-saving workflows, especially in corporate finance and project modelling.

Standalone planning and modelling platforms
-------------------------------------------

### 10\. Anaplan

_**Best for:**_ Enterprise-wide connected planning

[Anaplan](https://www.anaplan.com/)
 is built for big organisations that want to link finance with operations, HR, sales, and more. It supports real-time collaboration, lets you model across thousands of variables, and allows multiple teams to input forecasts simultaneously. Unlike Excel, it handles massive datasets and multi-dimensional modelling with ease. For FP&A teams at global companies, Anaplan is a powerful platform for rolling forecasts, scenario planning, and long-term strategy alignment.

### 11\. Workday Adaptive Planning

**_Best for:_** Rolling forecasts and workforce planning

[Workday Adaptive](https://www.workday.com/en-gb/products/adaptive-planning/overview.html)
 is a web-based planning tool that offers the familiarity of Excel with the control of a cloud platform. It’s especially strong in workforce planning and driver-based forecasting. Teams use it to build collaborative models where department heads submit forecasts and finance rolls them up in real time. Its dashboards and reporting tools make it easy to visualise performance, compare scenarios, and manage approvals across the business.

### 12\. Planful

**_Best for:_** Continuous planning and financial consolidation

[Planful](https://planful.com/)
 (formerly Host Analytics) is designed for finance teams that want to move beyond annual budgeting into continuous planning. It supports financial consolidation, real-time reporting, and driver-based modelling. With integrations to ERP systems, Planful helps automate variance analysis, reporting cycles, and rolling reforecasts. It’s an excellent fit for mid-market companies scaling up their FP&A capabilities.

### 13\. Vena Solutions

_**Best for:**_ Excel-first financial planning

[Vena](https://www.venasolutions.com/)
 is ideal for finance teams that want to keep using Excel but gain control, auditability, and centralisation. It connects Excel templates to a cloud database, adding workflow management, version control, and collaboration features. Vena shines in environments where spreadsheets are entrenched but chaotic—helping teams manage budgets, forecasts, and reports in a more structured, scalable way.

### 14\. Pigment

_**Best for:**_ High-growth companies needing agility

[Pigment](https://www.pigment.com/)
 is a modern planning platform that’s gained serious traction in tech and high-growth sectors. It’s flexible, visually appealing, and built for scenario modelling across finance, HR, and sales. The UI is intuitive, and the data model supports multi-dimensional planning out of the box. It’s especially useful for finance teams working in dynamic environments that need to pivot quickly and model multiple paths forward.

### 15\. Synario

_**Best for:**_ Scenario-rich long-term modelling

[Synario](https://www.synario.com/)
 is designed for deep scenario analysis and long-term planning. It’s widely used in project finance, higher education, and infrastructure, where complex financing and capital planning models are the norm. The tool provides visual modelling logic, automated audit trails, and tools for toggling between scenarios instantly. It’s ideal for anyone needing to model the downstream impacts of today’s decisions across multiple years.

### 16\. Quantrix

**_Best for:_** Multi-dimensional modelling without Excel limitations

[Quantrix](https://quantrix.com/)
 is a spreadsheet alternative for complex, multi-dimensional models. It separates logic, structure, and data, making it easier to maintain large models across product lines, geographies, and scenarios. Quantrix is favoured by finance teams in manufacturing, insurance, and other data-heavy sectors that struggle with Excel’s limitations. It’s especially helpful when one formula needs to apply across dozens of dimensions.

### 17\. Causal

_**Best for:**_ Modern modelling with visual outputs

[Causal](https://causal.app/)
 is a web-based modelling tool that lets users create models using natural language variables instead of cell references. It’s built for collaboration, version control, and storytelling. You can create live dashboards, visualise scenario comparisons, and share interactive models with stakeholders. Perfect for CFOs and startup founders who want to build, understand, and explain models without getting lost in spreadsheet hell.

### 18\. Daloopa

_**Best for**:_ Automating model updates and data extraction

[Daloopa](https://www.daloopa.com/)
 uses AI to extract financial data from filings (10-Ks, 10-Qs, PDFs, etc.) and feed it directly into Excel models. It automates the creation and updating of historical financials, saving analysts hours of manual data entry. It’s a powerful tool for equity analysts, private equity, and anyone who tracks lots of companies and needs to keep models up to date with the latest data releases.

### 19\. Cube

_**Best for**:_ Fast-growing teams that want Excel + control

[Cube](https://www.cubesoftware.com/)
 connects Excel and Google Sheets to a central cloud platform. This means you can keep using your existing templates while gaining version control, audit trails, and live integrations with ERP and CRM systems. It’s particularly popular with high-growth companies that have outgrown manual Excel processes but aren’t ready to jump fully into enterprise planning platforms.

How to choose the right financial modelling tools
-------------------------------------------------

### 1\. Do you want to stay in Excel?

**Yes: I want to stay in Excel.  
**  
Consider tools that enhance Excel’s native capabilities.

**_Try:_** Macabacus, UpSlide, Operis OAK, Modano, @RISK, S&P Capital IQ, PitchBook, FactSet, Bloomberg Excel Add-In, Daloopa

**No,** **I want to move away from Excel:**

Explore these alternative modelling platforms:

**_Try:_** Causal, Anaplan, Pigment, Synario, Quantrix

### 2\. What do you need most help with?

**Building better models f**rom scratch and want to speed up the process or standardise structure?****

*   *   _Try:_ Macabacus, Modano

**Reducing errors / improving auditability**

*   *   _Try:_ Operis OAK, Macabacus, Modano

**Data and integration**

*   *   _Try:_ Daloopa, S&P Capital IQ, PitchBook, FactSet, Bloomberg

**Connecting to live data sources (ERP, CRM, HRIS)**

*   *   _Try:_ Cube, Anaplan, Pigment, Planful, Vena

**Scenario modelling and stress-testing**

*   *   _Try:_ @RISK, Synario, Pigment, Causal, Anaplan

**Long-range or capital-intensive modelling**

_Try:_ Synario, Quantrix, @RISK

### 3\. What’s your collaboration and workflow setup?

**Need shared forecasting and data input across teams?**

*   *   _Try:_ Anaplan, Workday Adaptive Planning, Cube, Vena, Pigment, Planful

**Want built-in audit trails and approval workflows?**

*   *   _Try:_ Vena, Planful, Cube

4\. How do you report and present insights?

**Need to update PowerPoint reports from Excel?**

*   *   _Try:_ Macabacus, UpSlide

**Want dashboards or stakeholder-friendly visuals?**

*   *   _Try:_ Causal, Pigment, Planful, Anaplan

**What kind of organisation are you?**

| Organisation type | Best-fit tools |
| --- | --- |
| **Startups / high-growth companies** needing agility without enterprise complexity | Cube, Causal, Pigment |
| **Mid-market / enterprise FP&A teams** scaling your planning processes? | Planful, Vena, Workday Adaptive Planning |
| **PE, VC, investment banking** | PitchBook, CapIQ, Daloopa, Macabacus, UpSlide |
| **Project finance / infrastructure** / capital planning | Operis OAK, Synario, @RISK, Modano |

Final tip: Top features
-----------------------

*   *   If your biggest pain point is manual data entry → start with **Daloopa**.

*   *   If you build the same models repeatedly → try **Modano**.

*   *   If you’re managing 10 scenario variants for a capex-heavy project → look at **Synario**.

*   *   If your CFO wants real-time dashboards without Excel formulas → **Pigment** or **Causal**.

*   *   If your team insists on Excel but you need structure → **Vena** or **Cube**.

The best financial modelling tools for you
------------------------------------------

There’s no one-size-fits-all financial modelling tool. Some teams will benefit from staying in Excel but adding better structure and controls. Others will need cloud-based planning platforms to collaborate and scale. What matters is understanding the strengths and limitations of each tool—and choosing the ones that fit your stage, your team, and your business goals. The best financial modelling tools are the ones that fit your bespoke needs.
