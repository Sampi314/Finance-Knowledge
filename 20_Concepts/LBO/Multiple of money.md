# Multiple of money

> **TL;DR.** Multiple of money (MoM), or multiple on invested capital (MOIC), is a key return metric evaluated alongside IRR for leveraged buyouts and venture capital investments.

## When you'd use this

Investors use multiple of money to understand return expectations across different investment stages, and it serves as a key return metric to estimate the total cash returned to a sponsor in a leveraged buyout.

## The 30-second version

Multiple of money is evaluated alongside the internal rate of return (IRR) as a primary return metric. Depending on the stage of investment, VC's expectations for a multiple of money vary significantly. Seed investors typically aim for a 100x return; Series A investors seek a 10x to 15x return, and later-stage investors usually shoot for a 3x to 5x multiple of money.

## The full explanation

In both venture capital and private equity, multiple of money (often referred to as MOIC) acts as a key return metric. It is evaluated alongside the internal rate of return (IRR) to determine the success of an investment.

Return expectations for multiple of money depend heavily on the risk and stage of the investment. For Venture Capital:
- Seed investors shoot for a 100x return.
- Series A investors need an investment to return 10x to 15x.
- Later stage investors aim for a 3x to 5x multiple of money.

In the context of Leveraged Buyouts (LBOs), calculating the MOIC is one of the final steps in determining LBO return metrics, evaluated after calculating the exit equity value.
