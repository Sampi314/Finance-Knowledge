# Debt covenants

> **TL;DR.** Debt covenants are contractual provisions in credit agreements that restrict borrower activities and require the maintenance of certain financial metrics to protect lenders.

## When you'd use this

Debt covenants are central to credit analysis and leveraged finance (LBOs). They dictate the operational flexibility a company has after taking on debt and outline the conditions under which lenders can intervene if the company's financial health deteriorates.

## The 30-second version

Covenants act as rules of engagement between a borrower and a lender. They are divided into two main categories: maintenance covenants and incurrence covenants. Maintenance covenants require the borrower to meet financial tests at regular intervals (e.g., quarterly), triggering an automatic violation if metrics slip. Incurrence covenants only apply when the borrower takes a specific action, like raising more debt or paying dividends, meaning gradual business deterioration alone won't trigger a default.

## The full explanation

Debt covenants are a fundamental aspect of credit analysis, determining when lenders gain intervention rights. They protect lenders by providing early warning signals and limiting risky behavior that could impair the borrower's ability to repay.

**Maintenance Covenants**
These require passive, ongoing compliance. The borrower must continuously satisfy financial tests (such as total leverage ratios) at regular intervals. If the business underperforms and the metrics drop below the required thresholds, a covenant violation occurs automatically. This gives lenders leverage to negotiate amendments, demand repayment, or accelerate enforcement. They are common in revolvers and private credit.

**Incurrence Covenants**
These act as gates for specific transactions. They only apply when the borrower actively takes a restricted action, such as incurring new debt, making acquisitions, or paying dividends. The company can operate freely and even experience significant performance deterioration without technical default, as long as it avoids triggering those actions. These are typical in syndicated term loans and high-yield bonds.

**Covenant-Lite (Cov-Lite) Loans**
The syndicated loan market has seen a massive shift toward cov-lite structures, which lack maintenance covenants entirely. This provides maximum flexibility for the borrower but delays lender intervention during periods of financial stress.

## Pitfalls and gotchas

- Violating a maintenance covenant constitutes a technical event of default, triggering lender rights like loan acceleration.
- Startups and private companies taking venture debt must carefully manage operations so negative cash flow or net assets don't breach covenant minimums.
