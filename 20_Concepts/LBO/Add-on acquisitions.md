# Add-on acquisitions

> **TL;DR.** An add-on acquisition is the purchase of a smaller target company by an existing private equity portfolio company (the platform) to integrate operations and create value.

## When you'd use this

Private equity firms use this strategy, also known as "buy-and-build," to drive inorganic growth and create value. It is typically applied when a sponsor already owns a strong platform company in a fragmented market and wants to achieve growth through consolidation rather than organic means.

## The 30-second version

An add-on acquisition occurs when an established portfolio company (the "platform") acquires a smaller competitor or complementary business. This transaction allows the platform to quickly expand its market share, geographic reach, or product offerings. Because smaller targets often trade at lower valuation multiples than the larger platform company, the transaction immediately creates value through multiple arbitrage. Additionally, integrating the add-on can yield significant revenue and cost synergies.

## The full explanation

In a buy-and-build strategy, a private equity firm first acquires a strong, established market leader to serve as the "platform." The platform typically operates in a stable, fragmented industry and serves as the foundation for future consolidation.

Once the platform is in place, the sponsor pursues smaller "add-on" acquisitions. These targets are often underperforming due to a lack of resources or suboptimal management, presenting significant value creation opportunities. The acquired companies are integrated into the platform, leveraging its robust infrastructure, proven systems, and strong management team.

### Value Creation Strategies

Add-on acquisitions drive value through several mechanisms:
- **Multiple Arbitrage:** Add-on targets are usually purchased at lower valuation multiples than the platform company. Once acquired and integrated, the add-on's cash flows are valued at the platform's higher multiple, generating immediate incremental value.
- **Revenue Synergies:** The combined entity can benefit from increased pricing power, cross-selling opportunities, new distribution channels, and expanded geographic reach.
- **Cost Synergies:** The consolidation allows the platform to eliminate overlapping functions, reduce headcount, streamline processes, and gain negotiating leverage over suppliers.

## Excel and modeling notes

In LBO modeling, add-on acquisitions are frequently used to demonstrate value creation without relying heavily on debt or aggressive multiple expansion. Financial models often incorporate the expected synergies and multiple arbitrage from planned add-ons, though conservative models typically keep the exit multiple equal to the entry multiple.

## Interview-ready answer

An add-on acquisition is a "buy-and-build" strategy where a private equity firm's existing portfolio company acquires a smaller business. This creates value through multiple arbitrage—since smaller companies trade at lower multiples—and by realizing revenue and cost synergies through integration.

## Pitfalls and gotchas

- Integration challenges: Merging different corporate cultures, IT systems, and operations can be difficult and may disrupt the core business.
- Overestimating synergies: Buyers often project overly optimistic cost and revenue synergies that fail to materialize post-close.
- Distraction of management: The process of identifying, acquiring, and integrating targets can divert the platform management's attention from day-to-day operations.
