# Vacancy and credit loss

> **TL;DR.** Vacancy and credit loss refers to the deductions from a real estate property's potential gross income due to unoccupied units and tenants failing to pay rent.

## When you'd use this

You use this when estimating the Effective Gross Income (EGI) and Net Operating Income (NOI) of a real estate investment. It is a critical adjustment in pro forma financial modeling to reflect realistic revenue expectations rather than theoretical maximums.

## The 30-second version

Vacancy and credit loss are projected deductions from the gross potential rental income of a property. Vacancy costs occur because units are unoccupied and therefore generating no rent. Credit costs, also known as collection costs, occur when tenants default on their lease obligations or fail to pay rent on time. Subtracting these losses from the Potential Gross Income (PGI) yields the Effective Gross Income (EGI).

## The full explanation

When evaluating a real estate rental property investment, you first determine the Potential Gross Income (PGI). However, to estimate the approximate income that a property owner can earn on a rental property investment, you must adjust the PGI by deducting vacancy and credit losses.

- **Vacancy Costs:** The projected losses a property owner incurs because of vacant units. The vacancy rate assumption determines the estimated vacancy costs, which is the percentage of properties or units not occupied by a tenant.
- **Credit Costs:** Also known as “collection costs”, these are the losses that stem from tenants not fulfilling their obligations to pay rent. This could mean a delay in collecting rental income and late fees, or outright default on the lease leading to property foreclosure.

## Formula

Effective Gross Income (EGI) = Potential Gross Income (PGI) – Vacancy and Credit Losses

## Worked example

Suppose a real estate investor is attempting to estimate the effective gross income (EGI) of an existing property investment. The property has 100 units with a monthly rent per unit of $4k. The annual gross potential rental income is $4.8 million ($400,000 monthly x 12). Assuming other sources of income is $200k per year, the Potential Gross Income (PGI) is $5,000,000.

The total vacancy and credit costs are forecasted to be $800k, which the property owner determined based on historical data and performing market research. Therefore, our final step is to deduct the vacancy and credit losses from the PGI of the property to arrive at a pro forma effective gross income (EGI) of $4.2 million.

Effective Gross Income (EGI) = $5,000,000 – $800,000 = $4,200,000
