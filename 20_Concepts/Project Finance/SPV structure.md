# SPV structure

> **TL;DR.** A Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV) is a distinct legal entity created specifically to develop, own, and operate a project, isolating its financial risk from the sponsoring companies.

## When you'd use this

SPVs are universally used in project finance to ring-fence large, capital-intensive infrastructure and energy projects. They allow corporations to undertake risky ventures without exposing their entire balance sheet to potential bankruptcy if the project fails.

## The 30-second version

In project finance, an SPV (or project company) is established as a standalone business whose sole purpose is to execute a specific project. This creates a "bankruptcy-remote" entity: if the project fails, the lenders can only claim the assets and cash flows of the SPV, not the assets of the parent companies (sponsors). The SPV acts as the central contractual nexus, signing all agreements for construction, operations, and financing. Because debt capacity is based purely on the SPV's own cash flows rather than the sponsors' corporate balance sheets, it allows for high leverage (often 70-90% debt).

## The full explanation

A cornerstone of project finance is the creation of a Special Purpose Vehicle (SPV). The SPV is a separate legal entity with its own assets, liabilities, and legal status. Typical legal forms include partnerships, limited partnerships, or joint ventures.

### Ring-fencing and Non-Recourse Financing
The primary function of an SPV is to separate or "ring-fence" the project's financial risk. This creates a "bankruptcy-remote" entity. In corporate finance, lenders can generally lay claim to all assets of the borrowing company. By contrast, in project finance, lenders to the SPV have no recourse (or limited recourse) to the sponsors' assets. They can only claim against the project's assets and the cash flows it generates. This allows corporations to undertake massive projects without contaminating their core business risk.

### Contractual Nexus
The SPV becomes the central hub for a complex web of contractual agreements. It signs the Engineering, Procurement, and Construction (EPC) contracts, Operation and Maintenance (O&M) agreements, offtake agreements, and loan documents. This structure ensures clear delineation and allocation of risks among the parties best equipped to manage them.

### Debt Sizing Based on Cash Flows
Because lenders can only look to the SPV for repayment, the amount of debt the SPV can raise is determined solely by the predictability and stability of the cash flows the project itself will generate. This focus on cash flows, rather than corporate enterprise value, typically allows the SPV to achieve much higher leverage (debt-to-equity ratios of 70:30 to 90:10) than traditional corporate financing.

## Excel and modeling notes

Financial models in project finance are built from the perspective of the SPV. The model forecasts the SPV's cash flows over its entire lifecycle to determine its debt capacity and calculate distributions to the sponsors after all priority payments (the cash flow waterfall) are made.

## Interview-ready answer

An SPV is a standalone legal entity created to own and operate a specific project. Its main purpose is to ring-fence risk, allowing lenders to provide non-recourse financing based purely on the project's cash flows rather than the sponsor's corporate balance sheet. This structure protects the parent company if the project fails and enables high leverage for large infrastructure developments.

## Pitfalls and gotchas

- The optics surrounding SPVs can sometimes be negative.
- The non-recourse nature means SPVs have lower access to capital and must rely entirely on their own cash flow generation capabilities rather than the sponsor's credit rating.
- Project finance structures built around SPVs are inherently complex and rigid, making it challenging to adapt to significant changes in market conditions once established.
