# Operations phase financing

> **TL;DR.** The operations phase financing of a project covers the modeling and analysis of revenues and expenses once an asset is functional, emphasizing contracted revenues and operational logistics.

## When you'd use this

You apply operations phase financing concepts when modeling the cash flows of a fully constructed project. It is crucial for assessing long-term viability, servicing debt, and determining distributions to equity investors.

## The 30-second version

Once construction is complete, a project enters its operations phase. Financial models for this phase integrate operational inputs, such as contracted versus uncontracted revenues, and logistics like processing, transportation, and storage in energy assets. These models can incorporate historical data into their inputs to forecast future performance and debt service accurately.

## The full explanation

During the operations phase, the project generates revenue. Models must detail contracted revenues (e.g., via Power Purchase Agreements or PPAs) and uncontracted revenues. In industries like renewable energy, this involves modeling generation profiles and battery storage potential. In midstream oil operations, the focus is on processing, transportation via pipelines or rail, and storage logistics. Models for operating assets often combine historical data with financial statements to project future cash flows accurately. The operations tab adjusts calculations from an accrual basis to a cash basis via working capital assumptions.

## Excel and modeling notes

When modeling operating assets, incorporate historical information via an inputs tab that bridges financial statements and raw inputs. For large-scale portfolios (e.g., infrastructure funds with numerous assets), consolidating calculations on a single sheet can significantly improve model speed and performance. Also ensure that working capital elements are captured in the operations sheet to bridge from accounting metrics to cash.

## Interview-ready answer

In project finance, operations phase financing involves modeling the cash flows of a completed project, focusing on contracted vs uncontracted revenues and operating expenses. It assesses the project's ability to service debt (CFADS) and generate returns using historical data and detailed operational inputs.

## Pitfalls and gotchas

- Failing to accurately differentiate between contracted and uncontracted revenues.
- Overcomplicating models for large portfolios instead of consolidating calculations for speed.
- Ignoring working capital impacts which reconcile accrual revenues/costs to actual cash.
