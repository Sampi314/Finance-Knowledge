# Debt service coverage ratio

> **TL;DR.** The Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR) measures whether a property or project generates sufficient net operating income to fulfill its annual debt obligations.

## When you'd use this

You would use DSCR when analyzing the financial viability and credit risk of a commercial property or a project finance transaction. Commercial real estate lenders and institutional investors rely on this fundamental credit metric to assess the likelihood of timely debt repayment. It helps determine the maximum debt capacity a project or property can support given its cash flow profile.

## The 30-second version

The Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR) is a credit metric that divides a property's Net Operating Income (NOI) by its annual debt service (principal and interest payments). A DSCR greater than 1.0x means the property generates enough income to cover its debt, while a ratio below 1.0x indicates a shortfall. Most risk-averse commercial lenders require a minimum DSCR of 1.25x to ensure an adequate cash flow "cushion" against unexpected underperformance.

## The full explanation

The DSCR offers practical insights into a property’s credit risk as of the present date, and whether its cash flows can handle the debt burden (or if the borrower is at risk of default).

From the perspective of lenders, the factor that dictates if their target yield on the financing arrangement is met is the receipt of on-time payments without the borrower defaulting and becoming insolvent.

### Interpreting DSCR Values
- **Higher DSCR:** A higher DSCR implies that the property’s income is more than sufficient to meet its annual financial obligations comfortably (i.e. sufficient “cushion” to prevent defaulting on the loan even if the global economy unexpectedly enters a recession).
- **Lower DSCR:** In contrast, a lower DSCR coincides with greater credit risk (and represents a potential “red flag”), where the lender must perform rigorous diligence in anticipation of the worst-case scenario.

Real Estate Underwriting Parameters:
- **DSCR = 1.0x** → NOI = Annual Debt Service (Breakeven Point)
- **DSCR > 1.0x** → NOI > Annual Debt Service (Sufficient Income)
- **DSCR < 1.0x** → NOI < Annual Debt Service (Insufficient Income)

The range for the minimum DSCR required by lenders tends to fluctuate based on different factors, such as the lender’s current risk appetite, the type of property pledged as collateral, the current conditions of the real estate market, and the near-term economic outlook.

## Interview-ready answer

The Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR) is a critical credit metric used to assess whether a property or project generates enough net operating income to cover its annual debt obligations. It is calculated by dividing Net Operating Income (NOI) by the annual debt service. Lenders typically look for a minimum DSCR of 1.25x, which provides a 25% cushion in cash flow to protect against unexpected downturns and reduce the risk of default.

## Pitfalls and gotchas

- Relying on a DSCR too close to 1.0x leaves the property or project highly vulnerable to any drop in revenue or increase in operating expenses.
- The required minimum DSCR is not fixed and can vary depending on market conditions, the specific asset class, and lender risk appetite.
