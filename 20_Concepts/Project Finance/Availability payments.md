# Availability payments

> **TL;DR.** Availability payments are regular service fees paid by a government or contracting authority to a project company based on a facility's availability for use rather than its actual volume of usage.

## When you'd use this

Availability payments are common in public-private partnerships (PPPs) for public infrastructure such as schools, hospitals, or untolled roads. They are used when the government wants private sector investment and operation but the end users are not charged directly or the private sector does not want to bear volume or usage risk.

## The 30-second version

Under an availability payment structure, the contracting authority (usually a government entity) compensates a project company for the fixed costs of constructing and maintaining an infrastructure asset. The payment is made as long as the asset is available and meets specified performance standards. It protects private investors from usage risk—such as a hospital being built but underutilized—while still transferring construction and operational risk.

## The full explanation

Availability payments allow developers to recover their fixed operating costs, debt service, and required return on equity regardless of how often a facility is used. Variable operating costs, on the other hand, are typically passed through in the form of usage payments.

To receive the full payment, the project company must satisfy output specifications. These specifications must be specific, measurable, achievable, realistic, and timely (SMART). If standards are not met (for example, if a portion of hospital rooms is out of service due to maintenance failures), the availability payment is reduced proportionally.

## Worked example

Imagine a project company operates a hospital with 100 rooms, receiving an availability payment each quarter. The contract stipulates at least 95 rooms must be available at all times.
If poor maintenance causes a waterline to burst, rendering 55 rooms unavailable for 45 days in a 90-day period:
- The calculation takes the unavailable rooms (55) minus the permitted unavailable rooms (5), which equals 50 rooms.
- Multiply 50 rooms by 45 days to find the shortfall: 2,250 room days.
- Divide the shortfall (2,250) by the maximum potential room days (90 days × 100 beds = 9,000).
- The result is a 25% penalty. The project company will be hit with a 25% reduction in its availability payment for that period.

## Interview-ready answer

Availability payments are periodic fees paid by a government to a project company for making a public facility, like a hospital or untolled road, available for use to specific standards. They are designed to cover fixed costs—such as debt service and required equity returns—while variable costs are generally passed through, meaning private investors don't take on the risk of how often the facility is actually utilized.

## Pitfalls and gotchas

- Output specifications need to be clearly defined to prevent disputes about whether the asset is technically "available" or not.
- Penalties are applied when the availability drops below the agreed threshold, significantly impacting the project company's revenues. However, these penalties are typically capped at the maximum availability payment.
