# Pro forma combined model

> **TL;DR.** A pro forma combined model (or merger model) combines the historical financials of an acquirer and target with transaction adjustments to project the post-deal financial profile.

## When you'd use this

You would use a pro forma combined model to evaluate the financial impact of an M&A transaction before closing. It supports transaction evaluation, deal negotiations, and financing arrangements by showing how various scenarios affect the combined company.

## The 30-second version

Pro forma financial statements combine two companies' historical financials and apply acquisition adjustments. Key adjustments include purchase price allocation (fair value and goodwill), financing adjustments (new debt, interest, or equity issuance), and expected synergies. The output answers the critical question of whether a deal is accretive or dilutive to EPS, and helps assess creditworthiness through leverage and coverage ratios.

## The full explanation

A pro forma combined model translates transaction mechanics into concrete financial outcomes. It primarily involves three statements reflecting the combined entity immediately after closing.

### Key Components

1. **Purchase Price Allocation (PPA):** The acquirer revalues the target's assets and liabilities to fair value, identifies intangible assets (like customer relationships), and calculates goodwill (the residual excess purchase price).
2. **Financing Adjustments:** Reflects how the buyer funds the deal. Cash consideration reduces cash, debt financing adds liabilities and interest expense, and equity issuance increases share count.
3. **Synergy Adjustments:** Projects cost savings (headcount, procurement) and sometimes revenue enhancements. Transaction costs are typically expensed immediately.

### Building the Model

The process starts by gathering historical financials and determining the transaction structure. Next, perform purchase price allocation and calculate financing impacts. You then combine the balance sheets and income statements, applying all adjustments, to output pro forma EPS, leverage ratios, and return metrics.

## Excel and modeling notes

Ensure all adjustments that affect pre-tax income (like interest expense and synergies) are calculated on an after-tax basis. Avoid double-counting synergies if they are already in the base projections, and remember to include the foregone interest on any cash used for the acquisition.

## Pitfalls and gotchas

- Forgetting to tax-effect income statement adjustments like interest and synergies.
- Using unrealistic, overly aggressive synergy assumptions to make a deal look accretive.
- Ignoring purchase accounting effects, where fair value step-ups create additional depreciation and amortization that reduce pro forma earnings.
