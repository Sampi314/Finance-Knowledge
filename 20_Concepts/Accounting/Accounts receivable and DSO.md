# Accounts receivable and DSO

> **TL;DR.** Accelerating accounts receivable improves cash inflows and helps bridge the gap between short-term operational needs and long-term financial success.

## When you'd use this

You use these concepts to measure the efficiency of collecting receivables and the average days it takes to collect them.

## The 30-second version

Accounts Receivable Turnover measures the efficiency of collecting receivables. Days Sales Outstanding (DSO), or Accounts Receivable Days, measures the average days it takes to collect. Accelerating these can improve a company's working capital position without scrambling for external financing.

## The full explanation

Accounts receivable can be accelerated through several tactics. Invoicing best practices include sending invoices promptly (ideally within 24 hours of delivery) with clear terms and late-fee clauses. Offering early-pay discounts, like 2% off if paid within 10 days, motivates customers to pay quickly. Establish a systematic follow-up schedule for overdue invoices—emails, phone calls, and, if needed, formal demand letters. The more consistent your follow-up, the less likely customers will delay payment indefinitely.

## Interview-ready answer

DSO measures how fast you collect payments post-sale. Improving your DSO extends your working capital runway. This can be achieved through early-pay discounts or clear invoicing.

## Pitfalls and gotchas

- When offering early-pay discounts, you must factor the discount's cost against the benefits like reduced financing needs or interest savings.
- A large gap between DSO and DPO fosters liquidity.
