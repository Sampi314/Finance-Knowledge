Course Content
--------------

Our Project Finance Modeling Course will help you to build a best-practice project finance model and optimize it for both debt sizing and equity financing for infrastructure project finance deals. Using a case study of a PPP hospital project, participants will gain a thorough understanding of concepts related to Public Private Partnerships (PPPs/P3s) for both social and economic infrastructure. Additionally, the course includes a toll road model, with a detailed explanation of the revenue and cost drivers.

The content is designed to be broad enough to benefit professionals in other project finance sectors as well, such as mining, natural resources, and critical infrastructure development. Participants will develop models that sculpt debt based on a debt service coverage ratio (DSCR) and integrate key metrics such as the internal rate of return (IRR) to evaluate projects effectively.

#### Course Syllabus

| **SECTION** | **OVERVIEW** |
| --- | --- |
| **Pre-course material** | *   Course requirements and overview<br>*   Excel functions required<br>*   Shortcuts, named ranges, and data validation<br>*   Best practice principles<br>*   Best practice – flags and accounts<br>*   Impact of leverage on a project finance deal |
| **Project Finance Introduction** | *   Introduction to project finance<br>*   Typical investment structures<br>*   Tax and legal implications<br>*   Overall timeline and cashflow relationships<br>*   Major contracts and risk vs reward<br>*   Roles of different parties in the deal |
| **Project finance term loans** | *   Impact of leverage on a deal<br>*   Types of lenders and their mindset<br>*   Appropriate discount rates and interest repayments<br>*   Principal repayment options<br>*   Debt service cover ratio (DSCR)<br>*   Sculpting and sizing debt<br>*   Cash sweeps<br>*   Loan life cover ratio (LLCR)<br>*   Other debt ratios used for going-concerns<br>*   Understanding a Facility Agreement |
| **Cashflow Drivers** | *   Availability payments<br>*   Lifecycle costs<br>*   Revenue forecasting for patronage risk projects<br>*   Typical costs for infrastructure projects<br>*   Inflation calculations<br>*   Discussion of risk profiles of infrastructure assets, including:<br>    *   Airports<br>    *   Ports<br>    *   Rail<br>    *   Roads<br>    *   Social infrastructure<br>*   Major maintenance reserve accounts<br>*   Understanding:<br>    *   Project Agreements<br>    *   Concession Agreements<br>    *   O&M Agreements |
| **Waterfall and cashflow summary** | *   Typical waterfall structure vs cashflow statement<br>*   Accounting vs cash issues<br>*   Global differences in calculating the waterfall<br>*   Accounts required and the role of the administrative agent<br>*   Develop separate cashflow forecasts for debt and equity analysis<br>*   Develop upside and downside scenarios and sensitivities with data tables |
| **Debt service reserve account (DSRA)** | *   Requirement of (and implementation of) a DSRA<br>*   Impact of the DSRA on debt ratios<br>*   The debt service facility as an alternative<br>*   Develop model checks to ensure compliance with loan documents |
| **Construction phase** | *   EPC contract overview<br>*   Completion testing required and holdback payments<br>*   Value added tax challenges and solutions<br>*   Sources and uses<br>*   Model construction costs and understand funding challenge<br>*   Calculate total funding required<br>*   Discuss alternative drawdown orders |
| **Depreciation and tax** | *   Straight line, reducing balance, and MACRS depreciation<br>*   Model from EBITDA to EBT<br>*   Thin capitalization constraints on tax deductibility<br>*   Tax losses carried forward<br>*   State and local taxes<br>*   What happens when tax is ahead of CADS<br>*   International tax considerations (i.e. WHT, tax treaties)<br>*   Complete post tax valuations |
| **Returns** | *   Returns required for lenders, equity, and developers<br>*   Cap rate compression and the value in selling your project, calculating terminal values<br>*   Holdco debt and adding subordinated debt to increase returns (and risk)<br>*   Valuation comparables |
| **Core Outputs** | *   Comparison of accounting and tax depreciation<br>*   Developing an income statement and balance sheet<br>*   Converting quarterly calculations to annual outputs<br>*   Calculating working capital<br>*   Add deferred taxes to your model<br>*   Outputs required for an investment decision |
| **Modeling quality checks** | *   Adding model checks<br>*   How to review a model |
| **Optimizing a deal model** | *   Solve the price required to achieve a hurdle rate<br>*   Size the debt required in operations and construction<br>*   Introduction to VBA for project finance<br>*   Running scenarios with VBA<br>*   Simplifying alternatives to avoid VBA<br>*   Summary of core risks with analysis |
