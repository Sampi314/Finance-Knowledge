Final Friday Fix: January 2026 Challenge
========================================

__On the final Friday of each month, we set an Excel / Power Pivot / Power Query / Power BI problem for you to puzzle over for the weekend.  On the Monday, we publish a solution.  If you think there is an alternative answer, feel free to email us.  We’ll feel free to ignore you.__

**_The Challenge_**

This month’s Final Friday Fix is the first instalment of six \[6\] regarding the fundamentals of collecting and sorting data.  It challenges you to find the top three \[3\] and bottom three \[3\] ranked fruits according to their total quantities.  Provided is a scattered list of fruits and quantities:

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

An input is provided for Top / Bottom **N**.

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

You can download the original question file [here](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/SP-Top-N-Challenge-1-Challenge.xlsx)
.

There are solutions for versions of Excel that support dynamic arrays and for those that do not.  For those that do, there is a specific requirement:

*   the formula needs to be calculated in just one cell (no “helper” cells).

Otherwise, for both solutions:

*   no Power Query / Get & Transform or VBA!

_Sounds easy?  Then why not have a go?  We’ll publish one solution in Monday’s blog.  Have a great weekend in the meantime!_
