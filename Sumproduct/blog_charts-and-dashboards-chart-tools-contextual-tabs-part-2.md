Charts and Dashboards: Chart Tools – Contextual Tabs (Part 2)
=============================================================

20 December 2019

_Welcome back to Charts and Dashboards blog series. This week, we’ll continue to look at the Format sub-tab from the contextual Chart Tools tabs._

[Last week](https://sumproduct.com/blog/charts-and-dashboards-chart-tools-contextual-tabs-part-1/)
, I wrote about the Design sub-tab in the contextual tabs which appear on the Ribbon when I click on a chart. Now, let’s move to the next sub-tab: Format, which helps me manually design charts to my liking.

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/e774d10cbbb9450fc45efbe51abdf434-305.jpg)

Let’s break it down into smaller categories.

‘Current Selection’ says which part I am currently choosing to format. I can ‘Reset to Match Style’ which brings me back to the original one:

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/f32e5a15e2cf9c3e4d2d058458ce054d-308-1.jpg)

Next to it is the ‘Insert Shapes’, from which I can insert multiple shapes to my chart:

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/f1140ff857fc3b6f5f97a6a24f4a6fc7-292.jpg)

From the ‘Shape Style’ category, shape outlines, fills and other effect may be formatted:

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/72aa864d2854c6fefb1083fba0ab5792-269.jpg)

I can even add ‘WordArt’:

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/36776d1da4d05b45bb5a5d09375f407c-231.jpg)

In the ‘Accessibility’ category, if I click ‘Alt Text’:

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/23912d3b1671861e02bebcd5183f1607-204.jpg)

a dialog will pop out like this:

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/6f49c288a0d88a66b427eaf4ece923d6-172.jpg)

In the ‘Arrange’ category, the position of the chart may be adjusted:

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/b9ee28d90e6b5bc92ea4aeafdad51628-141-1.jpg)

Last but not least is the ‘Size’ category. This is very helpful when I am designing a dashboard, where I need to make charts identical in some size dimensions, by just setting the height and width to the same numbers:

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

In addition to the contextual ‘Chart Tools’ on the Ribbon, charts may also be quickly formatted using their side-menus. If I click on a chart, I will see on my right there three option boxes:

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

If I click the plus shaped button, there is a list of tick boxes where I can choose which to be added to my chart. I can also click on the arrows for more detailed options:

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

The second icon is the brush pen box, which is similar to the Design tab, where I may quickly choose my chart styles and colours:

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

Finally, the filter panel may be used to sort out which data I want to display on the chart:

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

A right click on the chart will trigger a menu list, which offers me most of the options from the contextual ‘Chart Tools’ tabs:

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

That’s it for this week; check back next week for more Charts and Dashboards tips.
