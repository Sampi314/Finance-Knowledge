AI Blog: Copilot in Excel – Requirements and Limitations
========================================================

___Welcome back to our AI blog series!  In the [last blog](https://sumproduct.com/?p=1035605&preview=true&_thumbnail_id=1035606)
, we talked about how Copilot can give you smart suggestions and help to automate repetitive tasks in Excel.  People might ask, “Do I have access?”.  At this stage, Copilot in Excel is still rolling out step by step and not everyone can use it immediately.  Let’s take a closer look at what’s required to run Copilot in Excel and its current limitations.___

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/image-99.png)

**_What You Need Before Using Copilot?_**

To use Copilot in Excel today, you need to check some conditions.

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/image-100.png)

Don’t be disappointed If you open your Excel today and cannot see Copilot.  Microsoft is rolling out feature by feature, region by region.  Just make sure your Excel is updated and your IT administrator has the right licences.

**_What Can Copilot Not Do Yet?_**

Even if you have Copilot in Excel, there are some restrictions you need to pay attention:

1.  **Usage quotas**.  The maximum is 100 calls per 10 minutes, or 300 calls per hour.  If you keep prompting Copilot over a short time, you may see “quota exceeded” message
2.  **Single sheet focus**.  Copilot works best when your data is all in one table (Table) on one sheet.  If you have multiple tabs (Sales, Customers, Summary), it does not yet connect them automatically.  For that, you may still need **INDEX MATCH**, **XLOOKUP** or Power Query / Get & Transform
3.  **No Power Query or complex statistical models**.  At this stage, Copilot cannot build Power Query steps or advanced econometric models.  It is more for descriptive analysis, data cleaning and visualisation.

**_Why You Should Still Try_**

Although Copilot in Excel has these weaknesses, it is still a big step forward.  With its current ability to generate formulae, classify data and suggest next steps, it already saves hours of manual work.  For more complex tasks, you can have a try on Excel Labs or combine Copilot with traditional Excel tools.  For example:

*   Use Power Query to merge tables and then let Copilot summarise the results

*   Use PivotTable for cross-sheet data and then let Copilot make the chart.

**_Word to the Wise_**

Copilot in Excel is powerful but not (yet) perfect.  You need the right subscription, licence and setup environment, and you need to know its limitations.  However, once you have access, even with quotas and single-sheet focus, it can already change how you work with data.
