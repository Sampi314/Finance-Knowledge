Power BI Blog: Mekko Chart (cont’d)
===================================

18 July 2019

_Welcome back to this week’s Power BI blog series. This week, we continue with Mekko Chart._

[Last week](https://sumproduct.com/blog/power-bi-blog-mekko-chart/)
, I created a Mekko chart with data shown in two dimensions, which allows easy and quick comparison between multiple variables:

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/23912d3b1671861e02bebcd5183f1607-272.jpg)

I would like to do some further editing works on this chart. I go to ‘Format’ mode. Here I can sort the data legend by descending order:

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/b9ee28d90e6b5bc92ea4aeafdad51628-187.jpg)

We can see the legend line has been changed:

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/0485ccbc83bdeec1d741bad442a1ea5f-155.jpg)

I want to try sorting the data series by descending order too:

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/daf8c4f0259ce428269c0d3d4badd32b-117.jpg)

It turns out a little messy so I will remove this sort:

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/22c6daeb82d7d69ac88f878227e04b28-87-1.jpg)

I also want to change the colours of legends to match with my organisation’s colour scheme, by clicking on ‘Data colors’:

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/a1537847463e660a31158c8032525438-75.jpg)

I add the title for the chart as well:

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/917da985be13220165c8d2823e95344f-63.jpg)

Next, I try putting data labels in place:

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/8c3be7af9f73d031acae69ed85a2e148-53.jpg)

However, it turns out that I don’t like many numbers to be displayed on my chart:

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/e63c0a4c21afc9afb438aacc09a59317-47.jpg)

Therefore, I remove the data label, and here is the final product of my creation today:

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/d082e3477129350b8a2a589156028e63-40.jpg)

That’s it for this week, check back next week for the next blog in the [Power BI series](https://www.sumproduct.com/thought/power-bi-tips-blog-series)
