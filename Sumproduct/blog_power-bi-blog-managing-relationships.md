Power BI Blog: Managing Relationships
=====================================

20 June 2024

_Welcome back to this week’s edition of the Power BI blog series. This week, we look at the recently added ‘Manage relationships’ button. Hmm… I wonder if that works for personal lives too?_

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/02781cd1166262531f94997da6e8df73-1.jpg)

There is a redesigned ‘Manage relationships’ dialog in Power BI Desktop. To open this dialog, simply select the ‘Manage relationships’ button in the Home tab on the Ribbon.

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/f32e5a15e2cf9c3e4d2d058458ce054d-1-1.jpg)

Once opened, you’ll find a comprehensive view of all your relationships, along with their key properties, all in one convenient location. From here you can create new relationships or edit an existing one, _viz._

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/f1140ff857fc3b6f5f97a6a24f4a6fc7-1-1.jpg)

Additionally, you have the option to filter and focus on specific relationships in your model based upon cardinality and cross filter direction.

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/72aa864d2854c6fefb1083fba0ab5792-1-1.jpg)

To make things even easier, Power BI can find and create relationships for you by selecting the ‘Autodetect’ button.

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/36776d1da4d05b45bb5a5d09375f407c-1-1.jpg)
