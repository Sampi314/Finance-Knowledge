AI Blog: Copilot in Excel – Best Practice and Prompt Tips
=========================================================

___Welcome back to our AI blog series!  We have already introduced Copilot’s features, automation and various requirements.  Now, it’s time to talk about how to use it effectively.  Just like using other AI tools such as ChatGPT or Gemini, the secret is in your prompts.  If you ask intelligently and specifically, you get better answers.  If you ask vaguely, you may get something random.  Here we have some Best Practice tips for working with Copilot in Excel.___

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/10/image-106.png)

**_1.  Use Structured Tables, Not Messy Data_**

As we mentioned before, Copilot works best when the data is in Excel Table format with proper headers.  If the data range is merged cells, blanks or inconsistent rows, Copilot may misunderstand.  Therefore, don’t forget to take a minute to clean your sheet and convert your data to a Table (**CTRL + T**) before asking Copilot.

**_2.  Be Clear and Specific in Prompts_**

Compare these two prompts:

*   Bad: _“Show sales trends”_
*   Good: _“Show monthly sales trend by ProductCategory for 2024 in a Line chart”._

The first prompt is too general and doesn’t provide Copilot with enough information to generate a meaningful analysis.  The second one is better because it provides Copilot with more detail: the metric (sales), the dimensions (month, category) and the format (Line chart).

**_3.  Start Small and Validate_**

Don’t throw your whole dataset at Copilot in the first try.  Begin with 100 rows or one month’s data, check the result, then expand to a full year.  Working with a smaller dataset reduces the potential errors caused by data inconsistencies or complex calculations.  Smaller datasets are processed more quickly, allowing you to iterate and refine your prompts more efficiently.  Then, starting small helps to avoid hitting quota limits, especially when working with large datasets or complex analyses.

**_4.  Human Review_**

Whilst Copilot can significantly accelerate data analysis, it’s important to remember that AI output is not perfect.  Maybe Copilot classifies customers into wrong groups or misses some business rules.  Treat Copilot as a valuable assistant but rely on your own expertise and judgment to validate the results.

**_5.  Refine the Prompt Step by Step_**

Usually, good prompts don’t come in one shot.  Often you need to adjust wording and refine. 

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

Remember when you are working with Copilot, treat it as a conversation.  Here is a simple flow to show how vague prompts can be improved step by step:

*   “Analyse sales” is too vague

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

*   “Summarise sales by category” works better, but is still broad

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

*   “Summarise the top 5 product categories by TotalSpend in 2024, return results in a ranked table with percentages” is clear, specific and actionable.

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

**_Word to the Wise_**

Using Copilot in Excel is not only about knowing the features, but also about knowing how to ask.  With clean data, specific prompts, small tests and human review, you can get better outcomes from Copilot.  Good luck!
