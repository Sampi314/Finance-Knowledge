Power BI Blog: Standalone Copilot Improvements in Power BI
==========================================================

_Welcome back to this week’s edition of the Power BI blog series._  _This week, we take another look at Copilot in Power BI._

The standalone Copilot in Power BI, also known as ‘chat with your data’, has been made easier to use quite recently.  Previously, Copilot asked users to choose from a list of possible data sources before answering questions.  Now, it handles that step automatically: if it finds a strong match, it selects it in the background and goes straight to the answer.  When more context is needed, Copilot will ask simple clarifying questions to find the right source.  Although the additional reasoning may require a few more seconds, obtaining an accurate response from a reliable source justifies the brief wait.

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

Additionally, in response to search questions like ‘Find reports about sales’, you’ll now see a much simpler text-based list of items.

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

This revised version of Copilot allows you to access it right on your home page, making it easier than ever to find what you’re looking for or ask _ad hoc_ questions about your data: simply type your question and hit **ENTER**.

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

If you prefer the old home page, you can simply switch back using the layout drop-down.

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

There are now new suggested prompts for recently opened items where Copilot offers prompt suggestions related to recently opened items to help you get started.

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

If you wish to add a report or other elements, items are now sorted by recency and easily filtered by type that aligns with other Copilot experiences across Microsoft.

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

The Report Copilot experience in Power BI has been upgraded, making it easier to create, edit and refine reports with the help of AI.  Indeed, with this update, Copilot in Power BI not only helps you create entire report pages in seconds but now also gives you more control, flexibility and visual intelligence.  This new Report Copilot experience is available now in the Power BI service and Power BI Desktop.

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

The new Copilot understands your data better.  Just tell it what you want to create and tweak the results as necessary: Copilot now selects the most appropriate visuals for your data automatically.

Furthermore, Copilot now supports a wider range of visuals, giving you more options to present insights into exactly how you envision them.  Describe them well, as Copilot now understands more nuanced instructions, producing more accurate and insightful results (theoretically!).
