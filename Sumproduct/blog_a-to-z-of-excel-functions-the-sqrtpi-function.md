A to Z of Excel Functions: The SQRTPI Function
==============================================

_Welcome back to our regular A to Z of Excel Functions blog.  Today we look at the **SQRTPI** function._

**The SQRTPI function**

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

This function returns the positive square root of a number multiplied by **π** (pi). 

It does seem a little arbitrary (the square root of **π** is approximately 1.77245385091).  **π** is an irrational number, which means it cannot be expressed as a simple fraction and its decimal representation goes on infinitely without repeating.  However, this expression and its square root are frequently used in mathematics, engineering and other sciences.  Examples include:

*   **Mathematical formulae and hypotheses:** it appears time and time again in mathematical expressions often when not expected, _e.g._

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

*   **Geometry:** **π** is defined as the ratio of a circle’s circumference to its diameter, making it a fundamental constant in geometry.  The square root of **π** often appears in formulae related to the areas and volumes of circles, spheres, cylinders, _etc_.

*   **Probability and Statistics:** the square root of **π** appears in various probability distributions, such as the Normal distribution  
    
*   **Physics:** **π** and its square root are relevant in many physical phenomena and equations. They appear in various equations including quantum mechanics and wave propagation.

The **SQRTPI** function employs the following syntax to operate:

> **SQRTPI(number)**

The **SQRTPI** function has the following argument:

*   **number:** this is required and represents the **number** for which you want the **positive** square root of this **number** multiplied by **π**.

It should be further noted that:

*   if the number is negative, **SQRTPI** returns the _#NUM!_ error.
