Power Pivot Principles: Filters
===============================

10 July 2018

_Welcome back to our Power Pivot blog. Today,_ _we discuss how to use filters in Power Pivot._

In [Power Pivot Principles: Introducing CALCULATE](https://www.sumproduct.com/blog/article/power-pivot-principles/ppp-introducing-calculate)
, we talked about how filters are a more transparent way of filtering the data compared to entering filters directly into the **CALCULATE** measure.

For example, say we have a simple PivotTable:

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

Imagine we want to filter the PivotTable by year; to do this, drag the ‘Year’ field into the ‘Filters’ area, _viz._

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

A ‘Filters’ row will appear allowing us to select which years we want to be displayed in the PivotTable.

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

If we select 2016 and 2017,

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

the PivotTable will filter out all other years from the data.

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

The filter will then change from ‘All’ to ‘(Multiple Items)’, clearly stating that the data is filtered. Ergo, it is a more transparent method of filtering, instead of having filters applied directly in a measure. It depends whether or not you require the filter to be part of the PivotTable context or not.
