_Welcome back to this week’s Charts and Dashboards blog series. This week, let’s continue to talk about PivotChart._

To recap, [last week](https://sumproduct.com/blog/charts-and-dashboards-pivotchart-part-1-creating-a-pivotchart/)
, we talked about PivotChart and how to create one. PivotCharts are complimentary visual representations of data in PivotTables. They display any data currently in a PivotTable in a chart, with that they also inherit the flexibility and interactivity of PivotTables.

PivotChart allows interactivity with the chart. I can apply filters to the data by clicking on the downwards arrow next to ‘Year’ and ‘Country’ fields:

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

These filters will also apply to the PivotTable linked to the PivotChart. For example, if I choose to see only Australia’s and Canada’s sales in three most recent years – 2017, 2018, 2019, both PivotChart and PivotTable are adjusted based on new filters, and note the filter icons in the ‘Year’ and ‘Country’ fields of PivotChart and PivotTable:
