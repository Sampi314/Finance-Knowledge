**_Are You in the Prime of Your Excel Knowledge?_**

A positive integer (whole number) is said to be **prime** if only two different numbers divide it: one and itself. This is why the number 1 isn’t prime (it has only one divisor).

**_The Challenge_**

Using Excel, can you create a formula, without using a user-defined function or VBA, that will determine whether a number is prime?

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/faa1de1e7700058f6864882afaf05fab.jpg)

Sound easy? Some of you might think this is trivial and have a solution in seconds. We’re going to bet if you are one of those readers, you are probably wrong – but more about that next week. We’ll publish our suggested solution in Monday’s blog. Have a great weekend!
