The financing of digital infrastructure projects presents huge growth opportunities. Learning the skills needed to build and analyse models that can effectively evaluate these projects, will be a competitive advantage for market participants.

This two-day interactive training course will introduce attendees to the essential tools and techniques to evaluate digital assets. Participants will dive into hands-on exercises, learning how to build clean, dynamic and presentable financial models, using the Forvis Mazars modelling methodology. The course will teach participants how to build a data center model from scratch using Excel, analysing project viability through cashflow analysis, and applying key metrics such as IRR, NPV, power usage effectiveness, and capacity utilisation. The course blends theory and practice discussing risk management and reviewing financing structures tailored to the digital sector.

### Who should attend this course?

Whether you’re a developer, owner, investor or advisor, Financial Modelling for Digital Infrastructure Projects offers critical insights and a solid foundation for making informed financial decisions to drive your projects forward into the dynamic and rapidly growing digital sector.

Course Agenda
-------------

### Day One

### Master proven techniques for best practice modelling, resulting in better models, built faster

*   Learn to work with our powerful and easy-to-follow financial modelling methodology
*   Discuss and identify good versus bad modelling techniques using real-life examples
*   Learn how to greatly increase consistency amongst your team in calculation techniques and presentation

Develop a robust and scalable model structure that can evolve with a typical project or transaction:  
Construction

*   Establish key constants in the model using full scope of Excel range name function
*   Construct modularised units that form the building blocks of a professional model, including (but not limited to: Debt and equity drawdowns during development phase

Operations:

*   Study capacity calculations and their relations around energy usage
*   Review revenue build-up based on capacity revenues, reimbursable power costs, recurring and non-recurring revenues
*   Discuss operational expenses, including a focus on the cost of water

### Day Two

### Understand the structure of cashflow waterfalls and the importance of ‘seniority’:

*   Focus on how to calculate Cash Flow Available for Debt Service
*   Learn how to layout line items in an efficient and communicable way
*   Understand the different types of circular references and how to avoid them

Integrate project finance term and learn powerful techniques for alternative repayment methods for debt sculpting:

*   Understand how to effectively model debt sculpting
*   Build in switches to allow different structures to be rapidly analysed, including capitalising interest
*   Work on the inclusion of target Debt Service Coverage Ratio repayment functionality
*   Discuss of linear and bullet repayment structures in project finance

Calculate key financial and operation portfolio metrics and understand the role of dividends policy:

*   Discuss the reason and impact of dividends policy on valuations
*   Integrate internal rate of returns and NPV, understand the role of the discount rate
*   Learn differences between NPV and XNPV functions and study common errors in their application
*   Study key metrics around operations such as Power Usage Effectiveness, Capacity Utilisation and Lease commitments

Build and analyse returns and exit strategies through the powerful scenario manager:

*   Integrate the internal rate of returns and NPV, understand the role of the discount rate
*   Learn differences between NPV and XNPV functions and common errors in their application
*   Review exit strategies through flexing key variables such as exit date and exit multiple
*   Produce data tables to provide meaningful analysis to present to key stakeholders
*   Learn how to save critical time and deliver rapid analysis using our unique approach to scenario management
