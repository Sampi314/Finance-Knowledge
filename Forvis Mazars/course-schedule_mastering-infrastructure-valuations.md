Mastering Infrastructure Valuations is a two day training course and workshop that provides real life skills and insight into how valuations in the infrastructure & energy spaces are performed and considered.

You will develop a deep understanding on the various approaches to valuing infrastructure assets supported through theory, real life examples and a hands on case-study.

Led by Forvis Mazars valuation experts you will learn how real life valuations are approached and carried out. You will also learn key financial modelling skills and appreciate how to approach & develop any model for your valuations.

### What you will take away from this course

*   An understanding the key concepts of valuation and the sources of information available to you
*   Insight into how to address practical challenges in applying valuation techniques
*   Sector specific insights and unique considerations for infrastructure valuations
*   A robust and best practice approach to the required modelling for your valuations

### Key learnings

*   Learn through an applied case study how to approach the valuation of an infrastructure asset
*   Discuss & review the various valuation methodologies understanding the pros and cons of each
*   Understand the theory & importance of discount rates and how these can vary across sectors
*   Learn efficient modelling techniques to deliver more analysis, faster and more accurately
*   Build a core understanding of how to use and adapt a model for valuation purposes

### Is this programme for you?

Yes, if you are involved in or wanting to understand the approach to infrastructure valuations from experts in the field. Typical attendees include investment analysts and corporate finance professionals.

### Course prerequisites

While no pre-course knowledge is strictly required, it would be advantageous for participants to have a good handle on Excel & preferably some financial modelling experience prior to attending the course. Upon registration you are entitled to our introductory material on Excel functions which will give you the confidence and skills to fully absorb the contents of this course.

Course Outline
--------------

### Gain a deep understanding of WACC and CAPM and the application of discount rates

*   Learn the difference between CAPM and WACC and when to apply each of these
*   Understand the distinction between FCFF and FCFE and how to identify these
*   Discuss key concepts such as alphas, betas & risk-free rates and how these influence CAPM
*   Calculate and apply WACC & CAPM to a case study & discuss the pros & cons

### Implement DCF analysis and learn techniques for simplifying this in your models

*   Understand the application of a valuation sheet and layer this into a case study model
*   Discuss the common errors to avoid and learn the best Excel functions for the job
*   Learn how to approach advanced topics such as midpoint discounting and varied timelines
*   Understand the interplay between input assumptions and choice of discount rate
*   Use sensitivity analysis to assess your discount rate and its underlying assumptions

### Learn how to approach a comparable valuation analysis

*   Gain an understanding of how to establish true comparability and alignment of key financials
*   Understand how comparable companies can be useful for infrastructure valuations
*   Review sector specific multiples and discuss the differences between these
*   Discuss the different types of multiples and apply these to a case study

### Benchmark and justify your valuation

*   Appreciate the importance of benchmarking your valuation and triangulating your results
*   Use benchmarking to critically evaluate the underlying assumptions of your valuation
*   Implement a valuation bridge into your analysis and learn and how this can be helpful

### Specific considerations for infrastructure and energy valuations

*   Learn how to account for macroeconomic factors such as inflation, interest rates and country risk premiums
*   Discuss & assess the impact that debt can have on equity valuations
*   Understand the approach to valuations in volatile or new sectors with a lack of benchmarks
*   Discuss the nuances of valuations in key sectors such as renewable energy and transport
