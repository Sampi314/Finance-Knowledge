# WACC Calculation Example

## Setup

We want to calculate the Weighted Average Cost of Capital (WACC) to use as the discount rate for a DCF valuation.

## Assumptions

- Cost of equity ($R_e$): 10.5%
- Pre-tax cost of debt ($R_d$): 6.0%
- Tax rate ($T$): 25%
- Equity weight ($E/V$): 80%
- Debt weight ($D/V$): 20%

## Walk-through

1. Weight the cost of equity:
   $0.80 \times 10.5\% = 8.4\%$
2. Calculate the after-tax cost of debt:
   $6.0\% \times (1 - 0.25) = 4.5\%$
3. Weight the after-tax cost of debt:
   $0.20 \times 4.5\% = 0.9\%$
4. Sum the weighted components:
   $WACC = 8.4\% + 0.9\% = 9.3\%$

## Result

The company's WACC is **9.3%**.

## What this teaches

- WACC combines the cost of equity and after-tax cost of debt, weighted by their respective proportions of total capital.
- The interest tax shield reduces the effective cost of debt (from 6.0% down to 4.5%).
- The final WACC of 9.3% represents the minimum return the company must earn to satisfy all capital providers.
