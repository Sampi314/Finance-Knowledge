# Company A vs Company B EV/EBITDA comparison

## Setup

This example illustrates why EV/EBITDA multiples produce more accurate comparisons than Equity Value multiples (like P/E) when companies have different capital structures. We will compare two companies with identical operating performance but different leverage profiles.

## Assumptions

**Company A:**
- Equity Value: $800M
- Debt: $400M
- Cash: $100M
- EBITDA: $200M

**Company B:**
- Equity Value: $900M
- Debt: $100M
- Cash: $50M
- EBITDA: $200M

## Walk-through

1. Calculate EV for Company A:
$$ EV_A = \$800M + \$400M - \$100M = \$1,100M $$

2. Calculate EV/EBITDA for Company A:
$$ EV/EBITDA_A = \$1,100M / \$200M = 5.5x $$

3. Calculate EV for Company B:
$$ EV_B = \$900M + \$100M - \$50M = \$950M $$

4. Calculate EV/EBITDA for Company B:
$$ EV/EBITDA_B = \$950M / \$200M = 4.75x $$

## Result

Company A's EV/EBITDA is 5.5x, while Company B's is 4.75x.

## What this teaches

- Although Company B has higher Equity Value, Company A is actually valued more expensively on an EV/EBITDA basis (5.5x vs 4.75x).
- Company A's higher debt load does not distort this comparison because Enterprise Value is capital structure-neutral.
- If we used a P/E multiple instead, we would get a misleading comparison because Company A's higher interest expense would reduce Net Income and distort the multiple.
