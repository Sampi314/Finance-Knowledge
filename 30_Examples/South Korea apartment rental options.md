# South Korea apartment rental options

## Setup

When renting an apartment in South Korea, there are typically two options: 전세 (Jeon-se) and 월세 (Wol-se). We will use these two options to illustrate the time value of money and opportunity cost. We want to determine which option is financially better.

## Assumptions

- Apartment value: $200,000
- Option 1 (Jeon-se): $150,000 upfront deposit (75% of value), $0 monthly rent. Deposit returned after 2 years.
- Option 2 (Wol-se): $10,000 upfront deposit (5% of value), $1,000 monthly rent ($12k/year, $24k for 2 years). Deposit returned after 2 years.
- Opportunity cost (investment return rate): 8.2% per year

## Walk-through

1.  **Analyze Option 1:** You pay a $150k deposit and get it back in 2 years. The nominal "money lost" to rent is $0.
2.  **Analyze Option 2:** You pay a $10k deposit, $24k in rent over 2 years, and get the $10k deposit back. The nominal "money lost" is $24k.
3.  **Factor in Opportunity Cost:** Option 1 requires an *extra* $140,000 deposit compared to Option 2. What could you have earned by investing that $140k instead of tying it up in a deposit?
4.  **Calculate lost earnings (Option 1):**
    - Year 1 Lost Earnings: $140,000 * 8.2% = ~$11,480 (rounded to $12k in the BIWS example)
    - Year 2 Lost Earnings: ($140,000 + ~$11,480) * 8.2% = ~$12,421 (so total lost earnings is ~$24,000)

## Result

With an 8.2% opportunity cost, the total cost of Option 1 (the lost investment earnings of ~$24k) is equal to the total cost of Option 2 ($24k in rent). If your opportunity cost (potential investment return) is *higher* than 8.2%, Option 2 is financially superior, even though it appears to cost more "on paper" in nominal dollars.

## What this teaches

- You cannot just look at the absolute nominal dollars paid and returned; you must consider what the money could earn if invested elsewhere.
- Receiving your full deposit back in the future doesn't mean the transaction was "free" because of opportunity cost.
- The time value of money fundamentally changes how you should evaluate upfront payments versus payments over time.
