# Commercial real estate cash-on-cash return example

## Setup

An investor purchases a commercial rental property and wants to calculate the implied cash yield for the current year.

## Assumptions

- Potential Gross Income: $100,000
- Vacancy and Credit Loss: $25,000
- Operating Expenses: $30,000
- Mortgage Payments (Debt Service): $20,000
- Equity Invested (Initial Cash Outlay): $200,000

## Walk-through

1. **Calculate Effective Gross Income (EGI)**
   - EGI = Potential Gross Income - Vacancy and Credit Loss
   - EGI = $100,000 - $25,000 = $75,000
2. **Calculate Net Operating Income (NOI)**
   - NOI = EGI - Operating Expenses
   - NOI = $75,000 - $30,000 = $45,000
3. **Calculate Annual Pre-Tax Cash Flow**
   - Cash Flow = NOI - Mortgage Payments
   - Cash Flow = $45,000 - $20,000 = $25,000
4. **Calculate Cash-on-Cash Return**
   - Return = Annual Pre-Tax Cash Flow / Equity Invested
   - Return = $25,000 / $200,000 = 12.5%

## Result

The implied cash-on-cash return is 12.5%.

## What this teaches

- Shows how to bridge from top-line potential income down to levered pre-tax cash flow.
- Demonstrates that the return is based solely on the actual cash invested, not the total property value.
