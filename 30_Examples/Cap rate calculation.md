# Cap rate calculation

## Setup

A commercial real estate (CRE) investment firm is analyzing a potential rental property investment opportunity—the strategic acquisition of an office building in NYC—at stabilization (Year 1).

## Assumptions

- Property Value: $84 million
- Potential Gross Income (PGI): $16 million
- Vacancy and Credit Losses: $800k
- Property Taxes: $4 million
- Property Management Fees: $2.5 million
- Property Insurance: $2 million
- Maintenance and Repairs: $1.2 million
- Other Direct Operating Expenses: $500k

## Walk-through

1. Calculate Effective Gross Income (EGI) by subtracting Vacancy and Credit Losses from Potential Gross Income (PGI).
   $16 million - $800k = $15.2 million
2. Deduct direct operating expenses from the EGI to find the Net Operating Income (NOI).
   $15.2 million - $4 million - $2.5 million - $2 million - $1.2 million - $500k = $5 million
3. Divide the commercial real estate property’s net operating income (NOI) by the current property value to arrive at a pro-forma cap rate.
   $5 million ÷ $84 million = 6.0%

## Result

The commercial rental property's implied pro-forma cap rate is 6.0%.

## What this teaches

- The effective gross income (EGI) of the property must be estimated as the first step toward determining the cap rate.
- The property's NOI at stabilization is estimated by subtracting direct operating expenses from effective gross income (EGI).
- The direct capitalization method states the value of a property can be estimated by dividing its stabilized NOI by the market cap rate.
