# Gross Potential Rent Calculation Example

## Setup

Suppose a commercial real estate (CRE) investor is creating a near-term pro forma forecast model on a potential investment, a commercial office building located in NYC. We want to determine the gross potential rent (GPR).

## Assumptions

- Total Number of Rental Units = 40 Units
- Gross Rental Rate Per Month = $25k

## Walk-through

1. Determine the gross rental rate per month ($25k).
2. Determine the total number of rental units (40 Units).
3. Multiply the total number of rental units by the gross rental rate.
4. Annualize the computed figure by multiplying by 12.
5. Gross Potential Rent (GPR) = 40 Units × $25k × 12.0x = $12 million

## Result

The Gross Potential Rent (GPR) is $12 million.

## What this teaches

- GPR is calculated by simply multiplying the number of units by the gross rental rate.
- Since the gross rental rate is expressed on a monthly basis, it must be annualized to arrive at the annual gross potential rent.
