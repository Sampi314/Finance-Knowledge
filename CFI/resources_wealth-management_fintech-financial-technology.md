What is Fintech? Overview of the Financial Technology Industry
==============================================================

The synergy between finance and technology that is used to enhance business operations and the delivery of financial services

Written by [CFI Team](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/about-cfi/)

Published January 30, 2020

Read Time 5 minutes

What is Fintech (Financial Technology)?
---------------------------------------

![Fintech (Financial Technology)](https://cdn.corporatefinanceinstitute.com/assets/fintech-financial-technology.jpeg)

As financial services companies adapt to rising consumer expectations and shifting technology, a wave of fintech innovation is transforming how people and businesses manage money. From digital banking to disruptive fintech startups, the industry is evolving faster than ever. 

But what is fintech, and why does it matter to today’s economy?

Fintech is a combination of the words “finance” and “technology”, and refers to the integration of technology into financial services to enhance efficiency, accessibility, and user experience. At its core, fintech, meaning the use of digital tools to streamline and modernize the financial industry, reflects a shift toward more user-centric and agile service models. The [fintech industry](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/resources/fintech/understanding-the-fintech-industry/)
 encompasses a broad spectrum of innovations, from mobile banking and digital payments to blockchain and algorithmic trading.

While fintech is often associated with cutting-edge technologies like artificial intelligence (AI), [blockchain](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/resources/cryptocurrency/blockchain/)
, and [decentralized finance (DeFi)](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/resources/cryptocurrency/decentralized-finance/)
, it also includes everyday financial applications, such as online banking, automated investing, and mobile payment platforms. Even your Starbucks app is a form of financial technology, in that it facilitates payments and a proprietary rewards program, using a mobile device.

### **Key Highlights**

*   **Fintech is the intersection of finance and technology, transforming the financial services industry.**
*   **Innovations in digital banking, payments, lending, and investing are redefining traditional finance.**
*   **The industry continues to grow, with new technologies shaping the future of financial services.**

**Evolution and Applications of Financial Technology**
------------------------------------------------------

What is financial technology if not the natural evolution of how we interact with money and financial data? While often viewed as a modern phenomenon, the foundations of finance technology have been in place for decades. Looking at key milestones offers valuable examples of financial technology that have shaped the industry’s ongoing transformation:

*   **1950s**: Introduction of credit cards, revolutionizing cashless payments.
*   **1960s**: The first automated teller machine (ATM) is deployed.
*   **1990s**: Online banking emerges, reducing reliance on physical branches.
*   **2000s**: The rise of PayPal and e-commerce-driven payment solutions.
*   **2010s-Present**: Blockchain, AI-driven finance, and digital-only banks reshape the financial landscape.

The technologies that underpin fintech business models vary considerably. They include blockchain technology, artificial intelligence (AI), [machine learning](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/course/loan-default-prediction-with-machine-learning/)
, and other big data functions like robotic process automation (RPA). Each use case is unique, but the underlying theme is a collective effort to disaggregate the financial services sector, which has historically enjoyed a highly protected status due to high levels of regulation.

**Key Aspects of Financial Technology**
---------------------------------------

Understanding what is the fintech industry goes beyond definitions—it’s about how technology is reshaping financial transactions in real time. From mobile payments to AI-driven lending, fintech is transforming service delivery while bringing new focus to regulatory compliance across the financial landscape.

### **1\. Digital Payments & Mobile Banking**

*   Mobile banking: something that hundreds of millions of people around the world take for granted – is actually technology supporting the delivery of traditional banking services.
*   Apps like Apple Pay, Google Pay, and Venmo simplify transactions.

### **2\. Lending & Credit Innovations**

*   Buy Now, Pay Later (BNPL) solutions from companies like Affirm and Klarna.
*   Peer-to-peer (P2P) lending platforms enable direct lending between individuals.
*   AI-driven underwriting and risk assessment streamline loan approvals.

### **3\. Blockchain & Cryptocurrencies**

*   Cryptocurrencies like Bitcoin and Ethereum offer alternative payment solutions.
*   Smart contracts enable decentralized transactions without intermediaries.
*   DeFi, short for decentralized finance, is a by-product of fintech that aims to eliminate intermediaries from traditional financial transactions.

### **4\. Investment & Wealth Management**

*   Robo-advisors (e.g., Wealthsimple) automate investment strategies.
*   Commission-free trading platforms (e.g., Robinhood) disrupt traditional brokerages.
*   The business of investing has been particularly transformed, with online discount brokerages forcing traditional firms to waive fees to stay competitive.

How Fintechs are Disrupting Traditional Financial Services Firms
----------------------------------------------------------------

As fintech continues to evolve, the [traditional financial services industry](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/course/banking-products-and-services/)
 is being reshaped in several key ways. In fact, the ongoing disruption is redefining fintech for both institutions and end users, pushing it beyond tools into a full reimagining of how money moves and works. Below are some of the most significant areas of disruption:

Traditional financial services institutions (mainly banks and credit unions) serve three core functions:

1.  **They hold money**, including deposits and a variety of investment products.
2.  **They lend money,** including both secured loans (like mortgages) and unsecured loans (like student lines of credit).
3.  **They move money:** everything from simple, everyday payments to international money transfers using global networks like [SWIFT](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/resources/foreign-exchange/swift/)
     (Society for Worldwide Interbank Financial Telecommunications).

Each of these core functions is being transformed by fintech innovations, providing consumers and businesses with faster, more accessible, and cost-effective alternatives.

### **1\. Payments & Money Transfers**

Fintech companies are offering faster and often cheaper alternatives to traditional money transfer networks like SWIFT. Examples include:

*   **Stripe and Square:** Revolutionizing online and in-person payments.
*   [**Cryptocurrencies**](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/course/introduction-to-cryptocurrency/)
     **& Blockchain:** Enabling decentralized and borderless transactions.

### **2\. Digital Lending & Credit**

Fintech innovations are streamlining lending processes, reducing reliance on traditional financial institutions.

*   **AI-powered loan underwriting** accelerates credit approvals.
*   **BNPL services** allow consumers to finance purchases interest-free.
*   **P2P lending** enables direct funding between individuals, bypassing banks.

### **3\. Digital-Only Banks & Investment Platforms**

*   **Neobanks** (e.g., Chime, Revolut) operate without physical branches, offering low-fee banking services.

[**Robo-advisors**](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/resources/wealth-management/robo-advisors/)
 and commission-free trading platforms democratize investing.

**The Future of Fintech**
-------------------------

Fintech now serves as a lens through which financial innovation is accelerating, reshaping systems in response to technology and consumer demand. As businesses and individuals increasingly adopt new technologies, fintech will continue to break down traditional barriers, offering greater accessibility, efficiency, and innovation in financial services.

As financial institutions, startups, and regulators navigate this changing environment, several key trends are set to define the future of fintech: The financial technology sector will continue to evolve, with emerging trends such as:

*   **Embedded finance**: Financial services are increasingly being embedded into everyday platforms, allowing consumers to access loans, payments, and banking services directly within apps they already use, such as e-commerce sites and ride-sharing platforms.
*   **Decentralized Finance (DeFi)**: Built on blockchain technology, DeFi aims to eliminate intermediaries in financial transactions, offering decentralized lending, trading, and investment opportunities.
*   **Regtech (Regulatory Technology)**: Automating compliance and risk management processes for financial institutions, reducing costs and improving accuracy in meeting regulatory requirements.
*   **Insurtech**: Leveraging AI and big data to transform insurance underwriting, risk assessment, and claims processing, leading to more personalized and efficient insurance products.

----------------------------------------------

From digital payments and AI-driven lending to blockchain-based financial systems, fintech is transforming every aspect of the financial ecosystem. Keeping up with these innovations is no longer optional, but a necessity for staying relevant in the industry.
