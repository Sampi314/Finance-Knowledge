What is Asset Disposal?
-----------------------

Asset disposal is the removal of a long-term asset from the company’s [accounting records](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/resources/accounting/three-financial-statements/)
. It is an important concept because [capital assets are](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/resources/accounting/types-of-assets/)
 essential to successful business operations. Moreover, proper accounting of the disposal of an asset is critical to maintaining updated and clean accounting records.

The asset disposal may be a result of several events:

*   An asset is fully depreciated and must be disposed of.
*   An asset is sold because it is no longer useful or needed.
*   An asset must be removed from the books due to unforeseen circumstances (e.g., theft).

### Journal Entries for Asset Disposals

The [journal entries](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/resources/accounting/journal-entries-guide/)
 required to record the disposal of an asset depend on the situation in which the event occurs.

Let’s consider the following example to analyze the different situations that require an asset disposal.

Motors Inc. owns a machinery asset on its balance sheet worth $3,000.

#### Scenario 1: Disposal of Fully Depreciated Asset

Motors Inc. estimated the machinery’s [useful life](https://smallbusiness.chron.com/determine-expected-useful-life-accounting-74452.html)
 to be three years. The annual [depreciation expense](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/resources/accounting/what-is-depreciation-expense/)
 is $1,000. At the end of the third year, the machinery is fully depreciated, and the asset must be disposed of.

### Asset Disposal on Financial Statements

The asset disposal results in a direct effect on the company’s financial statements. In all scenarios, this affects the [balance sheet](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/resources/accounting/balance-sheet/)
 by removing a capital asset.

Also, if a company disposes of assets by selling with gain or loss, the gain and loss should be reported on the income statement.
