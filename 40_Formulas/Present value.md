# Present value

$$PV = \frac{FV}{(1 + r)^n}$$

**Variables**

- $PV$ — Present value, the current worth of a future sum of money.
- $FV$ — Future value, the amount of money in the future.
- $r$ — Discount rate, representing the hurdle rate or cost of capital for a given period.
- $n$ — Number of periods into the future.

**Notes**

When compounding occurs over multiple periods, the future value is divided by the discount factor $(1 + r)^n$. This reflects the fact that as the time horizon ($n$) increases, the present value decreases because the future payment is farther away.
