# Hamada equation

$$\beta_{unlevered} = \frac{\beta_{levered}}{1 + (1-T) \times \frac{D}{E}}$$

$$\beta_{levered} = \beta_{unlevered} \times \left(1 + (1-T) \times \frac{D}{E}\right)$$

**Variables**

- $\beta_{unlevered}$ — Asset beta, reflecting pure business risk without debt effects.
- $\beta_{levered}$ — Equity beta, reflecting total equity risk including both business and financial risk.
- $T$ — Marginal corporate tax rate.
- $D$ — Market value of debt.
- $E$ — Market value of equity.
- $D/E$ — Debt-to-equity ratio.

**Notes**

The Hamada equation is the standard formula used in practice to convert between levered and unlevered beta. It assumes debt beta is zero and accounts for the tax shield benefit of debt through the $(1-T)$ factor. When relevering for a target company, ensure you use the target company's specific expected tax rate and target capital structure ($D/E$), rather than those of the comparable company you unlevered from.
