# Cash Flow from Financing

$$\text{Cash Flow from Financing} = \text{Debt Issuances} + \text{Equity Issuances} - \text{Share Buybacks} - \text{Debt Repayment} - \text{Dividends}$$

**Variables**

- $\text{Debt Issuances}$ — Cash inflows from borrowing funds from lenders.
- $\text{Equity Issuances}$ — Cash inflows from raising external financing by issuing shares.
- $\text{Share Buybacks}$ — Cash outflows to repurchase previously issued shares from the open market.
- $\text{Debt Repayment}$ — Cash outflows to repay the principal amount of debt.
- $\text{Dividends}$ — Cash outflows to equity shareholders as a form of return on their investment.

**Notes**

While debt and equity issuances are positive inflows, share buybacks, debt repayments, and dividends represent cash leaving the company, hence they are subtracted.
