# Capital Asset Pricing Model (CAPM)

## Formula

$$
E(R_i) = R_f + \beta_i \times (E(R_m) - R_f)
$$

Where:
- $E(R_i)$ = Expected return of the investment (Cost of Equity)
- $R_f$ = Risk-free rate
- $\beta_i$ = Beta of the investment (measure of systematic risk)
- $E(R_m)$ = Expected return of the market
- $(E(R_m) - R_f)$ = Equity Risk Premium (ERP)

## Plain English

The expected return on an investment is equal to a baseline risk-free rate (what you'd earn on a guaranteed investment like a government bond) plus a premium for taking on risk. This risk premium is determined by multiplying the asset's beta (how volatile the asset is relative to the overall market) by the equity risk premium (the extra return the overall market is expected to provide above the risk-free rate).

## Use cases

CAPM is the standard method used to calculate the Cost of Equity ($K_e$), which is a primary component of the Weighted Average Cost of Capital (WACC) used in Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) valuations.
