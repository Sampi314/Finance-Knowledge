# Equity Value

$$\text{Equity Value} = \text{Share Price} \times \text{Shares Outstanding}$$

$$\text{Equity Value} = \text{Enterprise Value} - \text{Debt} - \text{Preferred Stock} - \text{Non-Controlling Interest} + \text{Cash}$$

**Variables**

- $\text{Share Price}$ — The current market price of a single share of the company's stock.
- $\text{Shares Outstanding}$ — The total number of a company's shares held by all its shareholders, typically measured on a fully diluted basis.
- $\text{Enterprise Value}$ — The total value of the company's operating business to all capital providers.
- $\text{Debt}$ — All interest-bearing obligations of the company.
- $\text{Preferred Stock}$ — Equity that has a higher claim on assets and earnings than common stock.
- $\text{Non-Controlling Interest}$ — The portion of a subsidiary's equity not owned by the parent company (also called minority interest).
- $\text{Cash}$ — Cash and cash equivalents.

**Notes**

The first formula is used for public companies (market capitalization), while the second formula is the bridge used to move between enterprise value and equity value for any company.
