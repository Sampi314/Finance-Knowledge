# Retained earnings formula

$$
\text{RE}_{\text{end}} = \text{RE}_{\text{beginning}} + \text{Net Income} - \text{Cash Dividends} - \text{Stock Dividends}
$$

**Variables**

- $\text{RE}_{\text{end}}$ — The ending retained earnings balance for the period
- $\text{RE}_{\text{beginning}}$ — The beginning retained earnings balance from the prior period
- $\text{Net Income}$ — The total revenue minus all expenses, taxes, and interest (can also be a net loss)
- $\text{Cash Dividends}$ — Dividends paid out to shareholders in cash
- $\text{Stock Dividends}$ — Dividends distributed to shareholders in the form of additional stock

**Notes**

If a company operates at a net loss for the period, that loss is subtracted from the beginning retained earnings balance.
