# Days Inventory Outstanding (DIO)

$$\text{DIO} = \left( \frac{\text{Average Inventory}}{\text{Cost of Goods Sold}} \right) \times 365$$

**Variables**

- $\text{Average Inventory}$ — The average value of a company's inventory during a period (typically calculated as (Beginning Inventory + Ending Inventory) / 2)
- $\text{Cost of Goods Sold}$ — [[COGS and gross margin|The direct costs incurred by a company while selling its goods]]

**Notes**

Alternatively, DIO can be calculated using the Inventory Turnover ratio: $\text{DIO} = \frac{365}{\text{Inventory Turnover}}$
