# Control premium formula

$$\text{Premium} = \frac{\text{Offer Price} - \text{Unaffected Price}}{\text{Unaffected Price}}$$

**Variables**

- $\text{Premium}$ — The additional percentage an acquirer pays above a company's current trading price to gain majority ownership and control rights.
- $\text{Offer Price}$ — The final per-share price agreed upon in the acquisition.
- $\text{Unaffected Price}$ — The stock price of the target company before any news or rumors of the acquisition leaked, typically 1 day, 1 week, or 1 month prior to the announcement.

**Notes**

Control premiums typically range from 25-40%, though they can vary based on competitive bidding, strategic value, expected synergies, and overall market conditions.
