# After-tax Cost of Debt

$$\text{After-tax Cost of Debt} = R_d \times (1 - T)$$

**Variables**

- $R_d$ — Pre-tax cost of debt (e.g., Yield to Maturity or Risk-free rate + Credit Spread)
- $T$ — Corporate tax rate

**Notes**

This formula calculates the effective cost of debt financing for a company. Because interest payments are tax-deductible, the actual cost to the company is lower than the stated pre-tax interest rate. This tax benefit is known as the interest tax shield.
