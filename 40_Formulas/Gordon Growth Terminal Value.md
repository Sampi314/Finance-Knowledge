# Gordon Growth Terminal Value

$$\text{Terminal Value} = \frac{FCF_{n} \times (1 + g)}{WACC - g}$$

**Variables**

- $FCF_{n}$ — Free cash flow in the final projection year
- $g$ — Perpetual growth rate
- $WACC$ — Weighted average cost of capital ([[WACC]])

**Notes**

The perpetual growth rate ($g$) must be less than WACC. It cannot exceed long-term GDP growth and typically reflects long-term inflation or economic growth (e.g., 2% to 3% for US companies). This method is theoretically sound and used for stable, mature businesses with predictable growth.
