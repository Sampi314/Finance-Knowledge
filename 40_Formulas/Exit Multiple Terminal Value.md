# Exit Multiple Terminal Value

$$\text{Terminal Value} = \text{EBITDA}_{n} \times \text{Exit Multiple}$$

**Variables**

- $\text{EBITDA}_{n}$ — EBITDA in the final projection year
- $\text{Exit Multiple}$ — The valuation multiple applied at exit, usually based on comparable companies or precedent transactions.

**Notes**

While EBITDA is the most common metric, revenue multiples are sometimes used for high-growth or unprofitable companies. The exit multiple represents what a buyer would pay for the business at the end of the projection period. It assumes market conditions and company comparability remain similar at the exit date.
