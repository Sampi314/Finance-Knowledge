Power BI has a _**LOT**_ of chart types. More than 30 of them. So, picking the _correct_ chart for a data analysis situation is hard. Here is a picture of a frustrated Power BI developer trying to figure out the right chart for her data analysis.

![A Power BI developer frustrated trying to figure out which chart to use...](https://chandoo.org/wp/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/what-chart-do-i-use-powerbi.jpg)

In this article, let me explain when & how to use these Power BI charts along with my top tips to make them.

The Power BI Visualization (Charting) Options
---------------------------------------------

Here are the 30+ charting options in Power BI (as of July 2023). In this article, I am going to cover a majority of them.

![](https://chandoo.org/wp/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/SNAG-2737.png)

We can categorize these charts into below groups.

*   Bar / Column Charts 📊
*   Line & Area Charts 📈
*   Pie & Donut Charts 🍩
*   Card Visuals 💳
*   Tables & Matrices 📰
*   Scatter (XY) plots 
*   Maps 🗺
*   Everything Else

Let’s understand when & how to use these charts now.

Bar & Column Charts in Power BI
-------------------------------

**When:**  Use them to compare things

### **Examples**

[Default Title](https://chandoo.org/wp/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/barchart-power-bi.png)
[Column Chart](https://chandoo.org/wp/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/column-chart-power-bi.png)
[With "legend"](https://chandoo.org/wp/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/stacked-bar-chart-powerbi.png)
[With Levels](https://chandoo.org/wp/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/powerbi-chart-drilldown-demo.gif)
[Small Multiples](https://chandoo.org/wp/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/bar-chart-with-small-multiples.png)

### **Top Tips**

*   Use the “Legend” option to split the chart
*   If you put more then one field in the “axis”, you can **_drilldown_** on the charts. 
*   You can also split the chart into many using the “small multiple” option. [See this video to learn more](https://youtu.be/Jzu2wfyYF9I)
    .
*   When using “time” or “date” axis, try the column charts instead of bar charts. 

Line & Area Charts in Power BI
------------------------------

**When:**  Use them to see the trends

### **Examples**

[Line chart](https://chandoo.org/wp/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/power-bi-line-chart.png)
[Area chart](https://chandoo.org/wp/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/area-chart.png)
[Stacked chart](https://chandoo.org/wp/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/stacked-area-chart-powerbi.png)
[Don't do this](https://chandoo.org/wp/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/line-chart-dont-do-this-pbi.png)
[Forecasting](https://chandoo.org/wp/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/line-chart-with-forecast-powerbi.png)

### **Top Tips**

*   Convert line to “stepped” line chart by enabling the “stepped” option in formats.
*   Enable markers to improve the line chart readability.
*   When you have a line chart with date axis, you can use the “analytics” options to introduce forecast or trend lines.
*   Don’t make line chart with “things” on axis. They are meaningless (_most of the time)._

Pie & Donut Charts
------------------

**When:**  To understand the full picture & contribution

### **Examples**

[Pie chart](https://chandoo.org/wp/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/pie-chart-pbi.png)
[Donut chart](https://chandoo.org/wp/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/donut-chart-in-power-bi.png)
[Too many slices ?](https://chandoo.org/wp/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/dont-make-rainbow-pies-powerbi.png)

### **Top Tips**

*   Always customize the labels and legend to get best look for your pies (or donuts)
*   Avoid making too many slices. No one likes a tiny slice of pie.

Card Visuals
------------

**When:**  To share single numbers, KPIs & messages

### **Examples**

[Card](https://chandoo.org/wp/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/card-visual-powerbi.png)
[Multi-row Card](https://chandoo.org/wp/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/multi-row-card-demo-powerbi.png)
[New Card](https://chandoo.org/wp/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/new-card-visual-powerbi.png)
[Add Context](https://chandoo.org/wp/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/add-context-to-your-charts.png)

### **Top Tips**

*   Add context to your cards whenever possible. This is a powerful way to engage your audience. [Read this article for more](https://chandoo.org/wp/never-use-simple-numbers-in-your-dashboards/)
    .
*   Power BI recently introduced a “NEW Card” visual. Give it a try. It lets you build cards with more formatting options and it is faster than regular cards.

Table & Matrix Visuals in Power BI
----------------------------------

**When:**  To show details

### **Examples**

[Table](https://chandoo.org/wp/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/simple-table-pbi.png)
[Matrix](https://chandoo.org/wp/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/simple-matrxi-pbi.png)
[Formatted Table](https://chandoo.org/wp/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/enhanced-table-with-formats-powerbi.png)
[Formatted Matrix](https://chandoo.org/wp/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/formatted-power-bi-matrix.png)

### **Top Tips**

*   Adjust row padding on tables & matrices to make them look easy on eyes.
*   Add conditional formatting to one or two fields to elevate the data.
*   Apply correct sort order on your tables. If you hold SHIFT while sorting the tables, you can sort on multiple columns!
*   When you have lots of data, Filter your tables down to just important points.

Power BI Maps
-------------

**When:**  To explain geographical data

### **Examples**

[Map chart](https://chandoo.org/wp/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/power-bi-maps-demo.png)
[Azure Map](https://chandoo.org/wp/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/azure-map-visual-powerbi.png)
[3D Column](https://chandoo.org/wp/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/3d-column-chart-in-power-bi-map.png)

### **Top Tips**

*   Power BI has a lot of map visuals. Try them all to see which one gives you best fit for your data. My favorite is the new Azure Map Visual. [Learn more about it here](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/azure/azure-maps/power-bi-visual-conversion)
    .
*   While maps are useful, I find them clunky. So try tables or other charts from time to time.
*   Do not overlay useless information like traffic on to maps. Keep them light so the focus can be on the data.

Scatter (XY) Plot in Power BI
-----------------------------

**When:**  To explain 2 dimensions in one picture & to explore relationships

### **Examples**

[X Y Plot in Power BI](https://chandoo.org/wp/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/xyplot-power-bi.png)
[Animated X Y Plot](https://chandoo.org/wp/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/animated-scatter-plot-powerbi-demo.gif)

### **Top Tips**

*   XY plots are great for exploring relationships. But remember the golden rule – [Correlation is not same as causation](https://chandoo.org/wp/correlation-vs-causation/)
    .
*   If you have a Date dimension, use it on the “Play Axis” option to make a cool animated XY graph in Power BI.

Waterfall Chart
---------------

**When:**  To explain how things have changed from one point / place to another. 

### **Examples**

[Waterfall chart](https://chandoo.org/wp/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/waterfall-chart-demo-power-bi.png)

### **Top Tips**

*   Use the “explain” feature of Power BI to auto-generate useful waterfall graphs for you.

Don't forget
------------

**Don’t forget title, sub-title, legend, labels & tool-tips.** 

These can make or break a chart.

### **Examples**

[Default Title](https://chandoo.org/wp/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/barchart-power-bi.png)
[Great Title & Highlight](https://chandoo.org/wp/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/highlights-and-title-powerbi.png)
[Tooltips](https://chandoo.org/wp/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/powerbi-tooltips-demo.gif)

### **Top Tips**

*   Don’t settle for default titles. Write a great title & subtitle on your charts to elevate them.
*   Highlight important data points using conditional formatting feature. [Here is a tutorial.](https://chandoo.org/wp/conditionally-format-visuals-in-power-bi/)
    
*   Set up a tooltip page to further explain your data. [Here is a tutorial on Power BI Tooltips](https://chandoo.org/wp/how-to-create-tooltips-in-power-bi/)
    .

Everything Else...?
-------------------

**Use with caution.**

When in doubt, try them with your audience, get their feedback and proceed as needed.
