Mary Ellen, one of our readers, has an interesting conundrum,

_I have some data that goes to Pivot table then to pivot chart. There is a slicer to filter the data. But when I slice, my pivot chart gets messed up. How to have the slicer, but still see the insights in the chart?!?_

See above demo to understand:

This is because when you slice by a school, the pivot table gets filtered and hence % row total for that school becomes 100% (as there are no other schools).

_**How to fix the problem?**_ The easy answer is to remove the slicers. _But we want to have our slicers and eat a slice of them too._ So we crank up the Excel awesomeness valve and get to work. There are two ways to achieve what we want.

*   **Old school method:** Two pivot tables, some formulas & a line chart
*   **New Excel method:** Power Pivot and a line chart
