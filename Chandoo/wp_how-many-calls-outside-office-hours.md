**_Time for another Excel formula / Power Query challenge._** This is based on a common business data analysis problem. Say you have two tables – calls log and office hours. Call log tells when each call is received. Office hours tell us working hours for seven days of the week. We want to know **how many calls are outside office hours**.

![Count calls outside office hours - how to count or identify them? Business data analysis in Excel](https://chandoo.org/wp/wp-content/uploads/2019/05/calls-outside-office-hours.png)

Count calls outside business hours – problem
--------------------------------------------

So you are up for the challenge eh? Just follow the below steps.

*   [Download the problem data set](https://chandoo.org/wp/wp-content/uploads/2019/05/calls-outside-office-hours-problem.xlsx)
    .
*   There are two tables. calls & working.hours.

You have **three problems** to solve.

1.  Write a formula in calls table to identify if a call is outside office hours. Can be Yes/No or TRUE/FALSE
2.  Write a single formula outside the tables to count how many calls were made outside office hours. The answer should be 693. You can use helper columns (or the column in problem#1) if you want.
3.  Set up a Power Query to filter all outside office hours calls.
