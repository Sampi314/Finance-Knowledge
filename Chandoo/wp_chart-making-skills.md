### 14 basic skills for people making charts (or graphs or plots or ok, you get the point)

1.  Know when to **not** make a chart
2.  Know the basic chart types – [bar charts](http://chandoo.org/wp/tag/bar-charts/)
    , [line charts](http://chandoo.org/wp/line-charts/)
    , [scatter charts](http://peltiertech.com/WordPress/line-charts-vs-xy-charts/)
    , [bubble charts](http://chandoo.org/wp/tag/bubble-charts/)
    , [pie charts](http://chandoo.org/wp/2009/01/16/excel-pie-chart/)
    , [dot plots](http://peltiertech.com/Excel/Charts/DotPlot.html)
     and [more](http://chandoo.org/wp/category/visualization/)
    
3.  Know how to massage, pre-process, arm twist your data so that charts can be constructed
4.  Know formulas like [vlookup](http://chandoo.org/wp/2008/11/19/vlookup-match-and-offset-explained-in-plain-english-spreadcheats/ "vlookup help")
    , [match](http://chandoo.org/wp/2008/11/19/vlookup-match-and-offset-explained-in-plain-english-spreadcheats/ "match excel formula help")
    , [index](https://chandoo.org/wp/chart-making-skills/chandoo.org/wp/2009/05/19/dynamic-charts-in-excel/ "index formula - tutorial")
    , [indirect](http://chandoo.org/wp/2008/11/05/select-show-one-chart-from-many/)
     so that you can get what you want
5.  Know what chart junk means and how to get rid of it
6.  Know the types of [charts to avoid](http://chandoo.org/wp/2008/09/03/6-charts-to-never-use/)
     and the perils of using them
7.  Know how many and what colors to use. Understand concepts like contrast and repetition
8.  Know that [keeping it simple](http://chandoo.org/wp/2009/01/23/charting-principles-kiss/)
     is far more effective than keeping it complex
9.  Know that [charts are stories](http://chandoo.org/wp/2009/05/11/charts-are-stories/)
     and [give priority accordingly](http://chandoo.org/wp/2009/06/17/focus-on-story-not-formatting/)
    .
10.  Know [how to use chart templates](http://chandoo.org/wp/2009/03/10/using-excel-chart-templates/)
    
11.  Know charting concepts like [dynamic charts](http://chandoo.org/wp/tag/dynamic-charts/)
    , [sparklines](http://sparklines-excel.blogspot.com/)
    , [tag clouds](http://chandoo.org/wp/2008/04/22/create-cool-tag-clouds-in-excel-using-vba/)
    , [combining 2 chart types](http://chandoo.org/wp/2009/07/02/secondary-axis-combination-charts-howto/)
     etc.
12.  Know the limitations of tools and use the right ones for the occasion (excel for simple and easy graphs, [manyeyes](http://manyeyes.alphaworks.ibm.com/)
     for complex visualizations, [R](http://learnr.wordpress.com/)
     for fun and elaborate stuff, [Google spreadsheets](http://spreadsheets.google.com/)
     for maps and [organization charts](http://chandoo.org/wp/2008/04/15/generate-organization-charts-in-excel-howto/)
     etc.)
13.  Know that there are beautiful examples around us and learn from them \[[what your zoo can teach you about visualization](https://chandoo.org/wp/chart-making-skills/chandoo.org/wp/2008/08/05/visualization-lessons-from-zoo)\
    \]
14.  Know where to look when you are stuck – [PeltierTech](http://www.peltiertech.com/Excel/ChartsHowTo/index.html)
    , [PHD Charting Pages](http://chandoo.org/wp/category/visualization/)
    , [Google](http://google.com/)
    .

Now, it is time for the big screaming question:

**How many of these skills you have? Where do you think you should improve?**
