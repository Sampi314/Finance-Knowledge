**[Sachin Tendulkar](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sachin_Tendulkar)
 is undoubtedly the best cricketers to play One Day International Cricket.** He is a source of inspiration and joy for me and many others. So, naturally I was jumping with joy when I heard that he scored the highest ever runs in a single match on 24th of Feb. He scored 200 runs (first ever double century in one day cricket), batted for all the 50 overs and remained not out. A true symbol of passion and excellence. \[[his cricinfo profile](http://www.cricinfo.com/india/content/player/35320.html)\
, [commentary on 200 score](http://www.cricinfo.com/indvrsa2010/content/story/449829.html)\
\]

But what has all this got to do with Excel? Well, some of you know that I am fan of Sachin. Last time he became the highest scorer in test cricket, we celebrated that with a [dashboard of test cricket statistics](http://chandoo.org/wp/2008/10/20/excel-dashboard-tutorial/)
. Similarly, now too, I have prepared an info-graphic poster on Sachin showcasing his achievements. See it below,

### Behind the poster:

I have made this poster in Excel 2010 (it has [sparklines](http://chandoo.org/wp/tag/spark-lines/)
, that is why). The data is from [Cricinfo’s statsguru page](http://stats.cricinfo.com/ci/engine/player/35320.html?class=2;template=results;type=batting)
.

[Download the original poster excel file](http://cid-b663e096d6c08c74.skydrive.live.com/self.aspx/Public/vis-pro/sachin-200-runs-record-poster.zip)
 (you need Excel 2007 or above to play with this).

Also, if you want to see the entire poster as one image [click here](http://cid-b663e096d6c08c74.skydrive.live.com/self.aspx/Public/vis-pro/sachin-200-runs-record-poster.png)
