**Here are some Excel links that I recommend.** Go ahead and explore these and learn something cool.

**[Calculating the Clock Face Angles using VBA](http://www.dailydoseofexcel.com/archives/2011/04/21/clock-face-angles/)
**![Calculating the Clock Face Angles using VBA](https://chandoo.org/img/el/clock-face-angle-excel.png)

Dick at DDoE blog shares an interesting worksheet function & VBA code that would calculate the angle between needles of a clock at any given time. I remember solving similar problems back when I was preparing for MBA entrance exams in India. Go ahead and examine the code (or try to solve it on your own) to have some workout for your grey cells.

**[Pivot Tables Rock – Song on Youtube](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=GwbSJFtRmDw&feature=player_embedded)
**

We all know that Pivot Tables rock. They make everything right. But, now there is a song, R&B style. I love the lyrics, especially, “Sittin’ here I got so much data  
Gonna take forever so I think I’ll do it later  
Calculatin’ all these numbers by hand  
Can take a whole afternoon and that sounds pretty bland  
Until my friend tells me all about the pivot table  
Too good to be true, sounds more like a fable” part. Go ahead and watch it. Have a laugh. And if you want to learn more about Pivot Tables, [you know where to go](http://chandoo.org/wp/excel-pivot-tables/)
.

**[Sort Excel Tables by Clicking \[VBA\]](http://www.clearlyandsimply.com/clearly_and_simply/2011/05/sort-excel-tables-by-double-clicking.html)
**

Robert at Clearly and Simply shows us how to use a bit of VBA to enable click sort on Excel tables. Pretty interesting technique, especially for dashboards and reports.

**[Adding Horizontal Bands to Excel Charts](http://peltiertech.com/WordPress/excel-chart-with-horizontal-bands/)
**![Adding Horizontal Bands to Excel Charts](https://chandoo.org/img/el/BandedChartXY.png)

Chart recipe guru, Jon Peltier shows us how to add horizontal bands to Excel Charts. You can use banded charts to indicate performance levels (or any other metric levels). Go ahead and study the technique from Peltier’s blog.

**[Multi-Column Combo Boxes in Excel](http://blog.contextures.com/archives/2011/05/27/multi-column-excel-combo-box/)
**

![Multi-Column Combo Boxes in Excel](https://chandoo.org/img/el/comboboxmulticolumn02.png)Debra shows us a ridiculously simple way to show multi-column combo boxes in Excel. The trick is to use combo box active-x control with columnCount property set to 2.

**[The RAWR chart – understanding profits & units sold in phone industry](http://www.asymco.com/2011/05/18/the-rawr-chart/)
**

![The RAWR chart - understanding profits & units sold in phone industry](https://chandoo.org/img/el/rawr-chart-mobile-phone-sales-vs-profits.png)Horace writes at Asymco, one of my new favorite blogs, where he analyzes data on Mobile phone industry and presents insightful articles. Recently, he showed a chart called RAWR chart, to depict operating profit per unit & number of units sold by various mobile phone manufacturers. You can see how Apple differs from the rest of the pack in this very simple but powerful chart.
