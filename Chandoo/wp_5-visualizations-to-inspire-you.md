5 visualizations to inspire you \[Nov 21\]
==========================================

[Cool Infographics & Data Visualizations](https://chandoo.org/wp/category/cool-infographics/)
 - 1 comment

  

**[The best 3d pie chart ever](http://www.maryandmatt.net/store/cpc.html)
**

![The best 3d pie chart ever](https://chandoo.org/wp/wp-content/uploads/2008/11/chocolate-pie-chart-yummy.jpg)

We all know that 3D pies are not a very good way to express your story. I guess this one is an exception we all are happy to make \[via [PTS Blog](http://peltiertech.com/WordPress/2008/11/21/3d-pie-charts/)\
\]

**[How to solve problems – Interactive visualization](http://www.idiagram.com/CP/vacuum.html)
**

![How to solve problems - Interactive visualization](https://chandoo.org/wp/wp-content/uploads/2008/11/how-to-solve-problems.jpg)

This interactive visualization shows how to solve problems in a methodical way. It has great graphics and explains complex models used in problem solving in an interesting way.

**[How deep in a sh\*t people are](http://www.ohshiit.com/)
**

![How deep in a sh*t people are](https://chandoo.org/wp/wp-content/uploads/2008/11/oh-shiiit-how-many-searches.jpg)

Ok, this is the kind of graph that makes you go “wtf?… Really”. Some one with few dollars and a sense of humor built a site just to show how many search results are there for each of the variations of the phrase “oh sh\*t”. The fun part is they created the logo of the site from the number of search results each “i” has. (although first few seem to be kind of normalized..)

**[Using wikipedia and google maps to find the city boundaries](http://wikimapia.org/#lat=19.1977019&lon=72.8578949&z=11&l=0&m=a&v=2)
**

![Using wikipedia and google maps to find the city boundaries](https://chandoo.org/wp/wp-content/uploads/2008/11/wikimapia-google-maps-mashed-up-with-wikipedia-articles.jpg)

Maps always fascinate me. Their ability to connect one information to another to weave a story is just great. This one showcased above outlines the city boundaries based on the wikipedia articles (I guess it uses the geo-tagging of the articles done by authors to find the best possible rectangle to highlight)

**[Instant panoramas of cities of the world](http://360cities.net/)
**

![Instant panoramas of cities of the world](https://chandoo.org/wp/wp-content/uploads/2008/11/260-cities-panoramas-of-world-cities.jpg)

This is another creative map application using which you can see 360° panoramas of the world cities. Just click on a point in the map (they don’t have panoramas for all cities yet) and go browsing. In a way it is similar to [Microsoft’s Photosynth](http://livelabs.com/photosynth/)
, but uses map interface and limited to user uploaded panoramas. \[via [Tech Crunch](http://www.techcrunch.com/2008/11/21/360-cities-brings-stunning-spherical-panoramas-to-google-earth/)\
\]
