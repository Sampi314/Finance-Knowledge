How does this template work?
----------------------------

This template feels magical. To begin with,

### Zebra lines thru Conditional Formatting

Alternative rows of the template are shaded in dull gray color so that the template is easy to use. And this is achieved by [Conditional Formatting](http://chandoo.org/wp/2008/03/13/want-to-be-an-excel-conditional-formatting-rock-star-read-this/)
 & [SUBTOTAL formula](http://chandoo.org/wp/2010/02/09/subtotal-formula-excel/)
. _**A very ingenious use of SUBTOTAL formula so that the zebra lines preserve even after filtering data.**_

_**![Zebra Lines using Conditional Formatting](https://img.chandoo.org/a/conditional-formatting-rule.png)  
**_

### Custom Cell Formatting to Show Budget vs. Actual Variance

Another simple yet elegant solution. To highlight the variance between budgeted & actual gift value, Steven used [Custom Cell Formatting](http://chandoo.org/wp/2009/01/29/colors-in-excel-chart-labels-trick/)
.

![Custom Cell Formatting rules to highlight budget variance](https://chandoo.org/img/a/custom-cell-formatting-rule.png)

### Awesome Formulas to Summarize the Gift List

The top right area of Steven’s shopping list shows a clear summary of your Christmas shopping list. Each of the values in the summary are calculated by a clever formula. For example, the formula to show how many gifts are over the budget vs. how many are _on or under_ the budget is an intricately woven [SUMPRODUCT formula](http://chandoo.org/wp/2009/11/10/excel-sumproduct-formula/)
 with SUBTOTAL, OFFSET & ROW components. Go ahead and examine these formulas to learn more.

### You can filter the list and analyze by segment

The beauty of this template is that you can filter the list and analyze by segment. For example, you can filter all the gifts you are giving to **_friends_** and see whether you are with-in budget in that segment, the progress of gift selection & purchase etc. _**Very useful.**_
