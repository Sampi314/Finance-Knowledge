The High Stakes of Logos in Client Presentations
------------------------------------------------

Logos aren’t decorations. They represent your clients, counterparties, and your firm’s ability to execute. Using the wrong one can have outsized consequences: 

*   Outdated logos make you look sloppy, suggesting you’re not current with the market 
*   Low-resolution or inconsistent logos erode the professional polish that clients expect 
*   Incorrect logos, say, the wrong subsidiary or old brand name, can damage credibility in front of the very people you’re trying to impress 

Standalone Logo Tools Are Problematic 
--------------------------------------

Managing logos is far from simple. A lot of firms use standalone or bundled logo platforms to help them deal with it. However, that is rarely the right strategy. Sure, they promise to put everything front and center. However, the truth is that they can mean: 

*   **Added cost** – You’re paying for a separate contract (often tied to much larger, more expensive platforms you don’t really need) 
*   **Extra complexity** – Managing integrations between PowerPoint and other tools slows everything down 
*   **Fragmentation** – Instead of one central hub for assets, your team is bouncing between systems just to finish a single deck 

It’s a lot of overhead for something as simple as putting the right logo in a presentation.
