# Change to your excel set up to make your more productive

**Source:** https://www.financialmodellinghandbook.org/setting-up-your-excel-environment

---

I'm sure you're keen to get into the real meat of model building. However, it's worth taking 20 seconds to change a couple of the default settings in Excel, which are not helpful for modellers.

1 Maximise screen real estate.
------------------------------

Control+F1 will hide the ribbon. You can get it back when you need it by hitting the Alt key then choosing the keystroke for the menu you want. Also, minimise the formula bar with Ctrl+Shift+u. We're going to keep our formulas short, and they should always fit into the smaller bar.

![](https://www.financialmodellinghandbook.org/content/images/public/images/4ebee82d-26a6-4f73-93ea-577214fa905c_1694x1039.jpeg)

Go from this - with the ribbon and extended formula pane taking up about 25% of my screen

![](https://www.financialmodellinghandbook.org/content/images/public/images/ed835698-ad3f-4939-b39d-cccd45a36e94_1694x1039.jpeg)

To this. Now they take up around 10%. I can get the ribbon back at any time using the Alt key.

2 Stop moving down a row after you hit enter.
---------------------------------------------

Go to File / Option / Advanced and untick "After pressing enter move down". This change may irritate you at first, only because it's what you're used to, and change is hard. Stick with it. You'll quickly realise that this is a crazy default, only useful if you're doing data entry in a vertical list. Usually, when you press enter in a cell, you've just written a formula. At this point, you want to do one of two things. Thing one. Examine the formula to check if it makes sense. Thing two. Copy the formula across a timeline. Neither of these things involved you being in the row below the formula.

![](https://www.financialmodellinghandbook.org/content/images/public/images/1a72fcd7-7c6b-4ead-ab7c-182dd651195f_1018x814.jpeg)

Hunt it down in the Excel options. And untick it in the name of all that’s good in the world.

3 Untick "Allow editing directly in cell".
------------------------------------------

This doesn't make as much of a difference as the first two. It's still worth mentioning. I like to always look at the formula bar when editing formulas. Simply because it's always in the same place. Remember the muscle memory discussion? I'm doing whatever I can to cut down the number of moving parts in my modelling.

That's it.

These three changes will make a difference to your Excel productivity.
