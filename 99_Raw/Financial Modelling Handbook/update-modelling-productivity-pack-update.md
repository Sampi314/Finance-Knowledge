# Update: Modelling productivity pack

**Source:** https://www.financialmodellinghandbook.org/update-modelling-productivity-pack-update

---

I think the finish line is in sight, although the list of placeholders on the [table of contents](https://www.financialmodellinghandbook.org/contents/)
 seems to grow faster than the new links I'm adding.

Here are the major additions since my last update:

Productivity pack macros
------------------------

We've been pushing the productivity pack macros forward, improving old functions and adding new ones.

I've added [this download page](https://www.financialmodellinghandbook.org/financial-modelling-productivity-pack/)
, more [structured release notes](https://www.financialmodellinghandbook.org/financial-modelling-productivity-pack-release-notes/)
 and [this complete list of macro functions](https://www.financialmodellinghandbook.org/complete-list-of-productivity-pack-macros/)
. This [list of the range names you need to have in your model](https://www.financialmodellinghandbook.org/range-names-needed-for-productivity-macros/)
 to support the macros may be helpful.

New section: Model maintenance and review
-----------------------------------------

I've started a new section around model maintenance and review. See Section 5 on the [table of contents](https://www.financialmodellinghandbook.org/contents/)
 for that.

New chapters added
------------------

*   [Using grouping to see the big picture](https://www.financialmodellinghandbook.org/using-grouping-to-see-the-big-picture/)
    
*   [Reviewing a model - structured commenting and feedback](https://www.financialmodellinghandbook.org/structured-commenting-and-feedback-for-financial-model-review/)

*   [Managing import/export marking exceptions](https://www.financialmodellinghandbook.org/managing-import-export-marking-exceptions/)
    
*   [How to manage excel file size](https://www.financialmodellinghandbook.org/how-to-reduce-excel-file-size/)
    
*   [How to check and reset the excel end cell.](https://www.financialmodellinghandbook.org/how-to-check-and-reset-the-used-range-in-your-model/)
    

As ever, thank you for your support and encouragement. Please keep the feedback and ideas coming; they are really helping me to shape this.
