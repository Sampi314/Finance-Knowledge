**What is the transaction reporting threshold for transfer pricing in Singapore?**
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------

In Singapore, the Inland Revenue Authority of Singapore (IRAS) has established specific thresholds for reporting related party transactions (RPT) and preparing [transfer pricing](https://www.maxlewis.com.sg/our-services/transfer-pricing/)
 documentation.

**Reporting Related Party Transactions:**

*   **Threshold:** If the total value of RPTs disclosed in your company’s financial statements exceeds **SGD 15 million** for the financial period, you are required to complete the Form for Reporting RPT.
*   **Calculation:** The value of RPTs is the sum of:
    1.  All amounts received/receivable from related parties and all amounts paid/payable to related parties as reported in the Income Statement, excluding compensation paid to key management personnel and dividends.
    2.  Year-end balances of loans and non-trade amounts due from/to all related parties.
*   **Procedure:** From the Year of Assessment (YA) 2020 onwards, this form is part of the Corporate Income Tax Return (Form C). If your RPTs exceed SGD 15 million, indicate ‘Yes’ in Item 31 of Form C and provide the necessary details in the Related Party Transactions tab.

**Transfer Pricing Documentation:**

*   **Mandatory Preparation:** You must prepare transfer pricing documentation if your gross revenue exceeds **SGD 10 million** for the basis period or if you were required to prepare such documentation for the immediately preceding basis period.
*   **Exemptions:** Certain related party transactions are exempt from documentation if they do not exceed specified thresholds.
    *   **For YA 2025 and Before:**
        *   **SGD 15 million** per financial year for categories like purchases or sales of goods and loans.
        *   **SGD 1 million** per financial year for other categories, such as service fees, royalties, and guarantees.
    *   **For YA 2026 and Beyond:**
        *   The threshold for categories other than purchases, sales, and loans will increase to **SGD 2 million** per financial year.

*   **Completion Timeline:** Documentation should be completed by the filing due date of the Income Tax Return.
*   **Retention Period:** Maintain the documentation for at least five years from the end of the basis period in which the transaction took place.

[Default](https://www.iras.gov.sg/taxes/corporate-income-tax/specific-topics/transfer-pricing?utm_source=chatgpt.com)

It’s important to note that even if certain transactions are exempt from detailed documentation, all related party transactions must adhere to the arm’s length principle. Non-compliance can lead to transfer pricing adjustments and a surcharge of 5% on the adjustment amount, regardless of whether additional tax is payable.

For the most current and detailed information, refer to the IRAS Transfer Pricing Guidelines.
