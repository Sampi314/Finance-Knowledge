**Determining the True Worth of Your Assets**
---------------------------------------------

Ever wonder what your business is really worth? Not just the number on paper, but the real-world value? That’s where proper [business valuation](https://www.maxlewis.com.sg/)
 comes in.

Most business owners have only a vague idea of their company’s value. They might know their revenue or even profit margins, but actual business worth involves much more. Your equipment, intellectual property, customer lists, and brand reputation all contribute to your valuation.

When you know your true worth, you stop making decisions in the dark. You’ll know exactly what assets are performing and which ones are just taking up space on your balance sheet.

**Identifying Strengths and Weaknesses in Your Business Model**
---------------------------------------------------------------

Business valuation acts like a microscope on your operations. The process reveals exactly where you’re knocking it out of the park and where you’re leaving money on the table.

Maybe your customer acquisition costs are eating too much profit. Or perhaps your intellectual property is worth way more than you realized.

Many owners discover their business model has hidden gems or concerning cracks during [valuation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Valuation_(finance))
. One client found their service division was actually worth three times what they thought, while their product line was barely breaking even.

**Setting Realistic Growth Targets Based on Current Value**
-----------------------------------------------------------

Growth targets without proper valuation? That’s like planning a road trip without knowing your starting point.

With solid valuation data, you can set growth objectives that actually make sense. If your business is currently worth $2 million, aiming for $4 million in three years might be ambitious but achievable. Without that baseline, you’re just guessing.

Smart business owners use valuation as their north star for growth planning. They know precisely how much value different initiatives need to add and can prioritize accordingly.
