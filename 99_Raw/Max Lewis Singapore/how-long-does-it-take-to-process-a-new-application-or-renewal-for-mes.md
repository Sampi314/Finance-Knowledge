One of the most frequently asked questions is how long it typically takes to process a new application or renewal for the [Major Exporter Scheme (MES](https://www.maxlewis.com.sg/applying-or-renewing-major-exporter-scheme/ "Applying Or Renewing Major Exporter Scheme")
), and how long the MES validity period granted by IRAS will last.

General Timeline
----------------

On average, the entire process, from start to finish, should take about one month. However, this timeframe could be extended if the review process is unsatisfactory or if the preparer encounters issues while completing the Assisted Self-help Kit (ASK) templates. In some cases, applicants may need to apply for administrative concessions to request a waiver of penalties for errors discovered during the ASK review process.

Common Issues During the Process
--------------------------------

A common challenge is the lack of proper understanding regarding the sampling methodology set by IRAS. Errors in this area can delay the application process. Additionally, problems with accurately filling out the ASK templates can also cause delays.

What Happens After Submission?
------------------------------

Assuming everything goes smoothly, IRAS typically takes about one to two weeks to review the submission. If the application is approved, new applicants can expect the MES to be granted for up to one year. For renewals, the validity period could range from three to five years, based on our experience.

In cases where IRAS issues a conditional approval, the MES validity period is usually limited to one year, and taxpayers may need to provide a banker’s guarantee. However, the banker’s guarantee requirement can be waived if the taxpayer subsequently demonstrates compliance with GST regulations.
