Import GST Deferment Scheme (IGDS)
==================================

by [MaxLewis](https://www.maxlewis.com.sg/author/maxlewis/ "Posts by MaxLewis")
 | Mar 9, 2025 | [Taxation & GST](https://www.maxlewis.com.sg/category/taxation/)

In Budget 2010, IGDS was introduced with the aim to help GST-registered businesses which suffer tighter cash flow as a result of importing goods.

Payment of import GST is being deferred from the time of importation to the filing of GST return. This allows importers a credit period of 1 to 2 months, similar to what businesses enjoy for their local purchases.

The IGDS scheme is targeted at importers that has been importing or will be importing goods with good compliance records with Singapore Customs and IRAS.

Import GST is collected by [Singapore Customs (SC)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Singapore_Customs)
 on all goods imported into Singapore, except where: Relief has been granted under the GST (Imports Relief) Order; or Importer is under an import GST suspension scheme (e.g. Major Exporter Scheme)

Business without any GST schemes will need to pay GST upon importation and can only claim back at the time when it files its GST return. So there will be a time lapse between payment of import GST and claiming it back as input tax. This affects business’ operating cashflow and put a strain on their working capital’s needs.

Scope of IGDS
-------------

· To import goods in the course or furtherance of your business

· To import goods belonging to an overseas principal for sale in Singapore or re-export in the capacity of a GST agent (i.e. Section 33(2) or Section 33(A) agent)

IGDS will apply to both dutiable and non-dutiable overseas goods under the following circumstances:

· Direct imports

· Imports released from Zero-GST/Licensed warehouses into local market

To apply for IGDS status, you are required to submit the following documents:

· A duly completed and signed application form GST F22

· Review Section 1 (GST Practices) and Section 2 (Pre-filing Checklist) of ASK

· A duly completed “ASK: Declaration Form on Completing Annual Review & Voluntary Disclosure of Errors” https://www.iras.gov.sg/taxes/goods-services-tax-(gst)/general-gst-schemes/import-gst-deferment-scheme-(igds)

In conclusion, the Import GST Deferment Scheme (IGDS) provides significant relief to businesses that face cash flow challenges due to the immediate payment of import GST. By deferring the payment of import GST until the filing of the GST return, businesses can ease the pressure on their working capital and ensure smoother operations. To apply for IGDS status, businesses must meet certain compliance requirements and submit the necessary documentation to the relevant authorities.
