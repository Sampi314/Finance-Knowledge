Setting up the initial ownership split between founders is one of the most important, but most challenging, startup decisions. Here’s a practical guide for structuring founder equity.

Tips for Setting Up Your Cap Table
----------------------------------

*   **Founders Should Always Take Common Stock**  
    Founders and management should be issued [common stock](https://kruzeconsulting.com/blog/common-stock/)
     rather than preferred. Preferred stock typically comes with [liquidation preferences](https://kruzeconsulting.com/blog/liquidation-preference/)
    , which give one founder a special payout if the company is sold. That can create misaligned incentives and make venture capital investors wary of the company’s structure.​
*   **Include a Vesting Period for Founder Shares**  
    A standard [vesting period](https://kruzeconsulting.com/blog/startup-accounting/eso/)
     (typically 4-5 years with a one-year cliff) helps assure investors that founders are committed for the long haul. If someone leaves early, unvested shares can be reallocated to attract new talent and keep the team strong. Vesting protects those who stick around and keeps the cap table aligned with the company’s future needs.​
*   **Have Honest Conversations About Ownership Splits**  
    Equal splits are rare and not always fair. If one founder contributed the original idea or will take on more responsibility, consider adjusting percentages accordingly. Have this discussion early to prevent resentment as the company grows and roles shift. Align equity with expected contributions and bring in your advisors, such as [angel investors](https://kruzeconsulting.com/blog/what-are-angel-investors/)
     or [CFO firms](https://kruzeconsulting.com/fractional-cfo-services/)
    , to help mediate if needed.​
*   **File Your 83b Election Early**  
    Founders should file an [83b election](https://kruzeconsulting.com/blog/when-file-83b-election/)
     with the IRS within 30 days of purchasing their stock. This sets a low cost basis, potentially saving significant tax dollars when the company grows. Send your filing by certified mail and keep the receipt for life! The consequences of missing this step can be costly and difficult to reverse.

Why Structure Matters for Founders and VCs
------------------------------------------

A thoughtful ownership structure reassures investors that founders are committed, fairly incentivized, and able to attract top talent. Investors review cap tables to check for founder dilution, [healthy option pools](https://kruzeconsulting.com/blog/how-model-option-pool/)
, and balanced share allocations. Lopsided founder arrangements can raise red flags unless clearly justified. All parties should feel content with the split and their future role.​

Once you’ve handled these complex founder discussions, lean on reliable [cap table](https://kruzeconsulting.com/blog/startup-cap-table-cpa/)
 management software to keep ownership records clear and accurate as your team grows. A solid cap table isn’t just about numbers; it sets a foundation for trust and teamwork through every stage of your startup’s growth.
