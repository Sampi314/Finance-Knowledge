Choosing the right [cap table](https://kruzeconsulting.com/blog/startup-cap-table-cpa/)
 software is essential for any startup aiming to accurately manage equity ownership, keep employees and founders motivated, and make sure your fundraising and compliance processes go smoothly. [Cap table management](https://kruzeconsulting.com/blog/reconcile-cap-table/)
 may not sound exciting, but it’s a core startup function you can’t afford to get wrong, so here’s how you can make the best choice for your team.​

Why Great Cap Table Software Matters
------------------------------------

Cap tables show who owns what. This isn’t just a “nice to have” tool! It protects everyone’s interests, supports efficient [fundraising](https://kruzeconsulting.com/blog/raise-series-a/)
, makes audits easier, and helps maintain trust with employees and investors. Startups typically start with a spreadsheet, but as ownership structures get complicated, professional software is a must to avoid costly errors and headaches down the road.​

What to Look for in Cap Table Software
--------------------------------------

*   **Will Your Lawyer Use It?**  
    Most startups get their law firm involved in initial cap table management, and nearly all [VC-backed startups](https://kruzeconsulting.com/)
     rely on their legal counsel as gatekeepers for any cap table platform. Always check with your law firm about compatibility, because they’ll likely have a preference.​
*   **Cost**  
    Early-stage startups are rightfully budget-conscious. Look for vendors with transparent, low-cost entry tiers. ​
*   **Ease of Use**  
    If you’re managing your own cap table or letting board members process grants, user experience matters. Mobile-friendly, intuitive platforms allow VCs and founders to sign off on grants from anywhere, which streamlines important decisions.​
*   **Support for Securities**  
    Modern startups issue more than just [common shares](https://kruzeconsulting.com/blog/common-stock/)
    . Make sure your platform reliably manages all your securities: preferred and restricted stock, warrants, [SAFEs](https://kruzeconsulting.com/blog/safe-notes/)
    , convertible notes, and stock options. Some tools may not support every instrument, so double-check this before you commit.​

Features You Probably Don’t Need
--------------------------------

*   **Scenario Modeling**  
    Most startups do not need advanced scenario modeling tools. While it might sound impressive, Kruze Consulting finds that less than 1% of clients require this feature regularly, so focus your budget and workflow on the basics instead.​
*   **Exit Modeling**  
    Fancy exit models are more fun for daydreaming than for real decision-making. If you sell your company, specialists (and your lawyers) will handle the [true calculations](https://kruzeconsulting.com/founder-selling-exit/)
     when it matters. The extra cost rarely brings real value to early-stage teams.
*   **409A Valuations**  
    Many cap table tools bundle [409A valuations](https://kruzeconsulting.com/409a-valuation/)
     (used to set fair market option prices) as a perk. While convenient, these are available separately, so don’t choose your platform based on the promise of a “free” 409A valuation.​

Choose Carefully
----------------

Your cap table isn’t just paperwork. It’s at the heart of your startup’s trust and success. Choose software that matches your company’s stage, fits your team and board’s habits, and delivers only the features you truly need. From cost-effective solutions for fresh startups to robust tools for scaling teams, there’s a fit for every founder – just make sure you check with your legal team and focus on usability, not bells and whistles.​ Visit our [partner page](https://kruzeconsulting.com/partners/)
