[Raising venture capital](https://kruzeconsulting.com/blog/startup-venture-capital-assistance/)
 is an exciting milestone for founders, but the real work starts once the funds hit the account. Securing your startup’s next phase of growth requires smart, immediate actions. Here are five essential steps for setting your company up for long-term success after your round closes.

1\. Set Up a Dedicated Startup Bank
-----------------------------------

First, put your newly raised capital in the right place by opening an account at a [bank that specializes in startups](https://kruzeconsulting.com/best-business-banks/)
. Banks that cater to startups are popular for their deep experience, excellent customer service, and tech-forward systems. Startup-focused banks offer services tailored to early-stage businesses – including streamlined wire transfers, venture-friendly account structures, and support for rapid scaling. Getting your banking relationship right up front makes day-to-day operations and investor requirements much easier.

2\. Ramp Up Recruiting (Fast)
-----------------------------

Recruiting strong talent is almost always slower than founders expect – so get started as soon as your funding lands. Every month you’re late on hiring, you risk falling behind on the milestones promised to your investors. Leveraging [specialized recruiting partners](https://kruzeconsulting.com/blog/how-recruiting-costs-change-as-a-startup-grows/)
 can accelerate the search for the right people and ensure you stay on track with your growth plan. Early and proactive hiring solidifies your team and paves the way for product delivery and market traction.

3\. Hire a Professional Accounting Firm
---------------------------------------

Elevate your finances with a dedicated accounting partner – ideally, one that focuses on venture-backed startups (like Kruze Consulting). Accountants handle everything from tax filings and monthly closes to transparent reporting for your stakeholders. Proper financial operations give you clarity about your runway and burn rate, as well as instill confidence in your investors. Most VCs require regular [GAAP financials](https://kruzeconsulting.com/do-vcs-and-angels-really-care-about-gaap-compliant-financials/)
 and accurate, timely updates, so getting expert help now pays long-term dividends.

4\. Prepare for Your First Board Meeting
----------------------------------------

Your first post-funding [board meeting](https://kruzeconsulting.com/blog/startup-board-meetings/)
 sets the tone with your new investors. Arrive prepared with a clear financial plan, spending breakdown, and well-defined milestones for the year. A [strong board package](https://kruzeconsulting.com/blog/startup-board-presentation-template/)
 builds trust, clarity, and a constructive relationship moving forward – and demonstrates that your leadership is disciplined and investor-focused. The right preparation can make subsequent board meetings smoother and more productive.

5\. Explore Venture Debt – Early
--------------------------------

With fresh VC investment on your cap table, it’s an ideal time to line up a [venture debt facility](https://kruzeconsulting.com/blog/insights-into-venture-debt/)
. Venture lenders prefer to partner with companies right after an equity round, when investor backing is strongest and risk is lower. Setting up venture debt now gives you future flexibility: funds aren’t drawn down all at once, but you’ll have access when growth or unexpected needs arise. This [added runway](https://kruzeconsulting.com/best-place-to-optimize-your-runway-period/)
 can support scaling, acquisitions, or bridge gaps between rounds.

Hit the Ground Running
----------------------

Executing these five steps immediately after a [fundraising round](https://kruzeconsulting.com/blog/raise-series-a/)
 ensures stability, accountability, and readiness to capitalize on new opportunities. Kruze Consulting specializes in guiding venture-backed startups through banking setup, accounting, board preparation, and venture debt strategy – so your company can focus on delivering results and scaling for long-term success.
