Top Tech & SaaS Conferences for Founders in 2025/2026
=====================================================

![](https://cdn.prod.website-files.com/665dda875a2115953cd470ad/6686ebbbbe4d0ce9e774755d_virtual%20conference.webp)

Choosing the right conference can feel like a big bet. Your time is your most valuable asset, and spending it on the wrong event is a costly mistake. You need more than just interesting talks; you need opportunities to connect with investors, meet future customers, and learn from founders who have already scaled.

To help you decide, we’ve curated a list of the top tech and SaaS conferences that deliver real value. We focused on events known for high-quality networking, fundraising opportunities, and practical growth strategies.

‍

**Which Conferences Are Global Tech Hubs?**
-------------------------------------------

These large-scale events bring together the entire tech ecosystem, offering broad exposure and diverse networking opportunities.

‍

### **CES (Consumer Electronics Show)**

*   **Next Event:** January 6–9, 2026
*   **Location:** Las Vegas, NV
*   **Best for:** Consumer tech, AI, and enterprise partnerships.
*   **Why it’s worth it:** CES is the global stage for innovation. While massive, it offers a dedicated startup area, Eureka Park, where you can connect with investors, partners, and media. If your product touches consumer electronics, AI, or vehicle tech, this is a critical event for visibility.[‍](https://www.ces.tech/)
    
*   [Learn more about CES](https://www.ces.tech/)
    

‍

### **Web Summit**

*   **Next Event:** Multiple dates
*   **Location:** Lisbon (Portugal), Rio (Brazil), Vancouver (Canada) and Doha (Qatar)
*   **Best for:** Global networking and fundraising.
*   **Why it’s worth it:** Web Summit is one of the world's largest and most influential tech events. It attracts founders, executives from Fortune 500 companies, and investors from every corner of the globe. Its scale makes it a powerful platform for launching a product or making key international connections.
*   ‍[Learn more about Web Summit](https://websummit.com/)

Founder FAQs
------------

‍

**1\. Is it better to attend a large conference or a niche one?**

It depends on your goal. Go to a large conference for broad exposure, discovering new trends, and making diverse connections. These are great for brand awareness and serendipitous meetings. Attend a niche conference for targeted problem-solving, connecting with peers who face the same challenges, and meeting investors who specialize in your sector. If you can, do one of each per year.

‍

**2\. How can I maximize the ROI of attending a tech conference?**

Don't just show up. Plan ahead. Define one clear goal: is it to meet 10 qualified investors, sign 3 pilot customers, or learn a specific growth tactic? Use the conference app to schedule meetings weeks in advance. During the event, prioritize those meetings over random sessions. Afterward, follow up within 48 hours with a personalized message referencing your conversation. The real value is unlocked in the preparation and follow-up.

‍

**3\. Are virtual tech conferences still valuable for networking?**

Yes, but they require a different approach. In-person events thrive on spontaneous chats, while virtual events reward deliberate action. To succeed, actively participate in Q&A sessions, join topic-specific virtual roundtables, and use the attendee list to send direct, personalized connection requests. The networking is less random but can be more efficient if you put in the effort. Many of the best virtual tech conferences for startups now have excellent matchmaking tools to help.
