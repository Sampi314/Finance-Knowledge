### Excel Links for you

Moving on, it has been a while since I shared some Excel links. So here we go.

**[Why we can’t divide by zero?](http://datapigtechnologies.com/blog/index.php/the-mathematical-reason-you-can-never-divide-by-zero/)
**

Ever wondered why you cannot divide numbers with zero. Mike at Data Pig technologies explains the mysteries of number crunching. Interesting read.

**[Creating a spreadsheet based wizard](http://optionexplicitvba.com/2013/10/17/creating-a-spreadsheet-based-wizard/)
**

Wizard interfaces are very useful when you want to capture data or depict a process. Jordan at Option Explicit VBA shows us how to build a simple wizard using Excel.

**[Add new items to data validation drop-down](http://blog.contextures.com/archives/2013/12/19/add-new-items-to-data-validation-combo-box/)
**

We all know that [using data validation, you can restrict the list of items allowed in a cell](http://chandoo.org/wp/2008/08/07/excel-add-drop-down-list/)
. But what if you want to give user a choice to add items to the list? Debra shows us a smart piece of VBA code that does this. Check it out.

**[Weight scale chart in Excel (fun & awesome)](http://chandoo.org/forum/threads/weight-scale-chart-funchart.14828/)
**

![Weight scale chart in Excel - made by Debraj @ Chandoo.org Forums](https://chandoo.org/forum/attachments/weight-scale-chart-png.3779/)  
_Debraj_ at our forum built this cool weight scale chart using Excel. Very smart, funny and interesting to learn from.

**[Get the difference of selected cells in status bar!](http://dailydoseofexcel.com/archives/2014/02/10/subtracting-cells-in-the-status-bar/)
**

You know that when you select a range of numbers, you can look at status bar to get quick statistics of those numbers – like sum, average, max and min. But what if you want to know the difference of selected numbers (whether 2 numbers or 2 sets of numbers)? Dick at DDoE shows us how to use VBA to enhance the behavior of Excel status bar to do this. Very creative!
