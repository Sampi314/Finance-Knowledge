**[Pop, Soda or Coke? – Countywise terms used for sweetened carbonated water](http://popvssoda.com:2998/countystats/total-county.html)
**

![pop-vs-soda-by-county-visual](https://chandoo.org/wp/wp-content/uploads/2008/08/pop-vs-soda-by-county-visual.jpg)

The pop-vs-soda map tells how marketers have been effective and creating a habit in people. The blue colors are for pop, yellow for soda and red for coke. As you can see, quite a few people call any soda as coke, especially in the southern regions of US. \[via [strangemaps](http://strangemaps.wordpress.com/2008/08/18/308-the-pop-vs-soda-map/)\
\]

[**Document Contrast Diagram – State of Union Address in 2007 and 2008**](http://www.neoformix.com/2008/DocumentContrastDiagrams.html)

![document-contrast-infographic-state-of-union-address-2007-2008](https://chandoo.org/wp/wp-content/uploads/2008/08/document-contrast-infographic-state-of-union-address-2007-2008.jpg)

Visualizing text is a difficult thing to do. More so when you have 2 texts and need to compare. Document contrast diagrams seem like an interesting way to explore two large pieces of text to see how they space out. The state of union address for 2007 and 2008 are shown above, 2007 is on left. \[via [Tim Showers](http://www.timshowers.com/2008/08/visualization-strategies-text-documents/)\
\]

[**How poetry looks?**](http://www.esono.com/boris/projects/poetry08/)

![poetry-visualization-ideas](https://chandoo.org/wp/wp-content/uploads/2008/08/poetry-visualization-ideas.jpg)

VisualPoetry project aims to take various poetic texts and connect them to show how words (sequences) appear on various poems. Very beautiful. \[via [information aesthetics](http://infosthetics.com/archives/2008/08/visual_poetry_2007_2008.html)\
\]

[**History of World Records in Olympics**](http://www.nytimes.com/interactive/2008/08/16/sports/olympics/20080816_world_records.html)

![olympic-world-records](https://chandoo.org/wp/wp-content/uploads/2008/08/olympic-world-records-mens-freestyle-swimming.jpg)

Another NY Times Olympic visualization. This shows how in each successive olympics the world records are created. A simple graphic, but the message is powerful: **You should never give up.**, also see the [Olympic medal visualization](http://chandoo.org/wp/2008/08/06/olympic-medal-country-year-excel-bubble-chart/)

[**What happens when graphs go physical**](http://www.designboom.com/weblog/cat/10/view/3519/joshua-callaghan.html)

![physical-graphs-to-increase-awareness](https://chandoo.org/wp/wp-content/uploads/2008/08/physical-graphs-to-increase-awareness.jpg)

This is an innovative idea by [Joshua Callaghan](http://www.designboom.com/)
. He took the graphs like military spending by country (shown on the right), world population since 0 AD, [consumer confidence](http://www.designboom.com/tools/WPro/images/08julyblogs03/josh9.jpg)
 and converted them to public art so that many more people can become aware of the situation. Very powerful and effective way to tell the story. \[via [flowing data](http://flowingdata.com/2008/08/20/physical-graphs-as-critique-on-american-culture/)\
\]
