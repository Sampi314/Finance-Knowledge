Time for another round of cool charting / info-graphic ideas around the web.

**[Most Popular Baby Names – all the way from 1880](http://nametrends.net/)
**

![baby names trend from 1880 - most popular names of all](https://i287.photobucket.com/albums/ll133/pointy-haired-dilbert/charts-of-week/july-31/baby-names-100-popular-from-1880.jpg)

Nametrends is a cool website totally dedicated to analyzing and showing baby name trends. They have pretty interesting stuff like, how baby names ending with -lie (charlie, willie, ellie etc.) fared from 1880. Go ahead, see the neat stuff at [nametrends](http://nametrends.net/)
. \[via [kottke](http://www.kottke.org/08/07/baby-name-trends)\
\]

**[Flare toolkit for visualizing in Flash](http://flare.prefuse.org/)
**

![Flare toolkit for visualizing data](https://i287.photobucket.com/albums/ll133/pointy-haired-dilbert/charts-of-week/july-31/falre-toolkit-for-visualization.jpg)

Flare is a powerful tool for visualizing large amounts of data using Action Script. Check it out. \[via [FlowingData](http://flowingdata.com/2008/07/31/new-version-of-flare-visualization-toolkit-released/)\
\]

**[Funny map of online communities](http://www.xkcd.com/256/)
**

![Funny map of online communities - XKCD](https://i287.photobucket.com/albums/ll133/pointy-haired-dilbert/charts-of-week/july-31/map-of-online-communities-xkcd.jpg)

This cartoonish visualization from XKCD pokes fun at the sizes and boundaries of various online communities. Really hilarious and neatly done. \[via [cool infographics](http://coolinfographics.blogspot.com/2008/07/map-of-online-communities.html)\
\]

**[Visualizing design process](http://www.thinkdrawmake.com/design-process-visualization-5-michael-ditullo/)
**

![Visualizing design process](https://i287.photobucket.com/albums/ll133/pointy-haired-dilbert/charts-of-week/july-31/visualizing-design-patterns.jpg)

This is a non-linear info graphic that talks about how designers approach problems. The site [\>think>draw>make](http://www.thinkdrawmake.com/)
 is a dedicated to visualizations of design processes. See it. \[via [cool infographics](http://coolinfographics.blogspot.com/2008/07/map-of-online-communities.html)\
\]

**[What info is hidden in a bar code?](http://www.barcode-plantage.com/)
**

![What is in a barcode?](https://i287.photobucket.com/albums/ll133/pointy-haired-dilbert/charts-of-week/july-31/what-info-is-hidden-in-barcodes-coo.jpg)

This pretty visualization of bar codes uses information contained in the black stripes to generate Bezier curves (ahem!) that look like a tree, hence the name bar-code plantation 🙂 \[via [swissmiss](http://swissmiss.typepad.com/weblog/2008/07/barcode-plantag.html)\
\]
