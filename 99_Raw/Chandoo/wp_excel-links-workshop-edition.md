Let us start this week with some recommended Excel related links.

**[Excel is 25 years old – cool video](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MAAbxBsxhC8)
**

Microsoft made this code video showcasing how Excel’s 25 years has been. See it below or [watch it on youtube](http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=MAAbxBsxhC8)
.  

\[Related: [Brief History of Microsoft Excel in a time-line chart](http://chandoo.org/wp/2010/01/13/history-of-excel-timeline/)\
\]

**[Jumping to Last Real Cell](http://excel.tips.net/Pages/T003297_Jumping_to_the_Real_Last_Cell.html)
**

Lets say you have a large workbook and want to jump to last cell with data. You can ofcourse press CTRL+End key to do that. But this approach may fail if you have a lot of blank cells at the end. You can of course [find and remove blank cells](http://chandoo.org/wp/2010/02/23/find-and-remove-blank-cells/)
 using menu options. But if you want a macro based approach, read the linked tip. It can be quite handy.

**[Introduction to Custom Functions in Excel](http://excelunusual.com/archive/2011/01/writing-custom-excel-functions-in-vba/)
**

George’s [Excel Unsual blog](http://excelunusual.com/)
 is quite extrodinary. He regularly posts lots of advanced excel models & workbooks. In this short article, he shows how Excel custom functions (UDFs) work and how to write one.

**[Excel REPT Formula – 30 days 30 formulas](http://blog.contextures.com/archives/2011/01/16/30-excel-functions-in-30-days-15-rept/)
**

Debra has been doing an excellent series called “[30 days 30 formulas](http://blog.contextures.com/archives/category/30xl30d/)
“. She has already explained 15 formulas thru this series. In this article, she shows how to REPT formula. If you read any articles on [incell charting](http://chandoo.org/wp/tag/in-cell-charting/)
, you will realize that understanding and using REPT formula is critical to develop good incell charts.

\[Related: [Learn 51 Excel Formulas](http://chandoo.org/excel-formulas/)\
\]

**[Understand How Excel 2010 Slicers Work](http://www.jkp-ads.com/articles/slicers00.asp)
**

[Slicers](http://chandoo.org/wp/tag/slicers/)
 is a cool new feature in Excel 2010 that makes data analysis by filtering quite a breeze. In this exhaustive post, Jan Karel explains how to slicers and how to customize them (including changing slicers’ font, which is not possible thru ribbon)

\[Related: [Dynamic Dashboard using Pivot Tables & Slicers](http://chandoo.org/wp/2010/12/08/dynamic-dashboard-video-tutorial/)\
\]
