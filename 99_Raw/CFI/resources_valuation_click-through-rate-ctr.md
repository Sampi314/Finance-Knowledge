Click-Through Rate (CTR)
========================

The ratio of the number of users who click a specific link on a website or email to the number of users who viewed that link

Written by [CFI Team](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/about-cfi/)

Published November 6, 2019

Read Time 3 minutes

What is Click-Through Rate (CTR)?
---------------------------------

Click-through rate (CTR) refers to the ratio of the number of users exposed to a specific link on a website page or in an email who click the link and view the advertised product or service. CTR is primarily used by digital and online marketers to quantify the success and effectiveness of an email or [advertising campaign](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/resources/management/aida-model-marketing/)
.

### Interpreting Click-Through Rates

The growing popularity of the internet around the world is forcing companies to pay close attention to the success of their online marketing. Nowadays, many companies rely on online marketing campaigns, and the click-through rate is an effective tool to assess them.

A high CTR is generally a positive indicator of people being attracted by a business’ advertising. On the other hand, a low CTR reveals some problems with the design of a marketing campaign.

No defined standard CTR exists for a successful campaign because the rates can significantly vary depending on the circumstances and situations. We can say that, on average, a 2% CTR is generally considered as a highly successful rate.

### Importance of Click-Through Rate

For digital and online marketers, click-through rates quantify the success of online advertising or email campaigns. Essentially, CTR quantifies the reaction of users to marketing or advertising campaigns. The metric is frequently employed in evaluating which ads and [keywords](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/resources/valuation/pay-per-click-ppc/)
 are more successful than others.

If your CTR is low, it may indicate that you are targeting the wrong audience or spending money on irrelevant keywords. Additionally, the metric can help evaluate the success of different [marketing channels](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/resources/management/market-segmentation-and-targeting/)
 (e.g., website 1 delivers 1% CTR, and website 2 returns 3.5% CTR).

The click-through rate metric comes with one critical drawback. Although the click-through rate assesses how well users react to marketing or advertising campaign, it does not translate such success into monetary value. In other words, it is difficult to determine how specific marketing activities influence sales and revenue by only looking at the click-through rate.

Therefore, this metric is commonly used in combination with the conversion rate, which indicates the percentage of clicks that result in actual sales. Note that for email campaigns, the click-through rate is considered in combination with other metrics such as [open rate](https://mailchimp.com/help/about-open-and-click-rates/)
 and bounce rate.
