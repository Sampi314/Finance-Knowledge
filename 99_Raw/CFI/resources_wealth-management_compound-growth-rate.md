Compound Growth Rate
====================

A measure used to determine the growth rate over multiple time periods

Written by [CFI Team](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/about-cfi/)

Read Time 2 minutes

What is the Compound Growth Rate?
---------------------------------

The compound growth rate is a measure used specifically in business and investing contexts, that indicates the growth rate over multiple time periods. It is a measure of the constant growth of a data series. The biggest advantage of the compound growth rate is that the metric takes into consideration the compounding effect. Thus, it is especially significant in the assessment of [returns from investments](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/resources/accounting/what-is-return-on-investment-roi/)
.

![Compound Growth Rate](https://cdn.corporatefinanceinstitute.com/assets/compoubd-growth-rate.jpeg)

Unlike average growth rates that are prone to volatility levels, compound growth rates are not affected by [volatility](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/resources/career-map/sell-side/capital-markets/volatility-vol/)
. Therefore, they are more relevant in the comparison of different data series.

### Understanding the Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR)

The compound annual growth rate (CAGR) is one of the most frequently used metrics in financial analysis and financial modeling. In financial models, the CAGR is calculated for important operational metrics such as [EBITDA](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/resources/valuation/what-is-ebitda/)
, and also for capital expenditures (capex) and [revenue](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/resources/accounting/revenue/)
. Also, the CAGR can be used for the forecasting of future growth rates.

However, one should be careful in using the compound growth rate in financial analysis. The metric smooths the historical data, omits the effect of volatility, and implies the steady growth of the [data series](https://www.ibm.com/docs/en/SSRL5J_1.0.1/com.ibm.swg.ba.cognos.ug_cr_rptstd.10.1.1.doc/c_chart_data_series.html)
. Due to those reasons, the compound growth rate should best be used together with some other metrics, and the context of the given situation must always be considered.
