Dividend Growth Rate
====================

The percentage growth rate of a company’s dividend achieved during a certain period of time

Written by [CFI Team](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/about-cfi/)

Published February 20, 2020

Read Time 4 minutes

What is the Dividend Growth Rate?
---------------------------------

The dividend growth rate (DGR) is the percentage growth rate of a company’s [dividend](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/resources/accounting/dividend/)
 achieved during a certain period of time. Frequently, the DGR is calculated on an annual basis. However, if necessary, it can also be calculated on a quarterly or monthly basis.

![Dividend Growth Rate - Image of dividends increase](https://cdn.corporatefinanceinstitute.com/assets/dividend-growth-rate8.jpeg)

The dividend growth rate is an important metric, particularly in determining a company’s long-term profitability. Since dividends are distributed from the company’s [earnings](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/resources/accounting/retained-earnings-guide/)
, one can assess and analyze its ability to sustain its profitability by comparing the DGR over time.

### Dividend Growth Rate and a Security’s Pricing

Also, the dividend growth rate can be used in a security’s pricing. It is an essential variable in the Dividend Discount Model (DDM).

The dividend discount model is based on the idea that the company’s current stock price is equal to the [net present value](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/resources/valuation/net-present-value-npv/)
 of the company’s future dividends. Mathematically, the dividend discount model is written using the following equation:

*   **g** – the dividend growth rate
