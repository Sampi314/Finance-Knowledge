Visitor-to-Lead Conversion Rate
===============================

The proportion of visitors to a website that was converted into leads in a given period

Written by [CFI Team](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/about-cfi/)

Published November 5, 2019

Read Time 3 minutes

What is Visitor-to-Lead Conversion Rate?
----------------------------------------

Visitor-to-lead conversion rate (also known as traffic conversion rate) is a metric that indicates the proportion of visitors to a website that is converted into leads in a given period. Recall that a lead is a prospective customer who already expressed their interest in purchasing a [product or service](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/resources/management/products-and-services/)
.

![Visitor-to-Lead Conversion Rate](https://cdn.corporatefinanceinstitute.com/assets/visitor-to-lead-conversion-rate1.jpeg)

### Understanding Visitor-to-Lead Conversion Rate

Visitor-to-lead conversion rate is one of many variations of customer conversion metrics. The metric is specific to businesses with a strong online presence. It essentially reveals how well a website generates interest in a product or service among its visitors.

Undoubtedly, conversion rates play a crucial role in measuring the success and growth of a company. For example, visitor-to-lead conversion rate primarily evaluates the effectiveness of a company’s marketing team and its efforts to create appealing online content that urges visitors to become potential clients.

At the same time, the visitor-to-lead conversion rate cannot be used to assess [sales funnels](https://www.forbes.com/sites/ashleystahl/2017/09/20/what-is-a-sales-funnel-and-how-to-create-your-first-one/)
 or sales teams. The reason is that the metric does not reveal the conversion of a website’s visitors into customers. For such purposes, you can use other conversion metrics such as [lead-to-customer conversion rate](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/resources/valuation/lead-to-customer-conversion-rate/)
.

### How to Calculate Visitor-to-Lead Conversion Rate?

Visitor-to-lead conversion rate is calculated by dividing the number of a website’s visitors who were converted into leads by the total number of visitors. Mathematically, it can be expressed using the following formula:

Note that the calculation of the metric requires the precise definition of concepts such as lead, visitor, and conversion. One can calculate an insightful visitor-to-lead conversion rate only after properly defining the relevant terms.

### Interpretation and Strategies

Generally, there are no defined benchmarks for visitor-to-lead conversion rates. This is because conversion rates are generally tied directly to a company’s goals and strategies. Thus, each company must assess its lead conversion rates based on its objectives and growth perspectives. It is still worth mentioning that, for many companies, the lead conversion rates are generally extremely low.

Despite the lack of standard benchmarks, each company aims to maximize its conversion rates. The maximization of the lead conversion rate is directly related to content creation. A company must ensure that its website provides valuable information to its visitors and that the information can successfully present its product or service to the website’s visitors.

In addition, the website content must be relevant to the industry in which a company operates. This ensures that the company targets the right [demographic](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/resources/economics/demographics/)
. Thus, the use of the right keywords is critical. Many companies also rely on search engine optimization to make their websites more visible to those people who would be interested in their product or service.
