Callable Certificate of Deposit
===============================

An FDIC-insured time deposit with a bank or other financial institutions

What is a Callable Certificate of Deposit?
------------------------------------------

A Callable Certificate of Deposit is an [FDIC](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/resources/wealth-management/what-is-fdic/)
\-insured time deposit with a bank or other financial institutions. Callable CDs can be redeemed by the issuer before their actual maturity date, within a specified time frame and call price.

Like other regular CDs, a callable CD pays a fixed [interest rate](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/resources/commercial-lending/interest-rate/)
 over its lifetime. The interest rate is often higher than that of regular CDs. However, the issuers of callable CDs own a [call option](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/resources/derivatives/call-option/)
 on the CD and can redeem the deposit from you before the maturity date.

![Callable Certificate of Deposit](https://cdn.corporatefinanceinstitute.com/assets/callable-certificate-of-deposit.jpeg)

The issuing bank adds a call feature to the time deposit so that it does not need to continue paying higher interest rates to its holders even after a fall in interest rates. But for the issuers to redeem the CDs, they must offer a premium price to the holder as an incentive for taking the risk.

When the CD is called, the holder gets the principal amount back plus [accrued interest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Accrued_interest)
 on their investment. CDs do not come with an initial non-callable period when they cannot be redeemed. The bank can redeem it as early as six months after you purchase it and every six months thereafter.

### Important Terms Related to Callable CDs        

Here are some of the terms that are commonly used in the definition of callable certificate of deposit:

#### Callable date

The callable date is the date on which the issuer can call your certificate of deposit. The callable date may be as early as six months and can sometimes go up to two years. For example, if the callable date is six months, it means that the issuing bank may decide whether to call back the CD in six months and return the amount invested plus interest. Then, every six months after the callable date, the bank has the same option again.

#### Maturity Date

The term “maturity date” varies from the term “callable date.” The maturity date refers to the duration of time that the issuing bank can keep your money. In essence, the longer the maturity date is in the future, the higher the interest rate that its holders will earn.

For example, an investor may buy a two-year callable certificate of deposit that has a maturity of 10 years. It means that an investor has two years before the issuer redeems the CD. However, the actual amount of time that you must commit your money to the investment is 10 years. The maturity date can go even higher, to 15 years and 20 years, depending on the issuer.

### How Callable Certificates of Deposit Work

Unlike the regular certificate of deposits, a callable CD gives the issuing bank or financial institution more control over the investor’s money. Investors buy the callable CDs for a fixed duration of time in the future and earn a specified interest rate. However, the issuer owns the right to redeem the callable CDs before the stated maturity date.

If the investor decides to redeem the CD before the maturity date, the issuer will charge withdrawal penalties. The date of maturity and the callable date are usually specified in advance before the investors commit their money.

### Example: Callable CD

For example, let’s consider a callable certificate of deposit issued by Company ABC in 2010. It pays an interest rate of 8%, with a maturity date of 2020, and is callable in 2015 at 105% of the face value.

t means that Company ABC has the right to call the CD in 2015, usually if interest rates fall below the stated 8%. For the issuers to call the CD, they must pay the holder/investor an amount that is a little higher than the face value, i.e., 105% of the face value.

The difference between the amount paid and the face value is the call premium. If ABC postpones the call to 2018, it will pay a lower amount, like 103%. This is because the amount decreases as the CD approaches the stated maturity date.

### Advantages of Callable Certificate of Deposit

#### 1\. It pays a higher interest than regular CDs

The issuers of callable CDs offer a higher rate of return to investors to encourage them to invest their money in the instruments. The rate of return may be 0.5% or 1% higher than that of regular CDs. Although the difference may seem small, it results in higher margins for investors who invest millions of dollars.

#### 2\. Investors receive their full principal

In the stock market, investors may lose their principal amount if the market goes against them. But with callable CDs, investors receive all the principal amount that they invested with the bank when the latter calls the CDs before the maturity date. They also earn a premium on the principal amount when the issuer redeems the CDs on the callable date.

#### 3\. It pays a higher interest rate for a fixed period

Investors who have purchased callable CDs continue to receive higher interest rates even when the market interest rates fall below the issuer’s initial interest rate. If the market interest rates are significantly lower, the issuer may call the CDs.

### Disadvantages of Callable Certificate of Deposit

#### 1\. Investors will lose future interest gains

Although investors are paid interest on the amount invested, they stand to lose interest gains in the future since the CD goes for a fixed period. For example, if the issuer gives an interest rate of 7% and the market interest rates rise to 9%, the investors will not benefit from the increase.

#### 2\. Early withdrawals incur penalties

Sometimes, investors may need to withdraw their money before the maturity date to cater to emergencies. However, doing so incurs large withdrawal penalties that may sometimes go up to 25%. Making early withdrawals results in a loss of a large amount of interest.
