Property, Plant, and Equipment (PP&E) is a non-current, tangible capital asset on the [balance sheet](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/resources/accounting/balance-sheet/)
 of a business used to generate revenues and profits.  PP&E plays a key part in the [financial planning and analysis](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/resources/career-map/corporates/corp-fpa/)
 of a company’s operations and future expenditures, especially with regard to [capital expenditures](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/resources/financial-modeling/how-to-calculate-capex-formula/)
.

The PP&E account is often denoted as the net of accumulated depreciation. This means that, if a company does not purchase new equipment (capital expenditures is zero), net PP&E should slowly decrease in value every period due to [depreciation](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/resources/knowledge/accounting/what-is-depreciation-expense/)
. This can be better determined by a depreciation schedule.

The formula for calculating net PP&E is:

**Net PP&E = Gross PP&E + Capital Expenditures  – Accumulated Depreciation**
