Yield
=====

Income-only return on investment expressed as an annual percentage

What is Yield (Definition)?
---------------------------

Yield is defined as an income-only return on investment (it excludes [capital gains](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/resources/wealth-management/capital-gains-yield-cgy/)
) calculated by taking [dividends](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/resources/accounting/dividend/)
, coupons, or [net income](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/resources/accounting/what-is-net-income/)
 and dividing them by the value of the investment, expressed as an annual percentage.

Yield tells investors how much income they will earn each year relative to the market value or initial cost of their investment. The average yield of stocks on the [S&P 500](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/resources/fixed-income/sp-standard-poors/)
, for example, typically ranges between 2.0 – 4.0%.

![Yield Formula and Diagram](https://cdn.corporatefinanceinstitute.com/assets/yield.png)

### Percent Yield Formula

The percent yield formula is a way of calculating the annual income-only [return on an investment](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/resources/accounting/what-is-return-on-investment-roi/)
 by placing income in the numerator and cost (or market value) in the denominator.

**Percentage yield formula:**

\= Dividends per Share / Stock Price x 100

\= Coupon / Bond Price x 100

\= Net Rental Income / Real Estate Value x 100 (also called “[Cap Rate](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/resources/valuation/capitalization-cap-rate/)
“)

### Dividend Yield (Stocks)

When it comes to stocks, the income [stockholders](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/resources/accounting/stakeholder-vs-shareholder/)
 receive at an individual level is in the form of dividends. Dividend frequency varies but is typically quarterly (sometimes monthly, semi-annually, or annually).

Let’s look at an example of the percent yield formula in action for a dividend-paying stock. We can easily calculate it by working through an everyday example.

**Example:**

Sam is focused on capital growth but also wants to receive some income from his stocks. As a result, he decides to invest in shares of Goldman Sachs (NYSE:GS), which has a current share price of $231.69 and a quarterly dividend of $0.80 per share. What is the current dividend yield?

Answer:

$0.80 x 4 = $3.20 annual dividend

$3.20 / $231.69 = 0.00138

0.00138 x 100 = 0.138%

### Interest Yield (Bonds)

For [bond investors](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/resources/fixed-income/bonds/)
, the income they receive is in the form of [coupon](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/resources/fixed-income/coupon-rate/)
 payments, which can vary in frequency but are typically semi-annual.

**Example:**

Sara is planning for retirement and wants to focus on earning some fixed income payments to fund her lifestyle. She plans to purchase some First Data corporate bonds that have a coupon of 5.75% and can be purchased at a price of $105.21. What is the bond’s yield?

Answer:

5.75% x 100 = $5.75 per bond per year

$5.75 / 105.21 = 0.0547

0.0547 x 100 = 5.47%

### Rental Income Yield (Real Estate)

Another common example is in [real estate](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/resources/commercial-real-estate/real-estate/)
 when investors wish to know how much of a percentage return they will earn in rental income they will receive from a property, after taking into account all operating expenses. The is referred to as the [Capitalization Rate](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/resources/valuation/capitalization-cap-rate/)
 in real estate.

**Example:**

Linda is interested in buying an apartment and renting it out to earn some extra income. She can buy a two bedroom apartment unit for $875,000 and it will rent out for $2,700 per month. She will have total monthly costs (taxes, strata fees, etc.) of $975. What is the yield on her real estate investment?

Answer:

$2,700 – $975 = $1,725 net monthly income

$1,725 x 12 = $20,700 net annual income

$20,700 / $875,000 = 0.0237

0.0237 x 100 = 2.37%

.
