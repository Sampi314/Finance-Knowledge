A cash budget is a budget based on actual inflows and outflows of cash, as opposed to being based on accounting principles such as [revenue recognition](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/resources/accounting/revenue-recognition-principle/)
, matching, and accruals, which may result in very different numbers. A cash budget is important for personal and [business planning](https://corporatefinanceinstitute.com/resources/financial-modeling/what-is-financial-modeling/)
, as it indicates how much funding is required or how much surplus cash is produced over a period of time.

### Example of a Cash Budget Template

Below is an example of a generic monthly cash budget template in Excel that you can download and use for your own purposes.

The template includes several sections:

*   Cash in from the sale of goods/services
*   Cash out for expenses
*   Cash in/out for investing
*   Cash in/out from financing
*   Total change in cash in each period
