VRC’s recent media briefing on current market, deal, and valuation trends is featured in [_BVWire, a Business Valuation Resources (BVR)_ publication](https://www.bvresources.com/articles/bvwire/vrc-offers-an-update-on-deals-and-fund-valuations)
. The article highlights insights from VRC’s senior professionals on current market trends, valuation dynamics, and the outlook for deal activity and fund valuation practices.

During the media briefing held in late January 2026, senior VRC professionals shared their perspectives on evolving conditions in private and public markets, including activity in mergers & acquisitions, private equity, and private credit. Participants discussed a range of developments influencing valuation practices, including expectations for increased transaction momentum in 2026 and the continued growth and complexity of private credit and secondary transactions.

VRC leaders including [PJ Patel](https://www.valuationresearch.com/professionals/pj-patel/)
, [John Czapla](https://www.valuationresearch.com/professionals/john-czapla/)
, [Adam Smith](https://www.valuationresearch.com/professionals/adam-smith/)
, [Charles Sapnas](https://www.valuationresearch.com/professionals/charles-sapnas/)
, [Parag Patel](https://www.valuationresearch.com/professionals/parag-patel/)
, and [Chad Rucker](https://www.valuationresearch.com/professionals/chad-rucker/)
 also highlighted several market dynamics impacting valuation professionals and dealmakers, such as the expanding need for third-party valuations and fairness opinions in transactional contexts and the heightened demand for transparency driven by new retail-focused fund structures. Despite the growing role of technology, panelists emphasized that human judgment and rigorous methodology remain core to delivering defensible valuation conclusions.

With a continued focus on transparency, rigorous methodology, and forward-looking insights, VRC remains committed to helping clients and other financial and tax reporting stakeholders navigate complex valuation challenges. The coverage in BVR’s publication reflects VRC’s continued engagement with industry developments and its role in advancing discussion on valuation best practices — especially in an environment where both sponsors and investors seek greater clarity amid shifting conditions.
