On December 22, 2017, the Tax Cuts and Jobs Act (Tax Act) became law, resulting in significant changes to the treatment of corporate taxes, as well as [gift and estate tax](https://www.valuationresearch.com/services/tax-compliance-planning/exit-succession-planning/gift-estate/)
 provisions. Interpretation of how these changes will impact valuations is evolving. Download the Valuation Q&A with VRC’s [Chris Mellen](https://www.valuationresearch.com/professionals/chris-mellen/)
 addressing three general components:

1.  Changes impacting [gift and estate tax](https://www.valuationresearch.com/services/tax-compliance-planning/exit-succession-planning/gift-estate/)
     provisions
2.  The impact of changes to [corporate taxes](https://www.valuationresearch.com/services/tax-compliance-planning/)
     on company value
3.  A go-forward approach to valuations

_Please note, this article has been prepared for general information purposes, does not address the complexities of specific provisions, and does not constitute valuation, legal, accounting, tax, or financial consulting advice. Those interested in specific legal or accounting guidance should seek competent professional advice. Inquiries to discuss specific valuation matters are welcome._
