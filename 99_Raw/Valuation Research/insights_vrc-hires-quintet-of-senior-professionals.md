**New York, NY (June 19, 2025)** – Valuation Research Corporation (VRC), a leading global provider of independent valuation support and advisory services, today announced the hire of five senior level professionals. Chirag Goel, Andrew Hornback, Thomas Morley, Robin Muhmel, and Joseph Sisson will join VRC offices across the country. The senior hires bring years of experience in the valuation industry, data analytics, and technology, including previous roles with Big Four firms.

Goel joins VRC’s corporate team as chief technology officer and brings a decade of IT leadership experience from a major Wisconsin-based insurance company. His deep expertise in cloud-based initiatives and systems development will serve to support the many technology initiatives currently underway across the firm. He earned his Bachelor of Technology degree from Kurukshetra University, his Master of Science degree from Illinois Institute of Technology, and he earned his MBA from the University of Wisconsin–Whitewater.

Hornback joins VRC’s Boston office as senior vice president, financial technology. Prior to VRC, he served as manager, deals at PricewaterhouseCoopers. Previously, he was a senior associate at PwC focused on business valuations and modeling, and deals technology and innovation. He earned his Bachelor of Science in Business Administration (BSBA) from Indiana University East, and his Master of Business Administration from Vanderbilt University. He also earned his PhD in computer science from the Georgia Institute of Technology.

Morley joins VRC’s Boston office as vice president, financial technology. Prior to joining VRC, he was a senior manager supporting the business insights and technology team at Bristol Myers Squibb with a focus on data science and analytics. Morley earned his Bachelor of Science in Biotechnology from Rutgers University and his Master of Business Administration from Rider University.

Muhmel joins VRC’s Dallas office as vice president, machinery & equipment valuation. Prior to VRC, she served as a tangible asset specialist at KPMG where she performed valuation analyses for financial and tax reporting, management planning, and litigation. She has an Accredited Senior Appraiser Machinery & Technical Specialties Designation and earned her Bachelor’s degree from Louisiana State University.

Sisson joins VRC’s portfolio valuation group specializing in valuations of privately held debt securities, equity securities, and business enterprises. He is a subject matter expert in real estate. Prior to VRC, he was a director on the Global Real Estate team at Chatham Financial, a director at Duff & Phelps, and an associate director at Integra Realty Resources. He holds the Member of the Appraisal Institute (MAI) designation. Sisson earned his Bachelor’s degree from Pennsylvania State University.

“These professionals are outstanding additions to our teams across the U.S.,” said PJ Patel, co-CEO of VRC. “It’s exciting to expand the VRC team with individuals who bring deep expertise in valuation, data analytics, and IT.”

#### About VRC

Valuation Research Corporation is a full-service valuations partner and strategic consultant to private market investment funds, financial sponsors and their portfolio companies, and acquisitive global public and private companies. As one of the largest and best-resourced firms focused exclusively on valuation, VRC’s network of nearly 1,500 valuation professionals, which includes VRC’s 300+ U.S.-based colleagues, has provided objective, supportable conclusions of value to domestic and international clients since 1975. VRC has locations in Atlanta, Boston, Chicago, Cincinnati, Dallas, Los Angeles, Milwaukee, New York, Princeton, San Francisco, and Tampa; as well as international member firms operating as VRG in Argentina, Australia, Brazil, Canada, China, Colombia, Germany, India, Israel, Japan, Luxembourg, Mexico, Singapore, Spain, and the United Kingdom.
