**New York, NY (November 5, 2025)** – Valuation Research Corporation (VRC), a leading global provider of independent valuation support and advisory services, today announced that Senior Managing Director John Czapla, Managing Director Ryan MacLean, and Co-CEO PJ Patel will participate in panels at the PAC 2025 Annual Conference on Professional Accounting Futures.

The conference is a component of PAC’s mission of stimulating research and discussion on issues facing professional accounting. It will bring together accounting researchers, standards setters, regulators, and professional accountants with a view to advancing professional accounting service to society.

Ryan MacLean will present on the panel, _“Retailization of Private Markets,”_ which will be moderated by John Czapla. PJ Patel will moderate the panel, _“Financial Reporting Implications of the Rise of Private Capital.”_

The panels will run from 11:00–11:45 am EST and 3:15–4:00 pm EST, respectively, on Friday, November 7.

More information about the conference can be found [here](https://www.utm.utoronto.ca/pac/events-conferences/pac-annual-conferences-professional-accounting-futures/pac-2025-annual)
.

#### About VRC

Valuation Research Corporation is a full-service valuations partner and strategic consultant to private market investment funds, financial sponsors and their portfolio companies, and acquisitive global public and private companies. As one of the largest and best-resourced firms focused exclusively on valuation, VRC’s network of nearly 1,500 valuation professionals, which includes VRC’s 300+ U.S.-based colleagues, has provided objective, supportable conclusions of value to domestic and international clients since 1975. VRC has locations in Atlanta, Boston, Chicago, Cincinnati, Dallas, Los Angeles, Milwaukee, New York, Princeton, San Francisco, and Tampa; as well as international member firms operating as VRG in Argentina, Australia, Brazil, Canada, China, Colombia, Germany, India, Israel, Japan, Luxembourg, Mexico, Singapore, Spain, and the United Kingdom.
