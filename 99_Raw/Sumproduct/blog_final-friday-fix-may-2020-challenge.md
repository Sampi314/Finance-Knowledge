Final Friday Fix: May 2020 Challenge
====================================

29 May 2020

_On the final Friday of each month, we’re going to set an Excel / Power BI challenge for you to puzzle over so that you can get your “Excel fix”. Challenge your office colleagues to see who can solve the puzzle quickest. There are no prizes at this stage: you’re playing for bragging rights only!_

This month’s challenge revisits two previous ideas, [multiple number formatting using conditional formatting](https://www.sumproduct.com/thought/multiple-number-formatting)
 and [conditionally formatting chart axes](https://www.sumproduct.com/blog/article/monday-morning-mulling-december-2016)
. You have probably guessed, if we are happy to give you the links to similar problems, then the solution to this month’s challenge might be a little different…

**_The Challenge_**

To make things easier for you, let’s provide you with an [Excel starter file](https://sumproduct.com/assets/blog-pictures/2020/fff-mmm/may/sp-formatted-chart-data-labels-starter.xlsx)
 with pre-formatted chart data (all of the following inputs are numbers typed in, not text):

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/e774d10cbbb9450fc45efbe51abdf434-210-1.jpg)

All you have to do is create the associated Excel chart with replicated label formatting:

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/f32e5a15e2cf9c3e4d2d058458ce054d-202-1.jpg)

This chart has to work after you have closed the file and reopened…

This month’s challenge _might_ just drive you mad!

_Sounds easy? Then why not have a go? We’ll publish one solution in Monday’s blog. Have a great weekend in the meantime!_
