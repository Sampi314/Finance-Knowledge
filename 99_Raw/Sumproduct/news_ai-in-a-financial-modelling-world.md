AI in a Financial Modelling World
=================================

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/02/image-147.png)

The Microsoft Fabric User Group Network allows professionals from finance, analytics AI and data engineering to connect regularly to explore innovations in Microsoft Fabric, Power BI, Excel and AI-driven analytics.  There are currently 63 Meetup groups in 25 countries, reaching more than 110,000 members worldwide.  SumProduct’s **Ilgar Zarbaliyev** manages the Azerbaijan Power BI & Microsoft Fabric Community, and the Orlando Power BI User Group.

Last week, the Microsoft Excel Toronto Meetup Group (currently boasting nearly 6,000 members), invited a team from SumProduct to deliver a session called “AI in a Financial Modelling World”.  The link to the meetup is [here](https://www.meetup.com/msexceltoronto/events/313168646/)
.

**Liam Bastick**, **Kris Jaganah**, **Sam Ngo** and **Valentina Fan** provided a practical session grounded in real-world modelling challenges.  The team covered examples of what works and what doesn’t, emphasising that AI should assist modellers and not replace critical thinking.

The session explored how AI is reshaping financial modelling, especially within the Microsoft Excel ecosystem.  Some of the topics covered were:

*   where AI genuinely adds value
*   where AI is currently overhyped
*   what AI can automate reliably
*   where human judgment remains irreplaceable.

AI is no longer optional knowledge for financial modellers, and we recommend you follow our AI Blog.  We review AI tools and skills for 2026 [here](https://sumproduct.com/blog/ai-blog-ai-tools-reviewed-and-essential-ai-skills-for-2026/)
.
