AI Blog: Copilot Mode in Microsoft Edge – Part 2
================================================

_Welcome back to our AI blog series!  In Part 1, we explored how Copilot Mode helps you learn more efficiently inside Microsoft Edge.  Today, we’ll look at the new capabilities Microsoft recently added — making your browser smarter, hands-free and more context-aware._

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/image-53.png)

**_Effortlessly Explore the Web_**

With Copilot Mode enabled, opening a new tab offers much more than a blank page.  You’ll see a prompt where you can type or speak your task whether it’s asking questions, exploring ideas, reviewing content or launching websites.

Try Copilot Mode in Edge at [aka.ms/copilot-mode](https://aka.ms/copilot-mode)
. 

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/12/image-54.png)

Microsoft describes this as a combined chat, search and navigation bar designed to keep you focused in one place while AI works alongside you.

To try it, open a new tab and ask:

_“Create a list of must-visit places in Sydney, grouped by theme and travel time”._

Within seconds, Copilot returns a neat list including landmarks, beaches, food streets and cultural spots that is ready to save or export, all without switching tabs or apps.  This lets you explore the web without losing flow.

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

**_Get Help Instantly, Anywhere_**

Copilot Mode gives you real-time answers and recommendations as you browse.  When reading multiple pages, articles or product comparisons, you can ask Copilot to interpret them and surface the most relevant insights.

For example, while exploring several Excel learning blogs:

_“Summarise the key points and suggest a 2-week learning plan focusing on Power BI skills”._

Copilot looks through all the relevant content, extracts best-practice tips and drafts a study plan that you can copy directly into Word, OneNote or Excel.

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

**_Find What You Need Across Tabs More Quickly_**

With your permission, Copilot can analyse content across all open tabs, not just the one you are viewing.  This eliminates constant switching between pages.

Try opening several flight booking websites, then ask:

_“Across all these open tabs, which flight option from Sydney to Tokyo is the cheapest and most reliable on 15 December?  Highlight airline rating and total travel time__”._

Copilot scans each tab, creates a comparison table and flags the best choice.

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

This works well for:

*   research sessions
*   dashboard building
*   price hunting
*   apartment searches
*   job comparisons.

**_Stay Organised with Copilot Actions_**

Copilot Actions is a new feature that proactively suggests next steps based on your current task.  For instance, when reading travel guides, Copilot may offer:

*   add to itinerary
*   compare flights
*   check hotel availability
*   track these prices.

These prompts streamline your workflow, reducing friction and helping you act faster just like having a digital assistant anticipate your next move.

**_Keeping You in Control_**

Microsoft emphasises privacy and user control:

*   opt in manually
*   can toggle Copilot Mode off anytime
*   only reads tabs you approve
*   memory, voice and crosstab analysis can be disabled
*   can clear data whenever you wish.

All data is processed under Microsoft’s trusted security standards.

**_Word to the Wise_**

Copilot Mode in Microsoft Edge is not just about faster browsing.  It changes how you interact with information by transforming your browser into an active learning companion.

You may use it thoughtfully to:

*   keep privacy settings in check
*   verify summaries before using them professionally
*   experiment with structured prompts for clearer results.

Like other AI tools we’ve explored, Copilot rewards curiosity and precision.  The more intentional your prompts, the more valuable your insights.
