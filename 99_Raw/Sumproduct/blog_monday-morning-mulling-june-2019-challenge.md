_On the final Friday of each month, we set an Excel problem for you to puzzle over for the weekend. On the Monday, we publish a solution. If you think there is an alternative answer, feel free to email us. We’ll feel free to ignore you._

So, how did you try to fill all the blank rows? Here’s the trick:

First, select the whole column where you need to fill:

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/72aa864d2854c6fefb1083fba0ab5792-364.jpg)

Then, press **F5**, a **Go To** dialog will pop up like this, here click **Special**:

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/36776d1da4d05b45bb5a5d09375f407c-312.jpg)

Next, choose option **Blanks** in the **Go To Special** dialog:

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/23912d3b1671861e02bebcd5183f1607-277.jpg)

Excel will select blank cells in the chosen column. You will need to point the first blank cell to be equal to its precedent cell, then, press **CTRL + ENTER**.

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/6f49c288a0d88a66b427eaf4ece923d6-234.jpg)

And magic! The whole column will be filled in less than one second!

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/f1140ff857fc3b6f5f97a6a24f4a6fc7-392.jpg)

That’s our solution for this month’s Final Friday Fix: how did you fare?

_The Final Friday Fix will return on Friday 26th July with a new Excel challenge. In the meantime, please look out for the Daily Excel Tip on our home page and watch out for a new blog every business workday._
