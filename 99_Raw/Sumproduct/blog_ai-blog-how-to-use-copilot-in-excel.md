AI Blog: How to use Copilot in Excel
====================================

_Welcome back to our AI blog series!  After introducing how to play with the new **COPILOT** function in Excel, this time we see how we can use Copilot (the feature) to deal with daily tasks._

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

**_What is Copilot in Excel?_**

Microsoft describes Copilot as your AI partner and personal expert in Excel, helping to simplify data exploration, data analysis and reporting.  Instead of typing long and complex formulae or building charts from scratch, you can now use natural language to deal with your data.

**_Set up for Copilot in Excel_**

To use Copilot in Excel, you need to have both Copilot and Microsoft 365 subscriptions.  Additionally, you must ensure your Excel workbook is saved to OneDrive or SharePoint and double check that AutoSave is turned on.

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

**_Start with Copilot_**

Find the Copilot icon in the Ribbon (usually it is on the Home tab towards the right-hand side):

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

Click on the icon and you will see a chat window where you can have conversation with Copilot using natural language.  It is here you will also find suggested prompts for you to start the conversation; you can either choose one of these or ask your own questions.

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

**_Deal with the Dataset_**

The first step is always to import the data.  Copilot allows you to import data from the web, internal databases or even other Excel files.  No matter where the data are imported from, you need to format the data in either an Excel Table or a supported range.

For example, I have a Customer Behaviour dataset that comprises over 6,000 records, capturing detailed information on customer transactions in a retail environment:

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

To format the data as a Table, you can go to the Home tab and then ‘Insert Table’ (**CTRL + T**).

If you want to keep the data in a range, make sure the dataset meets all the requirements.  As Microsoft mentioned, these criteria may change due to improvements and updates.  They include the following considerations:

*   Only one header row
*   Headers are only on columns, not on rows
*   Headers are unique, no duplicate headers
*   No blank headers
*   Data is formatted in a consistent way
*   No subtotals
*   No empty rows or columns
*   No merged cells.

Here is an example of a range supported by Copilot.

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

Then, with the supported format, Copilot can help you to clean the data by identifying outliers, removing duplicates, or imputing missing values.

For example, you can simply ask, “_Are there any outliers in my data?_”

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

_“There are no outliers in the TotalSpend values for any region based on the interquartile range (IQR) method.  This means all regions have spending amounts that fall within a typical range, with no extreme values detected.”_

Copilot will go through the table and return the outliers and explain the results to you.

**_Generate and Explain Formulae_**

In the past, writing formulae for specific calculations could be challenging, especially for new users.  Now, Copilot can generate formula columns and rows automatically when you describe the calculation.

It can also create single-cell formulae that combine multiple steps into one neat expression.  The explanation comes with the result can help you to understand the formulae, learning how they work step by step.

For example, in the Customer Behaviour dataset, beside the **TotalSpend** values, I also want to see the average and median values by region, but I am not sure how to write the necessary formulae.  What I can do is ask Copilot, “_Summarise customer spending patterns by region with explanation”_ and I get the results as follows:

**_Powerful Insights and Suggestions_**

As we mentioned before, AI is good at pattern recognition.  Numbers and text both tell stories, and Copilot helps you uncover them faster than before.  As Microsoft explains, Copilot in Excel can:

*   **Highlight, sort and filter** key data points quickly
*   **Get insights from numerical data**, such as trends, variances or growth rates
*   **Extract insights from text-based data**, spotting keywords, sentiment or recurring themes.

The most amazing point is that instead of writing complex formulae by yourself, what you need to do is just describe tasks in plain English.

**_Data Visualisation_**

Presenting data well is just as important as analysing it.  With Copilot, you can:

*   create charts that highlight patterns, peaks or outliers
*   build PivotTables automatically, summarising large datasets in seconds
*   create lookups even if you are not that familiar with **INDEX MATCH** or **XLOOKUP** syntax. 

These features help you detect trends and create charts, which can be helpful in different business scenarios and industries, including financial analysis, marketing analysis, sales forecasting and inventory management.

In the Customer Behaviour dataset case, if we want to see the monthly sales trend, we may ask Copilot, _“Show me the monthly sales trend by product category in 2024”_ and then we get the chart that is exactly what we need.
