_Welcome back to our Charts and Dashboards blog series__. This week, I look at how to plot milestones on a timeline._

John, my favourite imaginary salesperson, has a list of tasks to complete to secure a new contract.

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

To make it clearer to follow, I am going to create this in milestones on a timeline. I am going to complete this in two blogs. This week I will arrange all my data, and next week I will complete the formatting.

I copy the original data and add a **Placement** column which will determine if the milestone appears above or below my timeline.

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

I am going to start by inserting a chart, but I make sure that I am not selecting any data, as I want to be in complete control of what appears in the chart. On the Insert tab, I choose to insert a ‘Line with Markers’ chart.

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

I relocate my blank chart and choose to ‘Select Data’ from the ‘Chart Design’ tab.

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

In the dialog that appears, I choose to ‘Add a Series’:

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

I begin by creating the timeline, so I add a series called **Date**, which will be linked to the **Task** cells in column **F**.

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

This doesn’t look right yet: I need to Edit the ‘Horizontal (Category) Axis Labels and select the dates in column **E**.

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

This looks more like a timeline. Now I can add my **Task** series.

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

This time I am going to use the **Placement** column as the values.

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

I have the key elements I need, now it’s time to format my chart. Next time I will change this:
