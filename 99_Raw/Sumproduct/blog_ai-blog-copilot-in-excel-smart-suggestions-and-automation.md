AI Blog: Copilot in Excel – Smart Suggestions and Automation
============================================================

__In the _[previous blog](https://sumproduct.com/blog/ai-blog-how-to-use-copilot-in-excel/)_ in this series, we introduced some of the basic functions like generating formulae, cleaning data and detecting trends.  Today, we go a step further.  Copilot is not only a formula writer, but also a thinking partner and task helper.  Two features that make it stand out are Smart Suggestions and Automating Repetitive Tasks.__

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

**_Smart Suggestions: When You Don’t Know What to Do Next_**

Sometimes in Excel, the most difficult part is not the calculation, but deciding what is the right analysis to run.  Copilot now helps with this too.  If you feel stuck, you can simply ask Copilot for suggestions.  For example, if you have sales data, you can say:

_“Show me a suggestion for a formula column.”_

Copilot may propose creating a column for growth rate or customer category.  This gives you an idea immediately without staring at blank cells.  If your analysis needs to be more specific or deeper, you can switch to ‘Advanced Analysis Mode’.  Copilot will generate charts using Python, such as:

*   Heatmaps to show correlations

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

*   Scatter plots for visualising clusters
*   Monte Carlo simulations to test different scenarios.

For example, we asked Copilot to perform customer segmentation.  Within seconds, it gave us clustering results and a Scatter Plot to show the groups.  This is something that usually requires coding in R or Python, but now it is one click in Excel (although Scatter charts may look similar).

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

**_Automating Repetitive Tasks: Save Time Every Day_**

The other feature that makes Copilot different from old Excel is its ability to automate your repetitive tasks.  Think about reports you need to prepare daily or weekly: sales summary, expense tracking, KPI dashboards.  Instead of writing the same formulae again and again, you can just say:

_“Create a daily sales summary by Region.”_

Copilot will build the table for you, with grouping and totals.  The magic part is, when tomorrow’s new data comes in, you just refresh, and Copilot repeats everything without any rework.

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

It also helps in formatting and structuring reports, reducing human errors and saving time.  For many analysts, this means less boring manual work and more focus on real insights.

**_Why it Matters_**

With these two \[2\] features, Copilot in Excel is more than a calculation tool.  It becomes a partner:

*   suggesting what you can do when you are unsure
*   automating what you don’t want to do again.

This combination is powerful because it helps both new learners and advanced users.  Beginners get ideas, whilst professionals save hours of repetitive effort.

**_Word to the Wise_**

Copilot in Excel is not just about writing formulae.  It gives you smart suggestions to inspire new analysis, and it can automate repetitive tasks to save time.

In the next blog, we will talk about requirements and limitations of Copilot in Excel, because many readers may ask: “Do I have access?”.
