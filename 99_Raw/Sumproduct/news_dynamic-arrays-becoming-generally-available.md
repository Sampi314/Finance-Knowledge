Dynamic Arrays Becoming Generally Available
===========================================

5 November 2019

It’s finally starting! We said we thought there might be an announcement at [Microsoft Ignite](https://www.microsoft.com/en-us/ignite)
 – and we were right! The long awaited roll-out of dynamic arrays starts now. Microsoft has announced they will slowly become Generally Available starting this week with Excel for the web.

You are going to need Office 365 at some point (it’s not coming to either Excel 2016 or Excel 2019), but Microsoft has stated that they “…will be rolling out to subsets of our users to continue to gather feedback and monitor quality…”.

Make sure you are part of the calculation revolution! If you have had your head in the sand for the past 12 months, please check out our crash course on dynamic arrays [\>here](https://www.sumproduct.com/thought/getting-arrays-spilling-the-beans-on-seven-new-functions)
 and what’s been going on in the past year or so since [here](https://www.sumproduct.com/thought/dynamic-arrays-one-year-on)
.
