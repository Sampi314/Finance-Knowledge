AI Blog: Manus: The Autonomous AI Agent Now Powering Meta’s Next Era of Intelligence
====================================================================================

_Welcome back to our AI blog series. In the past, artificial intelligence (AI) has been closely associated with chatbots that answer questions, summarise documents and generate text on request. That model is now starting to change. A new class of systems is emerging – autonomous AI agents that do not just respond, but act._

One of the most talked about examples of AI agents that act is Manus, an autonomous AI agent that moved firmly into the spotlight when Meta announced its acquisition in December 2025. Reported to be worth more than two \[2\] billion dollars (mere pocket change for SumProduct), this deal signals a clear shift in how large technology companies think about the future of AI from conversation to execution.

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

**_**_What Is Manus?  A New Breed of AI Agent_**_**

Manus is a general-purpose autonomous AI designed to complete tasks end-to-end with minimal supervision.  This means you give Manus a goal rather than a sequence of instructions.  It plans the steps, selects the tools, executes the workflow and returns a finished result.  That result might be a research summary, a data analysis, a piece of code or a fully generated report.

A helpful way to think about Manus is not as a chatbot, but as a digital collaborator.  Instead of asking, “What should I do next?”, you ask, “Please do this for me”.  The system then handles the intermediate decisions itself.

By early 2025, Manus was already processing trillions of tokens and running millions of virtual computing tasks for users around the world.  This is important because it shows Manus is not just a research demonstrative model, but also a system operating at real scale.

This ability to move beyond answers towards execution is why many observers see Manus as a meaningful step forward in autonomous AI systems.

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

**_**_Big Bet on Autonomous Agents_**_**

In December 2025, Meta announced that it would acquire Manus and bring its technology and team into its wider AI strategy.  Meta already owns some of the world’s most widely used platforms, including Facebook, Instagram and WhatsApp, as well as its broader Meta AI ecosystem.

This acquisition is not a small add-on.  It reflects a clear strategic direction.  You may have a question as many people have: why did Meta buy Manus?

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

There are three \[3\] key reasons behind this move.

*   **From passive assistance to autonomous action**

Most AI assistants today are reactive.  Manus brings something different: autonomy (a word used about 1,000 times so far in this article!).  It can plan and carry out multi-step tasks without needing constant human input  

*   **Integration at massive scale**

Meta’s products serve billions of users every day.  Embedding autonomous agent technology into these platforms could allow AI to move from answering questions to actively helping users complete tasks within messaging, content creation and business workflows  

*   **New monetisation paths**

Manus already operates as a paid service with real subscribers.  For Meta, this adds a potential revenue stream beyond advertising, particularly in professional and enterprise use cases.

It is considered Manus will initially continue to operate as a standalone product while Meta gradually integrates its capabilities into the wider AI stack.

**_Manus vs Traditional AI: What Is Really Different?_**

According to some observers, Manus will initially continue to operate as a standalone product whilst Meta gradually integrates its capabilities into the wider AI stack.

Chatbots operate by responding only when prompted.  In contrast, Autonomous AI agents can:

*   break a high-level goal into sub-tasks
*   interact with multiple data sources and tools
*   generate documents, spreadsheets and code
*   run automated workflows
*   deliver completed outcomes with minimal follow-up.

This shift turns AI from a conversational partner into a digital worker.

Imagine asking an AI to analyse competitor pricing, build a report and prepare a meeting summary without giving additional prompts.  That is the direction systems like Manus are aiming for.

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

**_Challenges and Regulatory Complexity_**

The acquisition has not been without its complications.  Since Manus originated from a team with its roots in China before relocating to Singapore, the deal has attracted regulatory attention.  Authorities are reviewing whether earlier technology transfers complied with export control rules.

Meta has stated that it will fully unwind any remaining Chinese ownership and wind down Manus operations in China.  This situation highlights a broader issue: as AI becomes more powerful, innovation increasingly intersects with geopolitics and regulation.

**_Word to the Wise_**

The acquisition of Manus is not just another AI headline.  It marks a shift from conversational AI towards autonomous action.  As the industry evolves, systems that can plan, execute and deliver outcomes will define the next phase of intelligence. 

We continue to move onwards: with Manus now part of Meta, we are likely to see AI move from talking about work to actually doing it.
