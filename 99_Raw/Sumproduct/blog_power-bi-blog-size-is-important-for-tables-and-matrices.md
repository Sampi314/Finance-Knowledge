Power BI Blog: Size is Important for Tables and Matrices
========================================================

_Welcome back to this week’s edition of the Power BI blog series._  _This week, we look at the automatic expansion of Tables and Matrices – and how it has now been revised._

Until recently, the Table and Matrix visuals automatically expanded their columns to fill the available space.  This feature was introduced for Tables in October 2025 and for Matrices in November 2025.  Many users felt this often meant that even small fields stretched wider than necessary, creating layouts that felt inconsistent or wasted screen real estate.

But now, that behaviour has been revised.  In reports created from this release onward, columns are once again will be sized by default based upon the content they contain.  This makes Tables and Matrices more compact, easier to read and better aligned with the actual data.

You can still manually adjust column widths or use ‘Grow to Fit’ to expand columns when needed.  However, by default, visuals now prioritise content-driven sizing, giving you simpler, more predictable layouts straight away.
