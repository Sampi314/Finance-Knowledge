Power BI Blog: Verified Answers Revised
=======================================

_Welcome back to this week’s edition of the Power BI blog series._  _This week, we consider recent improvements to Verified Answers._

Verified Answers now inherit and reflect the entire visual state: previously, this feature only captured filter states active at the time of creation.  Now, they now inherit the entire visual state, including slicers. 

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

Report slicers impacting the visual are being inherited and shown in the Verified Answer.

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

In addition to slicers, Power BI will support the following additional visual states:

*   Slicer selections
*   Field parameter choices
*   Cross filters and cross highlights
*   Drill-through filters.

Furthermore, there is now greater flexibility when configuring Verified Answers.  The limit for predefined filter permutations has increased from three \[3\] to 10, enabling more complex and nuanced configurations.

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

Microsoft has noted that most prompt requests naturally use between one \[1\] and three \[3\] filters, _e.g._

_“Show total sales for the region 1 in Q1”_

_“What is the usage of Product 1 in Country 1 during Month 1?”_

_“Show average satisfaction scores by City 1 and segment 1.”_

Still, some business questions require deeper combinations of filters and this expanded limit ensures verified answers can handle even the most detailed reporting scenarios.

Microsoft has also made substantial improvements in the Power BI engine room regarding how user prompts in Copilot are matched to Verified Answers and the appropriate filters.  Verified Answers now interpret and apply filters more accurately, reducing mismatches between what users ask and what’s displayed, and ensuring filters are included when they should be.

Microsoft has also enabled the highly requested new card visual that you can now set as Verified Answers.  The new card visual and Azure maps will be supported in Verified Answers creation and consumption.

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

These updates make Verified Answers feel native to your Copilot consumption experiences, adapting to slicers, filters, interactions and drill-through actions that users have come to expect from their visuals.  Consumers will get answers that more accurately reflect the context of their analysis.
