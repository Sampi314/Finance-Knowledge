Poisson D’Avril
===============

1 April 2018

Happy April’s Fool Day! In France, it’s called Poisson D’Avril. It is a special French tradition. According to [Frenchmoments.eu](https://frenchmoments.eu/april-fools-day-traditions-in-france-le-1er-avril/)
:

**_April Fools’ Day traditions are followed by all French children on 1 April. Paper fish are used to play an April Fools trick, involving sticking a paper fish onto the back of as many adults as possible, then running away yelling “Poisson d’Avril” (April Fish!)._**

In honour of this grand tradition our very own Cécile (with her French name) has written a brand new thought article about scenario modelling using the Inverse Poisson distribution right [here](https://www.sumproduct.com/thought/go-poisson)
