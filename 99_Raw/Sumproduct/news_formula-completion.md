Formula Completion
==================

Writing formulae in Excel could be time-consuming and difficult – even for us experienced users.  Selecting the right function, referencing the correct cells and ensuring the syntax is accurate can slow down your workflow and lead to mistakes.  Many users spend extra time searching for explanations and examples, troubleshooting errors or trying to remember the exact formula structure.

**Formula completion in Excel** is a new Copilot feature that is designed to solve these challenges.  Powered by AI models, formula completion proactively suggests and autocompletes formulae as soon as you type “**\=**”.  With formula completion you can create accurate formulae faster – and maybe they will even work!

When you type “**\=**” in a cell, Excel analyses the context of your workbook and suggests a formula completion suggestion, using the context of your worksheet, _e.g._ headers, nearby cells, formulae and tables / Tables.  Alongside the suggestion you will see a preview of the result, as well as a short description in natural language of the formula’s intent:

Here are some guidelines and tips for getting the best formula completion suggestion:

*   If you type “**\=**” and nothing appears right away, just wait a few seconds: Copilot’s suggestions may take a moment to load.  As Copilot generates the formula, a progress indicator will be displayed along the lower border of the cell:

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

*   Formula completion works best when there are clear labels / headers that help clarify the required context. Adding descriptive labels in the header or nearby cell greatly assists the process:

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

*   If you are struggling to find the correct formula, try refining the context and include additional details in adjacent cells or headers for better results:

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

Formula completion is rolling out for Copilot users on Excel for the web (US-English only) as well as Insider Beta on Excel for Windows.  Additional language support will be available in a future release.  It should be noted that formula completion is still in its early days, so what’s available may change as we continue to improve the experience.

If you’d rather not see formula completion suggestions in Excel for Windows, select **File -> Options -> Copilot**, then check the box to ‘Hide formula completion suggestions’ and select a timeframe.

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

An option to opt out in Excel for the web will be available soon.
