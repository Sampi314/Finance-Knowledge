AI Blog: How to Create Professional PowerPoint Slides in Minutes Using NotebookLM
=================================================================================

_Welcome back to our AI blog series.  Preparing presentations is something most of us do regularly, whether for internal meetings, client briefings or training sessions.  The challenge is rarely the ideas themselves, but the time it takes to turn rough notes, research or discussion points into a clear, professional slide deck_

Until recently, the process of preparing presentations involved a lot of manual effort: structuring the story, drafting slide text and searching for suitable visuals.  With the latest updates to NotebookLM, combined with Gemini and visual models such as Nano Banana (the popular codename for Google’s advanced AI image generation and editing models, such as Gemini 2.5 Flash Image and Nano Banana Pro), this workflow has become much faster and more accessible, even when using free tools only.

Now we are going to explore how [NotebookLM](https://notebooklm.google/)
 can help us move from scattered content to a polished presentation in minutes rather than hours.

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/01/image-81.png)

**_From Research Notes to Presentation Content_**

NotebookLM was originally positioned as an intelligent research assistant.  It learns directly from documents we upload and uses that context to summarise, explain and answer questions.  Its ability to go beyond clarification and actively generate structured content is impressive.

When we upload reports, meeting notes or draft materials, NotebookLM can now transform that information into a coherent slide outline.  Instead of staring at a blank slide deck, we start with a ready-made structure that already reflects our source material.  This shift alone removes one of the biggest barriers in presentation creation.

**_Getting Started with NotebookLM_**

Before generating any slides, we need to provide NotebookLM with source material.  This can be almost anything relevant: research papers, internal documents, existing slides or even informal notes.  The key point is that NotebookLM does not invent knowledge on its own.  Everything it produces is grounded in what we upload.

For example, I have uploaded the word files of our previous AI blogs introducing Copilot in Excel and pasted the link of SumProduct web.

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

Once the sources are in place, we can access the Studio panel and choose to generate a slide deck.  A short instruction is usually enough to get started, such as asking for a ten-slide presentation with clear headings and key talking points.  Within a short time, NotebookLM returns a structured outline that resembles the skeleton of a real presentation.

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

**_Shaping the Outline into a Real Presentation_**

The first version generated by AI should be treated as a draft.  This is the moment where human judgment matters most.  We can adjust slide titles, remove repetitive points and refine the language so that it matches our audience and purpose.

After reviewing the outline, we have flexibility in how we turn it into finished slides.  Some users prefer copying the content into Google Slides or PowerPoint, using familiar tools to apply branding and formatting.  Others may choose to work with Gemini’s Canvas, which can further refine text and suggest layouts.

Visuals are often the most time-consuming part of slide creation.  This is where Nano Banana becomes particularly useful.  Based upon slide content, it can generate diagrams or images that align with the message, reducing the need to search manually for graphics or icons.

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

**_Why This Workflow Is Useful in Practice_**

What makes this approach valuable is not just speed, but consistency.  Notes that might otherwise remain fragmented are turned into a logical narrative.  Presentations become easier to build, easier to revise and easier to reuse.

For professionals who prepare slides frequently, this means spending less time on formatting and more time thinking about insights, recommendations and decisions.  AI handles the heavy lifting of structure and draft content, while we remain responsible for accuracy and interpretation.

**_Word to the Wise_**

NotebookLM has quietly evolved from a research companion into a practical content creation tool.  When integrated with Gemini and Nano Banana, it allows us to build amazing presentations quickly, without relying on paid design platforms.

Used carefully, this workflow does not replace expertise.  Instead, it removes friction from repetitive tasks and gives us more time to focus on what really matters: communicating ideas clearly and effectively.
