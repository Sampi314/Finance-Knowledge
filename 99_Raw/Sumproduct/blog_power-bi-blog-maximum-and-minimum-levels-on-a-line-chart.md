Power BI Blog: Maximum and Minimum Levels on a Line Chart
=========================================================

15 April 2021

_Welcome back to this week’s edition of the Power BI blog series. This week, we will look at how to create maximum and minimum lines on a line chart._

Let’s take a look at an example of a line chart:

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/e774d10cbbb9450fc45efbe51abdf434-218.jpg)

This line chart displays the **Sales** over the first half of 2019 _(say)_. If we want to highlight the maximum and minimum points in this chart, we can use a built-in function contained within the Analytics tab. It works like this. With the visualisation selected, navigate to the Analytics tab:

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/f32e5a15e2cf9c3e4d2d058458ce054d-278.jpg)

We can expand the ‘Min line’ option to add a minimum line on the line chart:

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/f1140ff857fc3b6f5f97a6a24f4a6fc7-221.jpg)

We can adjust the colour and style of the ‘Min line’. In this example we have chosen to give it a red colour, and matching data label:

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/72aa864d2854c6fefb1083fba0ab5792-209.jpg)

Next, we can add a ‘Max line’, giving it a dark blue shade:

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/36776d1da4d05b45bb5a5d09375f407c-177.jpg)

These lines will update dynamically based upon the **x**\-axis filter selection (in this example the **Date** slicer). Compared to the previous screenshot, the maximum point has increased from 46,136 to 55,454, and the minimum point has decreased from 3,578 to 699.

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/23912d3b1671861e02bebcd5183f1607-156.jpg)

But what if we want a single dot on the line chart to highlight the maximum and minimum points? Well, more on that in the future blog!

That’s it for this week! A quick and easy guide on how to add minimum and maximum lines to the Line Chart visualisation in Power BI. Join us next week for more on Power BI.
