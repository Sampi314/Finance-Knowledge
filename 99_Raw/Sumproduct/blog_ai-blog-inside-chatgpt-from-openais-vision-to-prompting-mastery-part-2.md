**_What is a Prompt?_**

Generative AI refers to artificial intelligence (AI) models that can create new content such as text, images, music, code or even videos, based upon the instructions they receive.  Unlike traditional AI systems that only analyse or classify data, generative models produce original outputs that feel natural, creative and human-like.

In this context, everything begins with a prompt.  A prompt is the natural language input you give to an AI model to tell it what to do.  It serves as the bridge between human intention and machine response.

For image generation models, prompts are usually detailed descriptions of what you want to see.  For example, “A fat crocodile with a gold crown, wearing a three-piece suit, photorealistic, 4K studio lighting”.  The more specific the description, the closer the model comes to your vision.

In LLMs like ChatGPT-5, prompts can range from simple statements such as “The president of the US is…” to complex instructions involving datasets, documents or problem-solving tasks.  They can also be casual, like “Tell me a joke, I’m feeling down”.

The power of modern AI lies in how flexibly it interprets prompts.  Whether you need an itinerary for a trip, an explanation of advanced concepts or a creative story, the way you frame your input shapes the quality of the output.

Mastering prompts is not just about asking questions; it’s about learning to communicate effectively with AI.

**_Designing a Prompt_**

A prompt may seem like nothing more than plain text expressing what you want from an AI model.  However, there’s more to it.  Whilst models like ChatGPT-5 are incredibly powerful, they also have quirks.  They are trained to predict the next word and depending upon how you frame your input, the results can be either misleading (hallucinations) or highly valuable.

That’s why learning to design prompts is important.  A well-structured prompt can combine different elements:

*   **Instructions:** “Write a three-paragraph business proposal for launching a coffee shop”
*   **Questions;** “What are effective ways to reduce stress in the workplace?”
*   **Input data:** “Lena is a 30-year-old marketing manager from Berlin who wants to transition into data analytics.  Write a motivational letter for her”
*   **Examples:** “My friend enjoys movies like Interstellar, Arrival and The Martian but dislikes horror films.  Suggest three films for a movie night”.

Therefore, while prompts are written in natural language, they benefit greatly from structure.  The more thoughtful your design, the more precise and useful your AI-generated results will be.

**_Basic Prompt Examples_**

A prompt can include instructions, a question, input data or examples.  To get a meaningful result, at least a question or instructions should be present, whilst the other elements are optional.

Here are some simple cases with ChatGPT-5:

*   **Question + Instructions:** “How should I prepare my job interview presentation?  Suggest what sections to include, what tone to use and mistakes to avoid”
*   **Instructions + Input Data:** “Given the following details, write a motivational speech.  I’m from Sydney, Australia, recently completed a data science degree and I want to inspire high school students to explore careers in technology”
*   **Question + Examples:** “Here are some authors I enjoy: Haruki Murakami, Margaret Atwood and Neil Gaiman.  I don’t like Dan Brown or Nicholas Sparks.  What other writers would you recommend?”.

These examples show how prompts can guide the model to produce relevant, personalised outputs.  By mixing structure with creativity, you turn a general AI into a tool that fits your specific needs.

**_What is Prompt Engineering?_**

Prompt engineering is a fast-growing discipline focused on designing the most effective prompts for generative AI models like ChatGPT-5.  Instead of coding model architectures or tuning features, the goal is to craft inputs that guide the AI toward useful and accurate outputs.

Good prompt engineering requires both domain knowledge (to define what good results look like) and an understanding of the model (since different models react differently to the same prompt).  At scale, prompts can even be generated programmatically.  

For example, if you have a database of employees with short bios, you could use a template like “Given the following profile, write a professional introduction for LinkedIn: \[bio\]”.  A loop could then generate tailored outputs for each person automatically.

Similar to software engineering, prompt engineering is iterative.  It involves experimenting, refining and even applying practices like version control and testing.  With new tools emerging rapidly, prompt engineering is becoming a key skill for working effectively with AI.

**_Using Curly Brackets to Build Smarter Prompts_**

One effective way to improve prompt design is by using curly brackets **{ }** to create flexible templates.  This method allows you to swap out variables while keeping the structure consistent, making it easier to generate diverse outputs without rewriting the entire prompt.

For example, suppose we want to generate poems in different languages about Microsoft technologies. Instead of writing a separate prompt each time, we can use a template like the following.

Prompt Template:

“Write me a poem about {topic} in this {language}.  Make the poem 4 verses long and write it in rhyming couplets with 8 beats in each line.”

By filling in the placeholders, you can instantly create multiple variations:

“Write me a poem about Azure Cloud in French…”

“Write me a poem about Microsoft Teams in English…”

“Write me a poem about Power BI in Spanish…”

**_Word to the Wise_**

Prompts are the foundation of working with generative AI and mastering them unlocks the full potential of tools like ChatGPT-5.  From simple questions to structured instructions, adding input data or examples and even using curly brackets for reusable templates, the way you design your prompt shapes the quality of the response.

Prompt engineering is quickly becoming a core discipline, blending creativity with technical precision.  By understanding both the possibilities and limitations of AI models, you can avoid issues like hallucinations and instead generate content that is accurate, personalised and valuable.

In short, effective prompting is not just about telling AI what to do – it’s about learning how to communicate with AI strategically.  With practice, prompts evolve from simple inputs into powerful tools for writing, analysis, creativity and problem-solving.
