Charts and Dashboards: Learning our Lines – Part 2
==================================================

10 March 2023

_Welcome back to our Charts and Dashboards blog series. This week, we offer a tip to create a chart that shows the deviation from the average._

We have some simple sales data:

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/0ed35c2d0bda13383bf47425c55b704f.jpg)

[Last time](https://sumproduct.com/blog/charts-and-dashboards-learning-our-lines-part-1/)
, we created a Line chart to view the trend of the **Total** by **Month**. We looked at how to remove the gap between the end of the line and the ends of the horizontal axis:

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/736ec7b58038add77857f18543ec6ada.jpg)

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/99217a419c330044e0a69cf1faeaf74f.jpg)

This time, we’ll look at how we can create this chart:

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/af7c0c2d15c41e6f13e5a4580813a506.jpg)

We start with the same data, but we create some more columns.

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/d2d289a146030fc2b34cdcdf97a7cb58.jpg)

**Average** gives us the average salesperson’s sales, **Max** is the maximum, and **Min** is the minimum.

We then select the **Month** column, and the three \[3\] new columns, and choose a Line chart, as we did [last week](https://sumproduct.com/blog/charts-and-dashboards-learning-our-lines-part-1/)
:

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/5e8a2a520ae090ea99c0cc31eda5122a.jpg)

There are several ways to change the chart type for the data series. We can either right-click on an individual series, or we can select the chart and change the chart type by accessing the right-click menu or using the ‘Chart Design’ tab:

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/7227a5176fe382b9e90d745d0bc08c6a.jpg)

We change to a Combo chart:

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/45ccff45adaa5a683825f0dc1c464e27.jpg)

We then change **Average** to a Line, and **Max** and **Min** to Area charts.

![](https://sumproduct.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/05/1da0a4b885759c3e22301d9d53b83021.jpg)

Our chart is now taking shape. Next, we select the **Max** data series and double-click to access the ‘Format Data Series’ pane:

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

We change the Fill to ‘Solid fill’ and choose a green colour:

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

We can then use the dropdown next to ‘Series Options’ to select each of the other series.

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

We change the colour of **Min** to white:

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

We change **Average** to a black solid line and reduce the Width:

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

We also apply the fix we used [last week](https://sumproduct.com/blog/charts-and-dashboards-learning-our-lines-part-1/)
 to make sure our data reaches the edges of our chart by formatting the horizontal axis so that the ‘Axis position’ is ‘On tick marks’:

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

We can then delete the legend and change the title:

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

Our chart is complete.

Next time, we’ll look at a variation of this chart which highlights the negative and positive deviation:

![](<Base64-Image-Removed>)

_That’s it for this week, come back next week for more Charts and Dashboards tips._
