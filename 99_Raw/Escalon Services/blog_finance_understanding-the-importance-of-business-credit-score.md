Business credit score explained
-------------------------------

### What is a business credit score?

It gauges the overall creditworthiness of a business. It demonstrates how financially sound and reliable a company is and how likely it is to make its owed payments on time. 

A business credit score is also a numerical measure like a personal credit score. A higher score is generally better, but there are some variations among credit bureaus.

### How it works?

Three major credit bureaus determine business credit scores in the US: Equifax, Experian, and Dun & Bradstreet. The scores are calculated based on the business’s history of credit. This entails considering the payment history of loans, settlement of credit utilization, established trade lines, and the total debt level.

The business credit score determines a business’s creditworthiness for several things, including loans, insurance, collateral requirements, credit cards, and payment terms. 

Key benefits of having a robust business credit
-----------------------------------------------

  
Having an exemplary business credit score has many advantages, especially from the perspective of availing of business loans. Here are the main benefits:

### 1\. Quick loan approvals

Higher credit scores ensure eligibility for business loans as well as trade lines. Lenders are usually hesitant to lend money to businesses with poor credit histories for fear they won’t be able to make their interest payments on time or at all. As a business with an excellent credit score, you’ll likely be able to avail loans more easily and quickly.

### 2\. Favorable loan terms

A high [business credit](https://escalon.services/blog/6-steps-to-effectively-build-business-credit/)
 score lets you borrow loans at competitive interest rates and terms. Additionally, it expedites the loan approval process.

### 3\. Easier access to funds

A healthy credit score makes it much easier for companies to find investors willing to back them. Having investors of repute on board ensures you always have funds available for expansion and growth.

### 4\. Better payment terms from suppliers

Maintaining a solid credit score also bodes well for the credit terms suppliers provide. Knowing that your business is financially stable and capable of repaying debts promptly, suppliers will be more comfortable forgoing prepayment and allowing you to purchase on credit. 

### 5\. Protects personal finances

Building business credit allows you to segregate personal and business financial responsibilities. This ensures that your credit rating remains unaffected by any business’s economic challenges, as your company debts are reported separately on small business credit reports.

Pro tips to build a strong credit score
---------------------------------------

### 1\. Register your small business

Ensure that you register your business with the Secretary of State’s office. This move would help you separate your business as a separate entity, legally distinct from you as an individual, making it easier to establish business credit. 

### 2\. Open a business bank account

Establish a corporate banking account separate from your finances. Doing so will help you authenticate your firm as a separate legal entity.

### 3\. Pay creditors on time

Prioritize paying bills in full and on time. Consider leveraging real-time [financial reporting](https://escalon.services/blog/understanding-financial-reporting-what-is-it-and-why-it-matters/)
 to ensure timely payment of your bills and invoices.

### 4\. Get a business credit card

Opt for a secured corporate credit card backed by a deposit account. These cards will give your small business a solid starting point to build a solid credit history. 

### 5\. Establish trade lines with suppliers

Set up strong trade lines with suppliers and vendors who report payment activities to credit bureaus. This will ensure that your purchases and payments get reported to business credit reporting agencies.

### 6\. Reduce existing debt

Ensure you fulfill all your financial obligations. This entails paying off your business’s credit card debt and settling vendor payments and other outstanding balances.

The final word
--------------

  
Lenders, insurers, investors, and suppliers rely on business credit reports to evaluate a company’s financial health and creditworthiness. With a solid business credit, you’ll create a safety net for your small business, so you should be able to secure the business funding you need for financial stability and growth.
