NetSuite Planning and Budgeting Made Better with the Right FP&A Integration
---------------------------------------------------------------------------

Companies using ERP software experienced [an 11% reduction](https://blog.gitnux.com/erp-statistics/)
 in operational costs on average. If you’re currently using Oracle NetSuite, you’ve no doubt seen a wide variety of improvements across your organization. That said, many, _many_ organizations fail to extract maximal value from their ERP because they fail to make the most out of the NetSuite planning and budgeting potential. 

The Major Benefit of Planning and Budgeting with NetSuite
---------------------------------------------------------

One of the most laborious parts of FP&A is the manual entry of financial and operational data into spreadsheets and other forms. Gathering this data from across your organization takes hundreds of workforce hours and is, let’s face it, a menial task beneath the expertise of your financial team. 

Worse yet, despite all the advances in technology, FP&A is taking roughly [10% more time](https://www.mckinsey.com/capabilities/operations/our-insights/putting-the-a-back-in-f-p-and-a)
 than it was a decade previous. 

The major benefit of NetSuite budgeting and [forecasting](https://www.golimelight.com/glossary/forecasting)
 is that the ERP gathers all your data, consolidating it and making it easier to transfer over to Excel (or whatever tool you use to facilitate [FP&A](https://www.golimelight.com/glossary/financial-planning-analysis)
 activities). 

While this does save your team some time, it isn’t enough. NetSuite financial planning and budgeting functionality simply isn’t able to help your organization cut down on those hundreds of wasted hours because they still have to manually input the data from the ERP into your FP&A tool. 

In other words, planning and budgeting with NetSuite does help, but it by no means solves the problem. We’re talking about saving a few hours, not dozens of hours. 

But the good news is that you can quickly and easily enhance NetSuite budgeting and forecasting with one simple integration, **helping you cut down your reporting and budgeting time by as much as 75%**.

How can you accomplish this [NetSuite planning](https://www.golimelight.com/blog/top-3-budgeting-forecasting-reporting-platforms-netsuite)
 and budgeting optimization? 

Optimizing NetSuite Budgeting and Forecasting
---------------------------------------------

Planning and budgeting in NetSuite can be significantly improved by integrating it with an [FP&A platform](https://www.golimelight.com/)
 that **leverages the data consolidation functionality of NetSuite and gathers the data into the FP&A platform, saving your team hundreds of hours of time every year**.

By automating planning and budgeting in NetSuite with the right FP&A integration, you can save your financeteam hours of frustration and allow them to use that time much more productively by analyzing the data, helping you better determine high ROI resource allocation. 

Aside from NetSuite planning and budgeting enhancements, NetSuite forecasting (which is to say using the data from your NetSuite when forecasting) is much more robust. 

How to Improve NetSuite Forecasting and NetSuite Financial Planning with the Right Integration
----------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------

For many organizations, the real benefits of NetSuite planning and budgeting are in forecasts and planning. 

Yes, using NetSuite for reporting is obviously a benefit to your business in terms of making this business process much easier and simpler, but by leveraging the real-time data from NetSuite, you will improve forecast accuracy. 

Better yet, once the integrated financial data is examined using a robust FP&A platform, you’ll unlock a number of business insights that were formerly unavailable to your business. That’s because, when only combining Netsuite planning and budgeting with Excel, you’re using past financial statements and other data sources, often weeks or months behind when you’re finished with the process of transferring data over.

This is especially important in volatile economic times where [interest rates](https://tradingeconomics.com/united-states/interest-rate)
, pandemic closings, [bank collapses](https://www.golimelight.com/blog/challenges-of-financial-services-that-were-a-part-of-svbs-failure-mitigating-risk-effectively)
, etc., have all demonstrated that economic situations can dramatically shift in just a few hours or days. 

This also facilitates driver-based planning, whereby you can begin to really peel back the layers of your financial data, assessing where the growth areas are within your organization and enabling you to allocate the appropriate resources to this department in support of these high-yield efforts.
