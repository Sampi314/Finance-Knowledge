You can catch up on my previous posts in our [blog area](https://www.fullstackmodeller.com/blog)

What have I covered this week?
------------------------------

This week was a little tricky for revision as I was at the Global Excel Summit in London. My revision was therefore limited to four hours spread over Friday and Saturday.

I worked through the LNC Manufacturing case, logging my time at the end of each key build block:

Block 1: (1 hour) Read case, build core structure, revenue, CoS and balance sheet reconciling

Block 2: (45 mins) Depreciation, working capital, equity and dividends

Block 3: (30 mins) Debt, interest and tax

Block 4: (20 mins) Scenarios and tidy up

Block 5: (remainder) Final review

The first block felt like a real grind, the concepts are simple, but there is lots to get through in the first hour.

Given that we have four hours for the exam my timings feel pretty comfortable. I should have a reasonable amount of time to review at the end and some leeway should my brain decide not to work properly on the day.

Exam set up
-----------

The other key thing that I did in preparation for the AFM exam was to run through the proctoring setup. I recommend running through this as soon as you have the email link. FMI make the whole process very easy, with a clear process and instructions.

A few key points that I am pleased to have cleared up in advance:

The proctoring software requires Google Crome. I use Firefox so needed to download Crome and will need to remember to use Crome on the day.

I use my laptop and one large monitor. You are only allowed one monitor - this is easily changed in monitor settings (only show monitor 2).

My microphone did not work initially - I had to go into my microphone privacy settings to allow the proctoring software to access it.

The auto pick-up of my ID didn't work however much I waved it around in front of the screen. Eventually, I used the manual link - which just took a picture of the ID - super simple. On the day I will be clicking on that straight away.

And finally - I think I might have to tidy my office a little before the exam as they require a full scan of the room. All of the mess hidden just out of view of my webcam is going to have to go!

**What is the Financial Modeling Institute?**
---------------------------------------------

![FMI-1](https://www.fullstackmodeller.com/hs-fs/hubfs/FMI-1.png?width=774&height=585&name=FMI-1.png)

The Financial Modeling Institute (FMI) was founded in 2017 with the goal of “Promoting awareness, excellence and discipline in Financial Modeling through world-class accreditation programs”.

The Financial Modeling Institute (FMI) focuses on assessment, leaving the delivery of training to independent and proven partners like us at Full Stack Modeller.

**What are the FMI certification levels?**
------------------------------------------

The Financial Modeling Institute (FMI) has three certification levels. These are: Advanced Financial Modeler (AFM); Chartered Financial Modeler (CFM); and Master Financial Modeler (MFM).
