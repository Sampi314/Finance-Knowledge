_Short definition:_ [Vertical analysis](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/vertical-analysis/)
 is a [financial analysis](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/financial-analysis/)
 technique that expresses each line item in a financial statement as a percentage of a base amount within the same statement.

_Explanation:_ It allows for a more meaningful comparison of financial data within a single period, highlighting the relative proportions of different items within the statement. For the [income statement](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/income-statement/)
, the base amount is usually total revenue, while for the [balance sheet](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/balance-sheet/)
, it’s typically total assets.

_Example:_ In a vertical analysis of an income statement, each expense item (like cost of goods sold, [operating expenses](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/operating-expenses/)
, etc.) is shown as a percentage of total revenue. This helps identify which expenses are the largest relative to sales and how they have changed over time.

_Additional information (optional):_ Vertical analysis can be used to identify trends and anomalies in a company’s [financial statements](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/financial-statements/)
, compare companies of different sizes, and assess the efficiency of its operations. It is a valuable tool for understanding the composition of a company’s financial statements and identifying areas for improvement.
