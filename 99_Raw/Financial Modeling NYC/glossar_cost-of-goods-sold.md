_Short definition:_ [Cost of goods sold (COGS)](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/cost-of-goods-sold/)
 refers to the direct costs incurred in producing or purchasing the goods sold by a company.

_Explanation:_ It includes the cost of raw materials, direct labor costs, and manufacturing overhead. For merchandising businesses, COGS represents the purchase price of goods. COGS is a crucial component of the [income statement](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/income-statement/)
 and directly affects a company’s gross [profit margin](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/profit-margin/)
.

_Example:_ For a manufacturing company, COGS would include the cost of raw materials, wages paid to factory workers, and the cost of running the factory. For a retailer, COGS would simply be the purchase price of the goods sold.

_Additional information (optional):_ COGS is calculated differently for manufacturing, retail, and service businesses. It’s important to understand the specific calculation method for your industry to accurately assess a company’s profitability.
