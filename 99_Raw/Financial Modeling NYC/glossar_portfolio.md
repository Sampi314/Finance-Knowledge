_Short definition:_ A [portfolio](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/portfolio/)
 is a collection of investments held by an individual or organization.

_Explanation:_ It can include a variety of assets, such as [stocks](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/stocks/)
, [bonds](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/bonds/)
, real estate, [commodities](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/commodities/)
, and cash equivalents. The purpose of a portfolio is to spread risk and potentially increase returns by diversifying investments across different [asset](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/asset/)
 classes and industries.

_Example:_ An individual’s investment portfolio might include a mix of stocks, bonds, and mutual funds, while a company’s portfolio might include real estate holdings, investments in other companies, and cash reserves.

_Additional information (optional):_ Portfolio management involves selecting and managing investments to achieve specific financial goals, such as retirement planning or wealth preservation. Investors can choose to manage their portfolios themselves or hire a professional portfolio manager.
