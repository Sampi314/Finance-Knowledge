_Short definition:_ [Debt financing](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/debt-financing/)
 is the process of raising capital by borrowing money from lenders, with the promise of repayment with interest over a specified period.

_Explanation:_ It involves taking out loans from banks, issuing [bonds](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/bonds/)
, or obtaining credit lines from financial institutions. Debt financing allows companies to access capital without diluting ownership but requires regular interest payments and repayment of the principal amount.

_Example:_ A company might take out a bank loan to finance the construction of a new factory or issue bonds to raise capital for expansion projects.

_Additional information (optional):_ Debt financing can be a cost-effective way for businesses to access capital, especially when interest rates are low. However, it also increases a company’s [financial leverage](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/financial-leverage/)
 and risk, as it must make regular interest payments and eventually repay the borrowed amount.
