_Short definition:_ [Sensitivity analysis](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/sensitivity-analysis/)
 is a [financial modeling](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/financial-modeling/)
 technique used to assess how the outcome of a model changes when one or more input variables are adjusted.

_Explanation:_ Sensitivity analysis helps to identify the key drivers of a model’s results and to assess the potential impact of different scenarios on the model’s outcome. It is often used in [risk management](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/risk-management/)
 and decision-making to evaluate the robustness of a model and to determine the most critical factors affecting its outcome.

_Example:_ In a financial model for a new product launch, sensitivity analysis could be used to assess how changes in the product’s price, sales volume, or production costs would affect the projected profitability of the product.

_Additional information (optional):_ Sensitivity analysis can be performed using various methods, such as one-way sensitivity analysis (changing one variable at a time), two-way sensitivity analysis (changing two variables simultaneously), or [scenario analysis](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/scenario-analysis/)
 (evaluating the impact of multiple variables under different scenarios).
