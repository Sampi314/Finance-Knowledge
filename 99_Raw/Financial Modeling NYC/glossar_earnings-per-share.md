_Short definition:_ [Earnings per share (EPS)](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/earnings-per-share/)
 is a financial ratio that indicates the portion of a company’s profit allocated to each outstanding share of common stock.

_Explanation:_ EPS is a key metric for evaluating a company’s profitability and is often used by investors to compare the performance of different companies. It is calculated by dividing a company’s net income (after preferred [dividends](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/dividends/)
, if any) by the average number of outstanding common shares during a specific period.

_Example:_ If a company has a net income of $1 million and 1 million outstanding shares of common stock, its EPS would be $1.

_Additional information (optional):_ EPS can be reported in two ways: basic EPS and diluted EPS. Basic EPS considers only the outstanding common shares, while diluted EPS takes into account the potential [dilution](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/dilution/)
 from convertible securities, such as [stock options](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/stock-options/)
 and convertible 1 [bonds](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/bonds/)
.
