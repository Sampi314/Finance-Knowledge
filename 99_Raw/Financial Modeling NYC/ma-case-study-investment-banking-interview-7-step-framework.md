Mastering the M&A Case Study Interview: A Step-by-Step Approach
===============================================================

![Two women are involved in an M&A case study interview. ](https://www.financial-modeling.com/wp-content/uploads/2025/11/MA-Case-Study-Interview-Banking.jpg)

Landing an [Investment Banking](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/investment-banking/)
 role often requires navigating the M&A case study—a complex interview challenge designed to test your analytical thinking, commercial judgment, and ability to perform under pressure.

This guide provides the definitive **seven-step framework** used by top-tier candidates. Mastering this approach, rather than memorizing solutions, is the key to success.

* * *

Step 1: The Initial Setup—Deconstructing the Prompt
---------------------------------------------------

The first 5–10 minutes are the most critical. Do not rush into the model. Use this time to establish the framework, manage expectations, and show strategic control.

### 1\. Define the Objective (The “Why”)

Immediately clarify the overarching goal. Is the case about:

*   **Accretion/[Dilution](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/dilution/)
     (A/D)?** Testing [synergy](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/synergy/)
     [valuation](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/valuation/)
     and EPS impact.
*   **Strategic Rationale?** Testing commercial sense and market impact.
*   **Valuation Comparison?** Testing ability to synthesize different methodologies (DCF vs. Comps).

### 2\. The Information Dump and Key Assumptions

List all given inputs and categorize the assumptions you _must_ make.

*   **Given:** Target’s Revenue, EBITDA, Debt, Cash, Shares Outstanding, Transaction Price/Premium.
*   **Assumptions to Clarify:**
    *   **Financing Structure:** How will the Acquirer pay (Cash, Stock, Debt, or mix)? This is often the biggest sensitivity.
    *   **Synergies:** Are they included? If so, are they Revenue (harder to capture) or Cost (easier)?
    *   **Tax Rates:** Acquirer’s and Target’s corporate tax rates.

### 3\. Establish the Output and Timeline

Set expectations by stating your proposed framework (the next six steps) and asking the interviewer if they agree. This establishes your **Expertise** and control.

> “My proposed approach is to quickly define the transaction structure, run the Accretion/Dilution analysis, then summarize the key sensitivities before discussing the strategic implications. I estimate this will take 30 minutes. Does that sound right?”

* * *

Step 2: Transaction Structure and Sources & Uses
------------------------------------------------

Before any financial calculation, you must define the transaction’s financial architecture.

### A. The Sources and Uses Table

The Sources & Uses table confirms that the deal **balances**. It shows the total cash outflow required and where that cash will come from. This demonstrates fundamental M&A mechanics.

*   **Uses (Total Cost):** [Equity](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/equity/)
     Purchase Price (Purchase [Equity Value](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/equity-value/)
    ) + Refinancing of Target Debt + Transaction Fees.
*   **Sources (How to Pay):** Acquirer Cash + New Debt Issuance + New Equity (Stock) Issuance.

$$\\text{Total Sources} = \\text{Total Uses}$$

### B. Determining the [Pro Forma](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/pro-forma/)
 [Balance Sheet](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/balance-sheet/)

The structure determines the post-deal ownership and balance sheet adjustments.1 The most common pitfall here is the **Goodwill Calculation**.

$$\\text{Goodwill} = \\text{Equity Purchase Price} – \\text{Target Book Value} \\pm \\text{Fair Market Value Adjustments}$$

* * *

Step 3: Valuation Synthesis (Setting the Baseline)
--------------------------------------------------

You rarely build a full DCF model in a case study, but you must be able to rapidly synthesize valuation methods. Your analysis must justify the price being paid.

### 1\. The Multiples Check (Comparable Company Analysis)

Quickly run the implied multiples of the transaction against the peer group.

*   $\\text{Implied EV/EBITDA}$
*   $\\text{Implied P/E}$

> **Insight:** If the Implied EV/EBITDA is higher than the peer median, you must be prepared to articulate the **strategic premium**—why this target is worth more (e.g., control premium, unique intellectual property, proprietary technology).

### 2\. Precedent Transaction Analysis

How does the current deal’s premium (the percentage difference between the purchase price and the target’s pre-deal share price) compare to historical deals in the sector? A very high premium requires strong strategic justification.

* * *

Step 4: The Accretion/Dilution Analysis
---------------------------------------

This is the technical heart of the M&A case study. It tests your ability to model the combined entity’s earnings impact. The core focus must be on the **combined Net Income** and the resulting **Pro Forma [Earnings Per Share (EPS)](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/earnings-per-share/)
**.

### 1\. Calculate Combined Net Income

$$\\text{Pro Forma Net Income} = (\\text{Acq. Net Income} + \\text{Target Net Income}) – \\text{Total After-Tax Synergies} – \\text{After-Tax Financing Costs}$$

### 2\. Calculate Pro Forma Shares Outstanding

This step varies dramatically based on the financing:

*   **Cash/Debt Deal:** Shares Outstanding remains largely unchanged (minor impact from stock buybacks, if any).
*   Stock Deal: Add the shares issued to the Target’s shareholders.$$\\text{Shares Issued} = \\frac{\\text{Equity Purchase Price}}{\\text{Acquirer Share Price}}$$

### 3\. The A/D Result

$$\\text{Pro Forma EPS} = \\frac{\\text{Pro Forma Net Income}}{\\text{Pro Forma Shares Outstanding}}$$

$$\\text{A/D \\%} = \\frac{\\text{Pro Forma EPS} – \\text{Acquirer Standalone EPS}}{\\text{Acquirer Standalone EPS}}$$

*   **Accretion:** Pro Forma EPS > Acquirer Standalone EPS (The deal improves the Acquirer’s earnings).
*   **Dilution:** Pro Forma EPS < Acquirer Standalone EPS (The deal hurts the Acquirer’s earnings).2

* * *

Step 5: Sensitivity and Risk Analysis
-------------------------------------

A good analyst delivers a number; an expert delivers a range. You must show the interviewer that you understand the variables that could break the deal.

### Key Sensitivities to Test

1.  **Synergy Realization:** What happens to EPS if only $50\\%$ of cost synergies are achieved? This is the most common real-world sensitivity.
2.  **Purchase Price/Premium:** Test the A/D result at a higher or lower premium to understand the “break-even” point.
3.  **Financing Mix:** What if debt is more expensive? What if the Acquirer is forced to issue more equity (stock)?

### Strategic Risks

Go beyond the numbers and identify major integration risks (demonstrating **Commercial Judgment**):

*   **Integration Risk:** Potential culture clash, loss of key Target employees.3
*   **Regulatory Risk:** Anti-trust or HSR hurdles.
*   **Debt [Market Risk](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/market-risk/)
    :** Inability to secure financing at the assumed [interest rate](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/interest-rate/)
    .

* * *

Step 6: Strategic Rationale and The Recommendation
--------------------------------------------------

The [financial analysis](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/financial-analysis/)
 provides the _feasibility_; the strategic rationale provides the _justification_. Your recommendation must tie the numbers back to the commercial landscape.

### Justify the Deal (The 3 Core Drivers)

1.  **Financial Drivers:** The deal is accretive, creates tax benefits, or unlocks cost efficiencies (synergies).
2.  **Market Position Drivers:** The deal expands geographic reach, gains immediate access to new technology, or eliminates a competitor.
3.  **Value Creation:** The combined entity is worth more than the two standalone parts (the $1+1=3$ philosophy).

### The Recommendation

Your final recommendation must be decisive and nuanced.

> **Example Recommendation:** _“Based on the accretive EPS profile (3.5% accretion in Year 1) and the strong strategic fit in the \[Specific Market\] segment, I recommend proceeding with the [acquisition](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/acquisition/)
>  of the Target, provided we secure financing at the assumed rate. The key risk we must mitigate is the integration of the Target’s IT systems, which accounts for 60% of the projected cost synergies.”_

* * *

Step 7: Continuous Improvement—The Key to Success
-------------------------------------------------

The M&A case study is not an academic exercise; it’s a simulated workday. Interviewers are testing your **approach and communication** as much as your technical knowledge.

### The Analyst’s Checklist for Mastering Cases

| **Concept** | **What Interviewers Look For (EEAT)** |
| --- | --- |
| **Control** | Your ability to define the framework and timeline, showing **Expertise**. |
| **Communication** | Clearly articulate assumptions and sensitivities, demonstrating **Experience**. |
| **Synthesis** | Connecting the valuation multiples (Step 3) to the strategic rationale (Step 6). |
| **Risk Mitigation** | Identifying the top 2–3 risks beyond just the financial inputs. |

Memorizing the accretion/dilution formula is Step 4. **Mastering the seven-step approach is the path to receiving the offer.**
