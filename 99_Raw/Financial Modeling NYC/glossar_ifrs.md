_Short definition:_ IFRS, or International Financial Reporting Standards, is a set of accounting standards developed by the International Accounting Standards Board (IASB) that govern the preparation and presentation of [financial statements](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/financial-statements/)
.  

_Explanation:_ IFRS aims to provide a globally consistent framework for financial reporting, enabling investors and other stakeholders to compare and understand the financial performance of companies across different countries. It covers a wide range of accounting topics, including [revenue recognition](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/revenue-recognition/)
, [asset](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/asset/)
 [valuation](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/valuation/)
, and disclosure requirements.  

_Example:_ Under IFRS, companies are required to use fair value accounting for certain [financial instruments](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/financial-instruments/)
, which reflects their current market value rather than their historical cost.  

_Additional information (optional):_ IFRS is adopted by over 140 countries around the world, making it the dominant accounting standard for global businesses. While there are some differences between IFRS and US [GAAP (Generally Accepted Accounting Principles)](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/gaap/)
, efforts are underway to converge the two sets of standards.
