_Short definition:_ [Working capital](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/working-capital/)
 is a financial metric that represents the difference between a company’s [current assets](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/current-assets/)
 and [current liabilities](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/current-liabilities/)
.  

_Explanation:_ It measures a company’s ability to fund its day-to-day operations and meet its short-term financial obligations. A positive working capital balance indicates that a company has enough liquid assets to cover its short-term debts, while a negative balance suggests potential [liquidity](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/liquidity/)
 problems.

_Example:_ A company with $100,000 in current assets (e.g., cash, accounts receivable, inventory) and $70,000 in current liabilities (e.g., accounts payable, short-term debt) would have $30,000 in working capital.

_Additional information (optional):_ Working capital management is crucial for a company’s financial health. It involves optimizing the levels of current assets and liabilities to ensure sufficient liquidity while minimizing the cost of holding excess assets.
