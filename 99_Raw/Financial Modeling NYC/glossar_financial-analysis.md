_Short definition:_ [Financial analysis](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/financial-analysis/)
 is the process of evaluating businesses, projects, budgets, and other finance-related entities to determine their performance and suitability.  

_Explanation:_ It involves examining financial data, such as [financial statements](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/financial-statements/)
, to assess a company’s profitability, [solvency](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/solvency/)
, [liquidity](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/liquidity/)
, and overall financial health. Financial analysis can be used for various purposes, including investment decision-making, credit analysis, and performance evaluation.

_Example:_ A financial analyst might conduct a financial analysis of a company to determine its creditworthiness before granting a loan. They would examine the company’s [balance sheet](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/balance-sheet/)
, [income statement](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/income-statement/)
, and [cash flow statement](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/cash-flow-statement/)
 to assess its ability to repay the loan.

_Additional information (optional):_ Financial analysis uses various tools and techniques, such as [ratio analysis](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/ratio-analysis/)
, [trend analysis](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/trend-analysis/)
, and comparative analysis. It helps stakeholders make informed decisions about investments, lending, and business operations.
