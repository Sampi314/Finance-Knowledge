_Short definition:_ [Financial statements](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/financial-statements/)
 are formal records that summarize the financial activities and position of a business, individual, or other entity.

_Explanation:_ These statements provide a snapshot of an entity’s financial health and performance over a specific period. They are essential tools for investors, creditors, and other stakeholders to assess an entity’s financial performance, profitability, and [liquidity](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/liquidity/)
.

_Example:_ The main types of financial statements include the [balance sheet](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/balance-sheet/)
, [income statement](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/income-statement/)
, and [cash flow statement](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/cash-flow-statement/)
. The balance sheet shows a company’s assets, liabilities, and [equity](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/equity/)
 at a particular point in time, while the income statement reports a company’s revenues and expenses over a period. The cash flow statement tracks the flow of cash in and out of a company.  

_Additional information (optional):_ Financial statements are prepared in accordance with generally accepted accounting principles (GAAP) or International Financial Reporting Standards (IFRS). They are audited by independent accountants to ensure their accuracy and reliability.
