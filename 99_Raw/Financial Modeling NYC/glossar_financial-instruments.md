_Short definition:_ [Financial instruments](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/financial-instruments/)
 are tradable assets or contracts that represent a monetary value or a claim on future cash flows.

_Explanation:_ They facilitate the transfer of capital between investors and entities that need funding. Financial instruments can be broadly classified into four categories: cash instruments (e.g., currency, bank deposits), debt instruments (e.g., [bonds](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/bonds/)
, loans), [equity](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/equity/)
 instruments (e.g., [stocks](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/stocks/)
), and [derivatives](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/derivatives/)
 (e.g., [options](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/options/)
, [futures](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/futures/)
).

_Example:_ A bond issued by a company is a financial instrument representing a debt obligation, while a share of stock represents ownership in the company.

_Additional information (optional):_ Financial instruments are used for various purposes, including raising capital, managing risk, and generating returns. They are traded in different markets, such as stock exchanges, bond markets, and over-the-counter (OTC) markets.
