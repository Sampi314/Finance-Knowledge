_Short definition:_ [Shareholder’s equity](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/shareholders-equity/)
, also known as stockholders’ [equity](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/equity/)
 or owners’ equity, represents the residual interest in the assets of a company after deducting liabilities.  

_Explanation:_ It is the amount of money that would be returned to shareholders if all of the company’s assets were liquidated and all of its debts were paid off. Shareholder’s equity is a key component of a company’s [balance sheet](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/balance-sheet/)
 and can be broken down into various components, such as common stock, preferred stock, additional paid-in capital, and [retained earnings](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/retained-earnings/)
.  

_Example:_ If a company has total assets of $1 million and total liabilities of $600,000, its shareholder’s equity would be $400,000.

_Additional information (optional):_ Shareholder’s equity is often used as a measure of a company’s financial health and stability. A high level of shareholder’s equity indicates that a company has a strong financial foundation and is less reliant on [debt financing](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/debt-financing/)
.
