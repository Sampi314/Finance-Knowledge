How do I know which financial modeling course to choose for my specific goal? 

A frequently asked question we get is how the learning experience for interview prep differs from job-specific training. We firmly believe in hands-on training programs ranging from step-by-step model building blocks to valuation skills in both cases, meaning that you will build models live during the coaching sessions. Our Interview prep is designed to teach you the financial statement, accounting, and excel shortcuts, along with common tips to get you up to speed for excel modeling tests. In our job-specific training, e.g., for an active investment banking analyst, we will focus on more advanced topics and practical skills to arm you for complex problems that will hit your desk in your analyst or associate years. For example, this could be a financial analyst training program focusing on the differences of a corporate valuation model on a public company vs a private company. No matter your goal and current level, by the time you finish our training, you will be armed with knowledge of deeper financial concepts and advanced excel for financial modeling to take your career to the next level. 

What is the best way of learning financial modeling?

We believe that a c. five-week course with five two-hour coaching sessions and homework between each session is the best way to get you up to speed. We see many competitors offering things like 5-day financial analyst programs and large group classes, but we believe that these crash courses in finance are not optimal for learning, as you will always be bound to the slowest student in the class and very general topics, that may not fit the specific needs and details for what you are trying to accomplish.
