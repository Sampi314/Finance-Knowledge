_Short definition:_ [Equity](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/equity/)
 represents the ownership interest in a company or [asset](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/asset/)
.

_Explanation:_ In the context of a company, equity refers to the residual value of assets after liabilities (debts) have been paid off. It represents the shareholders’ claim on the company’s assets and earnings.

_Example:_ A homeowner’s equity is the value of their house minus the outstanding mortgage balance. For a business, equity can consist of various components like common stock, preferred stock, additional paid-in capital, and [retained earnings](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/retained-earnings/)
.

_Additional information (optional):_ Equity can be calculated as:

Equity = Total Assets – Total Liabilities

Equity holders have a claim on the company’s profits, either through [dividends](https://www.financial-modeling.com/glossar/dividends/)
 or through an increase in the value of their shares.
