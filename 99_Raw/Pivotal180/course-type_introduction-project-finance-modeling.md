Course Content
--------------

This course is designed to provide participants with knowledge of the core concepts that impact investments and debt structuring for project finance transactions. The program focuses on explaining the most important topics and modeling techniques such that students understand the many moving parts of a deal and how all the pieces tie together. Course participants will build an annual transaction model and complete case studies in that model to evaluate a renewable energy project finance transaction.

The Intro to Project Finance course is designed for beginning modelers and individuals who may not build models every day but for whom an understanding of project finance models and concepts is critical to decision making. The course utilizes a model for a solar or wind project but covers a broad set of topics such that it is also suitable for those in other project finance sectors like power or mining.

#### Course Syllabus

| **SECTION** | **OVERVIEW** |
| --- | --- |
| **Pre-Course** | *   Excel functions, formulas and shortcuts<br>*   Best practice modeling concepts<br>*   Intro to debt and equity<br>*   Benefits of leverage<br>*   Present value concepts and formulas |
| **Introduction to Project Finance** | *   What is project finance?<br>*   Investment structure<br>*   Passthroughs and blockers (USA) versus taxable entities<br>*   Role of parties and contractual cashflow<br>*   Allocation of risk<br>*   Project finance project cashflows |
| **Project Finance Term Loans** | *   Impact and effects of leverage<br>*   Lender returns and risk appetite<br>*   Determinants of leverage<br>*   Debt service coverage ratio (DSCR)<br>*   Weighted average cost of capital (WACC)<br>*   Debt repayment types<br>*   Sculpting and sizing debt<br>*   Debt ratios<br>*   Drivers of debt size |
| **Model Familiarization** | *   Model setup and structure<br>*   Model checks<br>*   Sensitivities & scenarios<br>*   Data tables |
| **Generation and the Grid** | *   Capacity versus energy<br>*   Capacity factors<br>*   Modeling generation |
| **Operations and Determining PPA Price** | *   Power purchase agreements (PPAs)<br>*   PPA risk and legal concepts<br>*   Modeling PPA & merchant offtake<br>*   Operating costs<br>*   Waterfall structure, returns & goal seek |
| **Depreciation and Tax** | *   Book versus tax depreciation<br>*   Taxable income (Earnings Before Tax)<br>*   Efficient versus inefficient (self-sheltering) taxpayers<br>*   Net operating losses (NOLs)<br>*   Present value benefits of depreciation and tax |
| **Tax Credits and IRA** | *   Production Tax Credit<br>*   Investment Tax Credit<br>*   Changes under the Inflation Reduction Act |
