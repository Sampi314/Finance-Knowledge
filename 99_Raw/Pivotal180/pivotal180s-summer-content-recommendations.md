Pivotal180’s Summer Content Recommendations 
============================================

By Bastian Stroemsheim | June 16, 2023

Taking a summer break from your financial models but want to stay connected with project finance? We have compiled a list of our best recommendations for summer readings and podcasts related to project finance, with a couple of bonus recommendations for movies/TV.

Our reading list is designed to give you a refreshing break from all the IRS guidance, and although it comes with tradeoffs, we have opted for the slightly more engaging options.

**The macro picture for decarbonizing and investing in renewable energy**

*   Speed & Scale: An Action Plan for Solving Our Climate Crisis Now (John Doerr)
*   Investing in the Era of Climate Change (Bruce Usher)
*   How to Avoid a Climate Disaster (Bill Gates)

**Can’t have enough of the sun?**

*   Solar Power Finance Without the Jargon (Jenny Chase)
*   Taming the Sun (Varun Sivaram)

**Dive further into the underlying model inputs**

*   The World for Sale: Money, Power and the Traders Who Barter the Earth’s Resources (Jack Farchy & Javier Blas)
*   The Price of Time: The Real Story of Interest (Edward Chancellor)

**For the captivating storytelling**

*   Superpower (Russell Gold)
*   Enron: The Smartest Guys in the Room (Bethany McLean & Peter Elkind)
*   The Power Broker (Robert Caro)

If you are more of an audio person, we would first recommend tuning in to our courses and interviews!

*   [In conversation with Lydia Li](https://pivotal180.com/?p=8938) 
*   [In conversation with Andrew Kessler](https://pivotal180.com/?p=9013) 
*   [In conversation with Gadi Taj Ndahumba](https://pivotal180.com/?p=9013)
    

In cases where a good podcast is all you truly search for, consider the following options:

*   Currents (Norton Rose Fulbright)
*   Catalyst with Shayle Kann (Canary Media)
*   Redefining energy

Lastly, for those who prefer something binge-worthy, we have a couple of suggestions for movies/TV. Although in some cases the ties to project finance are somewhat watered-down, you will still find some relevance. With that aside, our list includes:

*   The Big Short
*   Who Killed the Electric Car?
*   Chernobyl (HBO miniseries)
