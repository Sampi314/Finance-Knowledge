What is the structure of the online course & how many hours of content is the course?

The [online course](https://pivotal180.com/online-training/)
 is structured as follows:

1.  We have lessons, which cover the concepts of the items we will model, such as how to size debt for a project, or what is in a typical PPA Agreement
    1.  Within the lessons we also have Excel demonstrations, using simple annual numbers for 5-10 years
2.  In addition to this we have a full model walk-through, from start to finish talking through every line in the model
    1.  This is a video and is placed in between the lessons on concepts
    2.  The Excel files are also downloadable, but fundamentally you build up a model from start to finish
3.  The online class also has a discussion board to answer any questions you may have

The total content online at this stage is approximately 26 hours, this is equivalent to just over a full week course if it were delivered in person.

How much of this new project finance course overlaps with the renewables course?

**Do you have a detailed comparison about how much of this new project finance course overlaps with the renewables course?**

Similarities between courses

Project finance has many similarities between the renewable energy and infrastructure sectors. Many of the broader concepts are similar between the [courses](https://pivotal180.com/all-courses/)
, such as:

*   Overall concepts of project finance
*   Best practice modeling concepts
*   The broad approach to debt sizing
*   How to calculate construction funding and distributions
*   Tax and depreciation concepts
*   Debt service reserve accounts (DSRA)
*   3-way statements and accounts receivable and accounts payable

What are the main differences between the Project Finance and Infrastructure and Renewable Energy courses?

Differences between courses

The [project finance and infrastructure course](https://pivotal180.com/available-project-finance-courses/)
 has the following additions, not covered in the [renewable energy course](https://pivotal180.com/available-industry-related-courses/)
:

*   Description of Project Agreements and Concession Agreements common in infrastructure (as opposed to Power Purchase Agreements)
*   Case study is focused on a PPP hospital project
*   The model is less US-focused, meaning it covers:
    *   Taxable entities (i.e. SPVs who pay tax, which is common outside of North America) as well as the pass-through entities common in the US and Canada
    *   Reducing balance depreciation categories
*   Covers more in relation to modeling various depreciation categories
*   Debt sizing with gearing/leverage constraint added
*   Addition to construction funding: Government contributes a proportion of the capital costs
*   Distributions have the option to be limited to retained earnings constraints, common in many countries
*   Shareholder loans
*   Maintenance reserve accounts
*   Tax in the 3-way statements

Does the Tax Equity Online course provides a detailed dive into tax equity structuring?

It goes pretty deep! It goes through all of the [capital accounts, HLBV, sponsor buy-out options](https://pivotal180.com/available-tax-equity-courses/)

Does the modelling rely on macros or is it done through iteration?

We go through both of these approaches. We show iterations and how to enable them, but we also build up the macros AND show some ways to get around macros with algebra or based on commercial realities.

Do I need two monitors ?

To optimize the livestream training, we recommend that participants have 2 monitors (or one laptop plus one monitor). This enables students to watch the instructor while simultaneously implementing the techniques in their own Excel file. With dual screens, it is also possible for each participant to share their Excel file simultaneously, so that the instructor can see progress and assist – or even take remote control – when necessary.

This equally applies to the [online self-paced course](https://pivotal180.com/online-training/)
 where you can watch the videos while implementing the techniques on your own model.

Of course, this is not an absolute requirement. The training is completely workable with only a single laptop, but we have found that the comprehension and engagement has been better for participants with two screens than for those who are using a single laptop.
