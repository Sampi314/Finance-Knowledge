Course Content
--------------

Data centers represent a fast-growing global industry, already worth over $350 billion and projected to reach $1 trillion dollars by 2034. As cloud computing and artificial intelligence (AI) accelerate this expansion, there’s a rising demand for professionals who understand how these projects are financed, structured and modeled.

In this 5-video mini-course , you’ll learn some terminology, get a quick look at how retail and wholesale data center facilities earn revenue, the major cost drivers that shape their performance, and how they fit within the real estate / infrastructure spectrum. It’s a great starting point if you’re curious about one of today’s most dynamic asset classes.

This free course does not include a financial model or a deep dive into the subject. The course will later be expanded to become a mini-course with a financial model (not a free course).

The first (and free) version of this course will be released onlinein **December 2025**.The free course will be available to everyone for a period of 3 months. During that time, we will produce some more detailed lessons and a financial model which will be included in a paid version of the course

#### Course Syllabus

1.  **What are Data Centers? (The REAL Cloud Explained)**
    *   What Data Centers are
    *   Data Center applications
    *   Hyperscale vs. Edge Data Centers
2.  **How Data Centers Make Money**
    *   The Data Center business model
    *   Retail vs. Wholesale Data Centers
    *   Colocation agreements
    *   Remote hands
    *   Smart hands
    *   Cross connects
    *   IT load power consumption
3.  **Main Costs of Data Centers**
    *   Estimated power consumption of a Data Center
    *   Power Redundancies built into Data Centers
    *   Backup power vs. UPS
    *   Power costs
    *   Cooling costs
    *   Staff costs
    *   Other costs
4.  **Data Centers: Real Estate or Infrastructure?**
    *   Collateral vs. Cashflow based investments
    *   Differences in investment type
    *   Differences in asset complexity
    *   Differences in power procurement
5.  **Project Finance in Data Centers**
    *   Size of the Data Center market
    *   Major players in the industry
    *   Understanding a Data Center financial model
    *   Where to learn more about financial modeling
