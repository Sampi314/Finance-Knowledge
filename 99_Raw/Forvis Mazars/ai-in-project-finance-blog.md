AI in Project Finance: New Course and Webinar
=============================================

By [Carlie Hughes](https://financialmodelling.forvismazars.com/author/carlie-hughes/ "Posts by Carlie Hughes")

Thursday 12th February 2026

Artificial intelligence is rapidly reshaping how financial models are built reviewed and used particularly in capital intensive sectors such as energy and infrastructure. As project finance models grow in size complexity and stakeholder scrutiny AI presents a significant opportunity to improve efficiency consistency and insight while still maintaining robust governance and best practice standards.

> “AI is reshaping financial modelling with unprecedented speed and accuracy, but real impact comes from adopting it responsibly. One major focus must be to balance innovation with the risk, compliance, and governance frameworks our industry demands.” [Rickard Warnelid](https://www.linkedin.com/in/warnelid/)
> , Partner, Forvis Mazars APAC Energy

To help practitioners understand and apply AI in a practical disciplined way Forvis Mazars APAC Energy has launched two complementary initiatives the [AI in Project Finance webinar](https://financialmodelling.forvismazars.com/resources/ai-in-project-finance-webinar/)
 and the new [AI in Financial Modelling course](https://financialmodelling.forvismazars.com/course-schedule/ai-in-project-finance-modelling-course/)
.

The webinar \[[AI + Project Finance Models; Industry update](https://financialmodelling.forvismazars.com/resources/ai-in-project-finance-webinar/)\
\] provides a focused project finance specific introduction to how AI can be applied across the modelling lifecycle. It explores real use cases such as accelerating model build processes improving review and error detection supporting scenario analysis and enhancing documentation demonstrating how AI can augment rather than replace established modelling judgement.

Building on this foundation the [AI in Financial Modelling course](https://financialmodelling.forvismazars.com/course-schedule/ai-in-project-finance-modelling-course/)
 takes these concepts further offering a structured and hands on learning experience. The course is designed to equip participants with practical workflows tools and frameworks that can be applied directly to live models while addressing key considerations around governance transparency and model integrity. Together the webinar and course form a clear pathway from awareness and insight to applied capability.

Both initiatives are led by [Rickard Warnelid](https://www.linkedin.com/in/warnelid/)
 (Partner), [Joshua Grimm](https://www.linkedin.com/in/joshuagrimm/)
 (Director) and [Rael Levinsohn](https://www.linkedin.com/in/rael-levinsohn-a1b7474/)
 (Associate Director) from Forvis Mazars APAC Energy drawing on their extensive experience advising on complex project finance and infrastructure models across the region.

Whether you are beginning to explore AI’s role in financial modelling or looking to embed it into established project finance practices these initiatives provide a practical industry grounded perspective on what AI can realistically and responsibly deliver today.
