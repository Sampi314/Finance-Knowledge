Introduction to Battery Energy Storage Systems (BESS) is designed for energy professionals, project developers, investors, and advisors looking to grasp the technical and financial aspects of BESS projects.

Gain a clear overview of how BESS supports grid reliability and renewable integration, while unlocking revenue through services like peak shaving and frequency response. Learn about key components, system integration, performance metrics, market players, global trends, and project economics.

This course will also provide real-world case studies to illustrate how successful projects manage risk and deliver profitability and provide a deep dive into a typical BESS financial model.

#### This training course will enable you to:

*   Gain a clear understanding of BESS technologies and explore how BESS supports grid stability and renewable integration through services such as frequency response
*   Understand the commercial landscape, including major market players, global trends, and revenue opportunities
*   Analyse project economics with confidence, covering CAPEX, OPEX, financing structures, IRR, and payback periods
*   Learn from real-world case studies that highlight risk management, investment strategies, and successful project outcomes
*   et to grips with the financial model through an applied case study and understand how the technical and financial assumptions come together within this to help analyse BESS projects

### Who should attend this course focused on project finance models?

This course is ideal for anyone involved in the development, financing, or analysis of energy storage projects. Typical attendees include Analysts, Project Developers, Investment Managers, Engineers, and Advisors working across the energy, infrastructure, and finance sectors.

### Pre-course knowledge

No prior knowledge of battery storage systems or financial modelling is required. This course is accessible to professionals from both technical and non-technical backgrounds.

There are no prerequisites for this course.

Course Agenda
-------------

### Introduction to BESS Fundamentals

An overview of the role and importance of Battery Energy Storage Systems (BESS) in the energy transition. This section will introduce the key concepts of BESS technology and its applications within the energy markets.

*   Applications of BESS in energy markets, including ancillary services and grid support
*   Creating diverse revenue streams through BESS deployment
*   Supporting the renewable energy transition by addressing intermittency and enhancing grid reliability

### Technology overview of BESS

A high-level overview of the components and operation of BESS. This section will provide key entry level technical insights into batteries and the systems that support them.

*   Key system components, including batteries, inverters, and energy management software
*   Integration of BESS with grid infrastructure and optimisation of its performance
*   Delivering value through ancillary services and peak shaving capabilities
*   Key technical considerations that impact project economics, including duration, efficiency, and lifespan of storage systems

### Overview of Major Players in BESS Ecosystem

Understanding the key stakeholders within the BESS industry and their roles

*   Insights into global and European market trends, with a focus on industry dynamics and strategic approaches
*   Exploration of roles of major players, including manufacturers, developers, and investors

### Financing Strategies and Project Economics

This section will explore the financial considerations essential to BESS projects

*   Breakdown of Capital Expenditure (CAPEX) and Operating Expenditure (OPEX) for BESS installations
*   Financing structures, explained through real world example transactions
*   Common return metrics: payback periods, Internal Rate of Return (IRR), and Net Present Value (NPV)
*   Strategies to secure investment and mitigate financial risks in BESS projects

### Understanding co-located vs standalone BESS projects

This section will explore the specific relationship between BESS and renewables and how co-location can generate upside and reduce risk:

*   Introduction to co-located BESS, including examples of co-location with solar and wind
*   Key differences between co-located and standalone BESS in relation to grid connection, capex implications and operational flexibility
*   Commercial drivers for each model and matching project type to market structure
*   How revenue stacking differs between co-located and standalone assets
*   Investor risk and returns for co-located assets vs standalone assets
*   Outlook and emerging trends in the European market, with a detailed focus on the UK market

### Understanding the Financial Model though an applied case study

As part of this session, attendees will take part in an interactive case study focussed on a standalone BESS project, demonstrated through a financial model:

*   Understand where the financial model fits within the broader deal structure and its role in supporting investment decisions
*   Gain an understanding on how interpret a financial model and examine the key features of a typical BESS financial model, including structure, logic, and outputs
*   Explore the interaction between technical advisors and the financial model, and how technical inputs are translated into financial assumptions
*   Overview of best practice modelling techniques and tips and tricks to help you navigate complex financial models and find what you’re looking for quickly
*   Attendees will follow the lifecycle of a standalone BESS asset, from development through to operation, and review the financial model inputs step by step as each topic is covered throughout the training course.
*   From capex and opex to revenue stacking, degradation and financing, attendees will learn how commercial, technical, and market factors drive project value
*   Through a hands-on approach and introduction to best practise scenario & sensitivity management, key concepts are reinforced and will help attendees to apply them in a practical, investment-focussed context
*   By the end of the session, attendees will have a clear understanding of how standalone BESS projects are modelled, and the risks, assumptions, and opportunities that shape real world transactions.
