Gain valuable insights from this essential course designed for anyone interested in the creation of operational or ‘asset’ models. Deal Model to Operational Model Mastery course caters to all stakeholders, including project sponsors, bankers, advisors, and more.

This comprehensive program equips you with the necessary tools and knowledge to transform a deal model into an operational model, while enhancing your understanding of the principles and best practices that form the foundation of these models.

### This project finance modelling course will enable you to:

*   Develop a deep understanding of the differences between a deal models and operational models and the principles underlying each
*   Master best practice techniques to systematically and accurately develop an operational model from a deal model
*   Achieve consistent and accurate outputs using the Forvis Mazars Financial Modelling methodology
*   Learn efficient modelling techniques to deliver more analysis, faster and more accurately

### Who should attend this course focused on project finance models?

This course is ideal for anyone who needs to update, build, review or analyse operational models. Typical attendees include Analysts, Managers, Senior Managers, and Associate Directors.

### Pre-course knowledge

While no prior modelling knowledge is strictly necessary, participants are expected to have a good understanding of Excel and financial modelling. Upon registration, you will receive our introductory material on Excel functions, ensuring you have the confidence and skills to fully absorb the course content.

Course Agenda
-------------

**Master proven techniques for best practice modelling resulting in better models built faster**

*   Learn to work with the powerful and easy to follow Forvis Mazars Financial Modelling methodology
*   Discuss and identify good vs. bad modelling techniques using real-life examples
*   Greatly increase consistency amongst your team in calculation technique and presentation

**The importance of Operational models and their uses?**

*   Understand why assets require operational models following financial close
*   Outline the importance of the update process management for updating an operational model
*   Learn when an operational model is need and when should they be thought about

**Understand the structure of financial close model and the importance of ‘Actuals’ functionality**

*   Discuss common issues found when converting financial close models to operational models
*   Understand Forvis Mazars’s best practice approach to incorporating historic financial information into the model
*   Develop an interface with an accounting system data dump and the model to allow historic information to be easily incorporated
*   Understand how to convert accounting data into the required level of detail for forecasting purposes
*   Learn how to incorporate and reforecast the historic financial information obtained from accounting systems

**How to deal with information required beyond those provided by your accounting systems**

*   Gain an understanding of the additional information required for tax computations and how to incorporate this

*   Learn how to update depreciation calculations and how to incorporate historical capital expenditure in the computations

**Cleaning up the model and removing legacy calculations**

*   Learn to identify redundant sections as we transition from deal model to operational model
*   Understand how to fix errors logically as redundant calculations are removed
*   Learn how to remove items required for financial close but no longer required within the operational model

**Implementing signed debt agreements in the operational model**

*   Discuss the common reporting requirements for key stakeholders and how to ensure that the model is reporting these correctly
*   Gain insights on how to automatically calculate historic DSCR values into the model and understand the challenges created by differences between accounting data vs. actual cash flows
*   Understand how to adapt your debt repayment schedule within the model and how to adjust for a change in the drawdown profile
*   Learn the requirement of freezing debt size, debt repayments and debt interest

**Testing and checking to ensure that the operational model is forecasting appropriately**

*   Implement key checks that allow key stake holders to have confidence in model reforecasting correctly from the inputted historical data
*   Learn and perform tests that ensure that all historical information is considered and has been incorporated into the model
*   Identify issues within the testing and debug these errors
*   Gain skills to develop a comparison tool to allow analysis between financial close and reality
