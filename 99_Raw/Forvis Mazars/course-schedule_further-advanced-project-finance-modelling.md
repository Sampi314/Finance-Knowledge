Advanced financial modelling skills are essential for structuring, analysing, and evaluating complex financing arrangements. They enable professionals to build robust models that support decision-making, manage risk, and optimise outcomes.

This one-day interactive course focuses on the complex components of a project finance model. Participants will engage in hands-on exercises to build clean, dynamic, and presentation-ready models using the Forvis Mazars methodology.

The course highlights advanced financing features, including shareholder loans, cash sweeps, interest rate swaps, refinancing strategies, and essential tax elements. Participants will learn to model complex debt mechanics, incorporate hedging instruments, manage multiple debt tranches, and account for tax effects such as withholding tax and interest deductibility. The training blends theory with practical application and applies across sectors such as Mining, Oil & Gas, Power, Renewable Energy, Real Estate, and Infrastructure.

### Key learnings from this course:

*   Extend your project finance modelling skills to take a transaction from screening to financial close.
*   Develop techniques for multi-tranche debt sizing, covenant monitoring, and advanced structures, including shareholder loans and mezzanine debt.
*   Gain a clear understanding of re-leveraging during refinancing and how to correctly implement this.
*   Understand the mechanics and modelling techniques of Maintenance Reserve Accounts (MRA).
*   Learn advanced tax topics such as deferred tax, interest deductibility, and withholding tax.

### Who should attend this course focused on project finance models?

Whether you’re a developer, owner, investor, lender, or advisor, this course is designed for professionals who already understand project finance modelling and want to go deeper. Complex Financing Techniques in Project Finance provides advanced skills to tackle sophisticated debt structures, hedging strategies, and refinancing mechanics – helping you build deal-ready models and make informed decisions in the fast-growing infrastructure sector.

### Pre-course knowledge

Advanced project finance modelling experience is essential for this course. It does not cover fundamentals and is designed for participants who are comfortable building and working with complex financial models. Attendance on our Advanced Project Finance Modelling course is strongly recommended to ensure readiness for the advanced techniques covered.

Course Agenda
-------------

**Understand the uses of shareholder loans (SHL) in projects and learn how to implement them in your models**

*   Learn how to incorporate shareholder loan as a formal source of funding within the project finance waterfall
*   Integrate SHL interest, rolled-up interest, and repayment mechanics from first principles, ensuring correct treatment of subordination and cashflow priority

**Gain insights into the applications of senior debt cash sweeps and how they integrate debt mechanics**

*   Discuss the strategic use of senior debt cash sweeps in strengthening lender security
*   Implement a cash sweep based on defined trigger events

**Deepen your understanding of interest rate swaps as hedging instrument in project finance**

*   Understand the benefits of swaps and their treatment as hedging instruments
*   Learn how to incorporate interest rate swaps directly into the debt mechanics

**Understand the concept of ‘debt freezing’ and apply this to existing debt mechanics**

*   Discuss the importance of debt freezing and its usefulness in sensitivity analysis
*   Learn & implement a mechanism for freezing debt size and repayment profiles

**Learn how to handle multiple debt tranches within the capital structure and manage their sizing, service and covenants correctly**

*   Build a robust mechanism to size and structure multiple debt tranches within the model, reflecting individual terms for each facility
*   Break multiple sizing circularities using VBA driven optimisation techniques

**Gain insights into the use of refinancing & re leveraging of debt**

*   Understand the strategic rationale and financial benefits of refinancing, including re leveraging
*   Learn how to introduce refinancing into a financial model by restructuring the debt size

**Build a Maintenance Reserve Account (MRA) and integrate it into the cashflow waterfall with correct seniority**

*   Learn how to construct a Maintenance Reserve Account using first principal calculations
*   Understand the benefits of an MRA in strengthening project

**Integrate withholding tax into investor cashflows to accurately assess post tax returns and distribution outcomes**

*   Discuss the rationale behind withholding tax
*   Learn how to apply WHT to dividends and shareholder loan interest within the model

**Understand interest deductibility rules and model their impact on taxable income**

*   Learn the core concept of interest deductibility and the key elements of the calculation, including tax EBITDA limits, and de minimis thresholds
*   Explore carried forward disallowed interest balances and review common modelling mistakes, reinforcing how to structure calculations to avoid errors and ensure accurate tax forecasting

**Understand the concept of  deferred tax assets and liabilities and learn how these can impact your model them**

*   Learn the core concepts underpinning deferred tax, including temporary differences and timing differences
*   Build deferred tax asset and liability calculations from first principles and Integrate deferred tax movements into the financial statements
