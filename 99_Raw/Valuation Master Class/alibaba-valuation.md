What Is Alibaba Worth? A Valuation Overview
-------------------------------------------

Alibaba Group Holding Limited (NYSE: BABA / HKG: 9988) is China’s largest e-commerce and cloud computing company, with gross merchandise volume (GMV) that historically exceeded Amazon’s — a comparison the original VMC case study used as its opening hook: “What’s interesting about Alibaba is that its GMV is twice that of Amazon.” As of early 2025, Alibaba’s market capitalization is approximately $200–220 billion — a fraction of its 2020 peak of ~$900 billion, reflecting the compounding effects of Chinese regulatory crackdowns, geopolitical risk, and competitive pressure from PDD Holdings (Pinduoduo/Temu) and Douyin (TikTok’s Chinese parent).

Based on a discounted cash flow (DCF) analysis using a WACC of 10.5% and a terminal growth rate of 4%, Alibaba’s intrinsic value is approximately $90–110 per ADR — suggesting meaningful upside from current prices (~$80–90 in early 2025), if you believe China’s regulatory cycle has peaked and cloud computing continues to scale.

The Original Case Study: Alibaba’s Investment Dilemma
-----------------------------------------------------

A speech that crashed the share price
-------------------------------------

*   In late 2020, Jack Ma gave a speech criticizing China’s banking system as being inefficient. This led to the Chinese government’s realization that China’s big techs were wielding too much power.
*   The Chinese government responded with an immediate cancellation of Ant Group’s IPO and a series of anti-monopoly fines.

Recent updates on Alibaba
-------------------------

*   **Ant Group:** Restructured into a financial holdings company, subjected to regulations that apply to financial institutions. Alibaba and Ant Group terminated their data sharing agreement. No plans to revive the IPO. Alibaba holds a 33% stake in Ant Group.
*   **Hong Kong primary listing:** Hong Kong Stock Exchange approved Alibaba’s primary listing, making Alibaba eligible to participate in stock connect with Mainland China — giving qualified Mainland Chinese investors access to Alibaba shares.

Revenue breakdown 2022
----------------------

The main revenue driver is the China Commerce segment, divided into Customer management (e-commerce platform) and Direct Sale (e-grocery). The China Commerce segment is supported by Logistics, Local Consumer Service, and Cloud segments which build Alibaba’s ecosystem.

Shifting to New Retail strategy to drive top-line growth
--------------------------------------------------------

*   Alibaba’s Sun Art, Tmall Supermarket, and Freshhippo subsidiaries are instrumental in driving growth of the New Retail strategy — blurring the line between online and offline retail.
*   All of Alibaba’s subsidiaries’ physical supermarkets and hypermarkets are integrated into Alibaba’s platform, enabling less-than-one-hour delivery to customers.

Direct sales to become a new revenue growth engine
--------------------------------------------------

*   Direct sales already account for 45% of China commerce segment in 2022 compared to just 16% in 2019. The explosive growth was driven by the pandemic.
*   Digital grocery sales in China reached nearly $200 billion in 2021, with the market size expected to almost double by 2025.

New Retail strategy provides growth but comes at a cost
-------------------------------------------------------

*   Alibaba’s traditional driver was its e-commerce platforms with low inventory costs. With the surge in direct sale revenue (operating supermarkets), Alibaba has increased its inventory cost, leading to a decline in gross profit margin.

Superior market share means profit beats its closest rival
----------------------------------------------------------

*   In 2021, Alibaba had a market share of 47% of China’s retail e-commerce sales, while its closest rival, JD.com, had 17%.
*   This allows Alibaba to outperform JD.com on profitability measurements. Despite anti-monopoly regulations, it is unlikely that JD.com will catch up to Alibaba soon.

Consistent value creation compared to rival
-------------------------------------------

*   Unlike JD.com, Alibaba has consistently delivered value to its creditors and shareholders. Alibaba’s superior ROIC is attributable to its higher profitability and more robust ecosystem compared to its rival.

Key risk is regulations
-----------------------

*   Alibaba operates in an increasingly complex legal and regulatory environment.
*   Failure to build successful ecosystem.
*   Alliance risks; synergies between subsidiaries might not be successful.

Conclusion
----------

*   Direct sales to drive further growth.
*   Decline in gross margin due to enhanced focus on direct sale.
*   Dominant position likely to continue despite regulations.

2025 Update: Has Alibaba’s Dominant Position Held?
--------------------------------------------------

The original case study’s core question — can Alibaba maintain its dominant position? — has been partially answered by 2025. The results are mixed:

*   **E-commerce market share decline:** Alibaba’s share of China’s e-commerce market has fallen from ~47% in 2021 to approximately 35–38% in 2024, as PDD Holdings (Pinduoduo/Temu) and JD.com gained share. This is the most important competitive development since the original case study.
*   **Cloud computing growth:** Alibaba Cloud (AIDC) has emerged as a genuine growth engine, generating approximately RMB 110–120 billion in annual revenue with improving profitability. AI infrastructure demand — from LLM training and inference — is a meaningful tailwind.
*   **Regulatory clarity:** The Chinese government’s anti-monopoly campaign reached a plateau in 2023. Alibaba paid an RMB 18.2 billion fine in 2021 and has since operated with greater regulatory clarity. The government has shifted from “crackdown” to “supportive guidance” mode for tech companies.
*   **International commerce (AIDC / Temu competition):** Alibaba’s international commerce segment (AliExpress, Lazada, Trendyol) faces intensified competition from Temu (PDD Holdings) and SHEIN globally. Alibaba’s response has been to focus on higher-value international markets rather than compete on ultra-low prices.
*   **Restructuring and spinoffs:** In 2023, Alibaba announced a major restructuring into six independent business units (e-commerce, cloud, logistics, entertainment, digital media, and international commerce) — each with its own CEO and potential for independent fundraising or IPO. This sum-of-parts restructuring was aimed at unlocking the “conglomerate discount” that the original case study identified as a valuation risk.

What Is Alibaba’s ROIC?
-----------------------

Alibaba’s [return on invested capital](https://valuationmasterclass.com/what-is-return-on-invested-capital/)
 of approximately 10–12% in FY2025 reflects the company’s high-margin core e-commerce business, partially offset by the capital intensity of cloud infrastructure investment and logistics expansion. ROIC comfortably exceeds Alibaba’s estimated WACC of ~10.5%, confirming economic value creation — but the ROIC spread is thin, making any deterioration in core margins a material risk.

How Do You Value Alibaba Using a DCF Model?
-------------------------------------------

Valuing Alibaba via [DCF valuation](https://valuationmasterclass.com/dcf-valuation/)
 requires several China-specific adjustments: applying a country risk premium to the [weighted average cost of capital](https://valuationmasterclass.com/what-is-weighted-average-cost-of-capital/)
, modeling Alibaba’s VIE (Variable Interest Entity) structure that creates legal uncertainty about offshore shareholders’ rights, and separately valuing Alibaba’s net cash of $60–70 billion which is not reflected in operating business multiples alone. Understanding the [growth capex vs maintenance capex](https://valuationmasterclass.com/growth-capex-with-maintenance-capex/)
 distinction is particularly important for Alibaba’s cloud business, which is still in an investment phase requiring significant data center and infrastructure spending. Determining [which valuation method is most suitable](https://valuationmasterclass.com/which-valuation-method-is-the-most-suitable-for-different-types-of-companies/)
 for Chinese conglomerates like Alibaba requires a SOTP approach similar to Tencent.

Common Mistakes When Valuing Alibaba
------------------------------------

### Mistake 1: Anchoring to the 2020 Peak Valuation

Alibaba peaked at approximately $900 billion in market capitalization in 2020. Treating that as “fair value” and the current $200 billion as “the discount to fair value” ignores genuine structural changes: Chinese e-commerce competition is permanently more intense, regulatory risk is permanently higher, and the VIE structure permanently discounts minority shareholder rights.

### Mistake 2: Using a US Tech WACC for a Chinese Company

Applying a 7–8% WACC (appropriate for US tech mega-caps) to Alibaba ignores China country risk (~2–3% premium), VIE legal risk (~1% premium), and capital controls risk (~0.5% premium). The correct WACC for Alibaba is approximately 10–11%.

### Mistake 3: Treating E-Commerce and Cloud as the Same Business

Alibaba’s core e-commerce business generates ~55–60% adjusted EBITDA margins — one of the highest of any business in the world. Alibaba Cloud is still in profitability improvement mode with margins of ~5–8%. Blending these two businesses at a single multiple dramatically understates the core e-commerce value and overstates the cloud discount.

### Mistake 4: Ignoring the Net Cash Pile

Alibaba holds approximately $60–70 billion in net cash, which represents approximately 30–35% of its market capitalization. Analysts who value Alibaba solely on an earnings multiple without adding the cash balance systematically understate intrinsic value.

Frequently Asked Questions About Alibaba Valuation
--------------------------------------------------

### What is Alibaba’s market cap in 2025?

Alibaba Group Holding (NYSE: BABA / HKG: 9988) has a market capitalization of approximately $200–220 billion as of early 2025 — representing approximately 78% decline from its 2020 peak of ~$900 billion. The decline reflects Chinese regulatory crackdowns, geopolitical risk discount, competitive pressure from PDD Holdings, and slower growth in China’s e-commerce market.

### Is Alibaba stock undervalued in 2025?

At approximately 12–14x forward earnings and 10x EBITDA, Alibaba appears meaningfully undervalued relative to US tech peers like Amazon (25x) and Google (22x). However, the discount is partly justified: Alibaba carries a China regulatory risk premium, a VIE legal structure discount, and increased competitive pressure in its core market. Our base-case DCF implies intrinsic value of approximately $90–110 per ADR, suggesting 15–30% upside from early 2025 prices if regulatory clarity continues.

### What is Alibaba’s ROIC?

Alibaba’s return on invested capital is approximately 10–12% in FY2025. The core China commerce segment generates substantially higher ROIC (~25–30%), while cloud computing and international commerce are in investment phases with lower near-term returns. ROIC marginally exceeds WACC (~10.5%), confirming Alibaba is creating economic value — but the thin spread requires close monitoring.

### What happened to Alibaba after Jack Ma’s speech?

Jack Ma’s October 2020 speech criticizing China’s banking regulators triggered a government crackdown that resulted in the cancellation of Ant Group’s $37 billion IPO (then the world’s largest planned IPO), a record RMB 18.2 billion antitrust fine on Alibaba, and a sweeping regulatory review of China’s tech sector. Jack Ma largely retreated from public life and Alibaba’s stock fell approximately 70% from its 2020 peak to its 2022 trough.

### How does Alibaba compare to Amazon?

Alibaba’s GMV historically exceeded Amazon’s, but Amazon’s revenue ($600B+) is substantially larger because Amazon uses a direct sales model while Alibaba primarily earns marketplace commissions. Amazon’s AWS cloud is significantly larger and more profitable than Alibaba Cloud. Amazon trades at ~25x forward earnings with US-market risk; Alibaba at ~13x forward earnings with China-market risk. On an adjusted ROIC basis, the two businesses are broadly comparable in core operating quality.
