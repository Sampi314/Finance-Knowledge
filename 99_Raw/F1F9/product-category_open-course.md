Our Courses
-----------

What will I learn?
------------------

You will learn what it means to be a productive financial modeller and how this is different from being an Excel expert. Our courses follow the same best practice principles and the FAST Standard regardless of whether they are entry-level or advanced. Furthermore, the techniques that you learn on entry-level courses continue to be applied even when what being modelled is highly complex.

You will learn that acquiring clarity on how to model a particular cost (for example) improves your understanding of its calculation, how it might be funded and how it is accounted for. Likewise with sector knowledge: you will understand the difference between what is being modelled (“conceptual modelling”) and how it is being modelled (“spreadsheet engineering”). You will also understand the bridge that exists between conceptual modelling and spreadsheet solutions.

Most important, you will learn that it is possible to build models that others find easy-to-read and easy-to-navigate even where the case study is highly complex. Keeping a high complex model transparent is a great skill – and one well worth acquiring.

Who Should Attend?
------------------

We welcome course participants from a wide range of backgrounds and sectors, including accountants, bankers, engineers and lawyers. Some experience of financial modelling – even if self-taught – is often an advantage since it is then possible to compare current practice against best practice. But we also work with people that have no financial modelling experience (but know that they need it to excel in the role) and highly experienced financial modellers. Regardless of experience, the quality and productivity of modelling will improve through the introduction of a standardised approach to model design and construction.
