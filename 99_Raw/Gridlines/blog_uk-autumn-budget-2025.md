UK Autumn budget 2025: Key takeaways for intrastructure and project finance
===========================================================================

*   November 27, 2025

**By Morag Loader, Director of Accounting and Tax**

On 26 November 2025 the Chancellor of the Exchequer, Rachel Reeves, delivered her second Autumn Budget, one of the most widely anticipated and discussed ahead of the official delivery in recent years, with a number of measures trailed in advance.  Full details of the Autumn Budget 2025 measures can be found at [Budget 2025 document – GOV.UK](https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/budget-2025-document "https://www.gov.uk/government/publications/budget-2025-document")
   

### Overview

The Chancellor’s main change for companies in the project finance infrastructure sector was a reduction in the rate of tax relief on capital expenditure. The government stated that its intention is to make targeted changes to the capital allowances treatment of main rate assets in a way that preserves the value of relief for future investment. More detail below. 

### Capital Allowances

The Chancellor is reducing the writing down allowance on the main rate capital allowances pool from 18% to 14% from April 2026, meaning that it will take longer for businesses to receive tax relief for their capital costs. However, it may enable them to use any carried forwards losses more swiftly.  A new 40% First Year Allowance (FYA) was announced, but as many companies already take advantage of the Full Expensing regime, this is unlikely to be widely used in the corporate sector. The new 40% FYA will be available for most expenditure on assets for leasing and expenditure by unincorporated businesses from 1 January 2026.  As unincorporated businesses are ineligible for Full Expensing, there may be a take up in that sector. Cars, second-hand assets and assets for leasing overseas will not be eligible for the new allowance.

### UK Listing Relief

Taking effect from 27 November 2025, transfers of a company’s securities will be eligible for relief from the 0.5% Stamp Duty Reserve Tax charge for three years from the point the company lists on a UK regulated market. The City has been campaigning for this measure to encourage listings in the UK and so will welcome the announcement.

### Oil and Gas Price Mechanism/Energy Profits Levy

The Chancellor has confirmed that the temporary Energy Profits Levy (EPL) will be replaced by the permanent Oil and Gas Profits Mechanism (OGPM). The OGPM will be a revenue-based mechanism which only operates in times of high prices and will replace the EPL when it ends in 2030, or earlier if the EPL price floor triggers. The rate will be 35% with thresholds of $90/barrel (oil) and 90p/therm (gas). This provides some certainty for energy companies regarding the direction of travel. The government is also announcing legislation that confirms that no payments can arise under a Decommissioning Relief Deed in relation to the Energy Profits Levy, which excludes tax relief on decommissioning expenditure.

### Landfill Tax 

The government will not be proceeding with converging the two rates of Landfill Tax, as consulted on earlier this year. Instead, it will increase the standard rate of Landfill Tax by RPI and the lower rate by the cash amount of the increase in the standard rate, maintaining the differential between the two rates in cash terms. The government will also retain the tax exemption for backfilling quarries to ensure that housebuilders and the construction sector continue to have access to a low-cost alternative to landfill. 

### Transfer Pricing

The government is planning to legislate in Finance Bill 2025-26 to simplify the taxation of related party transactions, non-resident companies trading in the UK, and profits diverted from the UK, for chargeable periods beginning on or after 1 January 2026. This reform has been discussed for some time and is likely to be a welcome simplification for companies. 

### Climate Change Levy (CCL)

The main rates of CCL for gas, electricity and solid fuels will be uprated in line with RPI from 1 April 2027. The main rate for liquefied petroleum gas will continue to be frozen. The reduced rates will remain at an unchanged fixed percentage of the main rates.

### Business Rates

From 1 April 2026, business rates bills in England will be updated to reflect changes in property values since the last revaluation in 2023. As a result of the revaluation, the small business multiplier will decrease from 49.9p in 2025-26 to 43.2p in 2026-27, and the standard multiplier will decrease from 55.5p to 48p. The government is introducing a higher rate on the most valuable properties such as warehouses used by large online retailers – those with rateable values of £500,000 and above, representing around 1% of properties. This higher rate is being set at 2.8p above the national standard multiplier. The high-value multiplier will be 50.8p in 2026-27.

### Electric Vehicles (EV)

The government is introducing a 10-year 100% business rates relief for eligible EV charge points and EV-only forecourts, to ensure that they face no business rates liability.  Accompanying this, the government will extend the 100% first year allowances for zero emission cars and EV charge point infrastructure by a further year.

### Customs Duty

In its efforts to support fair competition between high street businesses and online retailers following the recent rapid growth in low value imports, the government will remove the customs duty relief for low value imports – goods valued at £135 or less – and reform the way these goods are declared into the UK. From March 2029 at the latest, low value imports will be charged customs duty just as goods imported in bulk already are and new import arrangements will be put in place. 

### 10 – Year Infrastructure Strategy

The government published a 10-Year Infrastructure Strategy in June 2025 which sets out plans to use private finance to support its infrastructure ambitions.  A new Public-Private Partnerships (PPP) model is being developed by the National Infrastructure and Service Transformation Authority, which will build on lessons learnt from past and current models and harness private sector expertise. Privately financed projects and programmes – including PPPs – will also be considered for projects that decarbonise the public sector estate.

### Tax Certainty for Investment Projects

The Chancellor has confirmed a new service to provide major investment projects with advance tax certainty, committed to in the Corporate Tax Roadmap. It will be launched in July 2026.

### Corporate Interest Restriction (CIR) Technical Update 

The government will legislate in Finance Bill 2025-26 to make technical amendments to CIR in respect of relief for certain capital expenditure. The changes take effect for periods ending on or after 31 December 2021. Further details are awaited.

### Our View

In this Budget companies did not suffer the tax increases that some were expecting. The main change was a reduction in the rate at which tax relief can be claimed on the main rate pool for capital allowances, which is a timing difference rather than a permanent tax increase, and may enable some companies to use more brought forward losses more quickly. As Full Expensing relief is already used by many companies the effect is more one for legacy capital assets and for those companies who do not use Full Expensing due to the interaction with the tax loss regime. We will wait to see whether business confidence is boosted by the measures overall.
