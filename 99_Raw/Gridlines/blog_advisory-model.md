A legacy advisory model, increasingly out of step
-------------------------------------------------

Every industry has adapted to the shift: remote teams, global talent pools, faster, leaner execution. And yet, most of the financial advisory world, including financial modelling consultants, still operate on a structure designed for face-to-face meetings and local delivery.

At a glance, the model looks global. But underneath, it functions more like a network of semi-autonomous regional firms stitched together by brand. That has real consequences for how advice is delivered.

Teams are typically staffed from the local office, not the global talent pool. Expertise is influenced based on proximity, not always fit. Cross-border collaboration is possible, but rarely seamless or incentivised.

This isn’t about capability. It is about structure.

Clients now operate across time zones. Communication is asynchronous. Talent is deployed based on skill, not geography. But advisory firms often still deliver in a model designed for 2012.

And that creates friction: in responsiveness, in team fit, and in the speed at which decisions move.

The firms aren’t broken. Their models just reflect a different set of constraints. Constraints that are disappearing fast.

Some firms are adapting. Others are watching and waiting. But the direction is clear.

Why the shift in the advisory model matters
-------------------------------------------

This shift isn’t just about delivery structure. It’s subtle but it’s gathering momentum: a change in the nature of value in [project finance advisory](https://www.gridlines.com/financial-advisory/)
 itself.

Where clients once paid a premium for bespoke thinking, proprietary knowledge, or a blue-chip logo, the value now sits elsewhere:

### 1\. From advice to execution in the advisory model

For a long time, project finance advisory was built on bespoke problem-solving. Every deal felt unique. Every transaction demanded a tailored solution, often crafted from scratch.

That’s changing. Deals are becoming more standardised. More repeatable. The capital structure looks familiar. The risks are known. The structures are tried and tested.

Clients aren’t looking for theoretical advice about how a project could be structured. They already know. They’re looking for advisors who can execute:

– Build the model quickly and accurately.  
– Review and sign off without drama.  
– Get the fundraising done.  
– Keep the process moving at pace.

The premium is shifting away from thinking about the problem to delivering the solution. From mystique to clarity: clients can look through the brand to the individuals doing the work. Credibility now sits with the team, not the logo.

### 2\. How AI and information flow have transformed the advisory model

For decades, advisory firms traded (consciously or not) on an imbalance of information. They knew things clients didn’t. They’d seen more deals. They held the war stories and the playbooks.

But the way information moves has completely [changed financial modelling.](https://www.gridlines.com/blog/ai-in-financial-modelling/)

AI has flattened the information gap. It’s accelerated access to knowledge, patterns, and benchmarks.

But even before AI, the direction was clear. Data flows faster. Networks are wider. The days when knowledge was something proprietary, something hidden behind firm walls, are fading. You can’t sell what everyone already knows.

The value now is in judgement, execution quality, and speed. Not in hoarding knowledge.  
What matters is execution, experience, and the ability to move. The firms that win will be the ones that can match the pace, transparency and structure of the clients they serve.

### 3\. The diminishing power of brand within the advisory model

Historically, the firm name on the front of the report was a kind of insurance policy. A proxy for quality. A way for decision-makers to de-risk procurement:

“If something goes wrong, no one will question why we hired them.”

But that’s weakening.

Why? Because clients now have full visibility of who they’re actually hiring. LinkedIn has quietly disrupted the brand premium. You can check the CVs. You can see the deal experience. You can look straight through the badge on the door to the people behind it.

Clients are increasingly asking:

– Who’s actually doing the work?  
– Do they have the skills? The judgement? The experience?

And if the answer is yes, the logo matters a lot less than it used to.

### 4\. The value in advisory has shifted.

The truth is simple:

– Execution matters more than theatre.  
– Judgement matters more than proximity.  
– Transparency matters more than mystique.

Clients don’t need gatekeepers. They don’t need advisors to interpret the world for them. They’re perfectly capable of doing that themselves.

What they need are partners who can execute cleanly, reliably, and fast — without the friction of old models, old pricing structures, or old ways of thinking about “advice.”

### What is the traditional advisory model?

The traditional advisory model is structured around regional offices with face-to-face meetings and local delivery, often resulting in fragmented teams working independently and expertise delivered based on proximity rather than skill fit.

### Why is the traditional advisory model becoming outdated?

Because client needs have changed. They now operate globally, work asynchronously across time zones, and require faster execution with direct access to skilled teams, which the regional model struggles to provide.

### How does the modern advisory model work?

A modern model is global-first, integrated, skill-based rather than geography-based, modular, and designed to deliver consistent, seamless service regardless of location.

### What role do technology and AI play in the advisory model?

AI accelerates access to data, knowledge, and best practices, flattening the information gap between firms and clients, shifting value toward execution speed and quality rather than proprietary knowledge.

### How does the advisory model impact client outcomes?

A streamlined advisory model reduces friction, improves responsiveness, leverages the best expertise worldwide, and ultimately enables faster, more reliable execution of financial advisory services.
