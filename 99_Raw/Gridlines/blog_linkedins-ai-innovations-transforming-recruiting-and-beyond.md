AI // LinkedIn Goes big on AI
-----------------------------

LinkedIn has upgraded its AI capabilities, introducing new features across its product line:

*   Recruiter has added generative AI to enhance search efficiency for recruitment professionals;
*   LinkedIn Learning incorporates an AI-driven “learning coach” offering tailored to learners;
*   Accelerate is a new AI tool for marketers, aiming to streamline campaign processes.

[Read more](https://techcrunch.com/2023/10/03/linkedin-goes-big-on-new-ai-tools-for-learning-recruitment-marketing-and-sales-powered-by-openai/)

Resources for modellers
-----------------------

[**Business Analysis Lifecycle**](https://www.financialmodellinghandbook.org/financial-modelling-is-part-of-a-bigger-process/)
. Building a spreadsheet model is always a means to an end. The purpose of any model is to generate insights to help answer critical business questions. The model is a laboratory. It’s a place where we can test hypotheses about the business. This is why I love modelling. Done well, it can help us explore real-world business dynamics and ultimately make better decisions. [This chapter](https://www.financialmodellinghandbook.org/financial-modelling-is-part-of-a-bigger-process/)
 of the Financial Modelling Handbook explains how modelling fits into the wider business analysis process.

[**Financial modeller’s corner podcast**](https://open.spotify.com/show/1AD5DxVXaTQGwGJGFS6cYq)
. Paul Barnhurst is hosting a new podcast featuring discussions with Financial Modellers from across the globe. It’s a well produced and useful resource for modellers.

[**Modelling best practices guide**](https://www.linkedin.com/posts/fminstitute_a-guide-to-best-practices-structural-financial-activity-7114956415227592704-k4cx?utm_source=share&utm_medium=member_desktop)
. The Financial Modelling Institute has published a useful paper on modelling best practices around structure, format and formula tips. “Best practice” can sometime seem a bit of an overused term in modelling since it means different things to different people. This contains some useful pointers nonetheless.
