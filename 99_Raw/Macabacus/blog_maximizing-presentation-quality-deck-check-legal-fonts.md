Precision Matters: Over 200 Automated Checks
--------------------------------------------

When time is tight, and expectations are high, the last thing you need is a last-minute formatting mistake. Deck Check runs over 200 automated checks, helping you spot and fix inconsistencies and issues easily.

Some of the highest-impact checks include:

*   **Bullet Punctuation Consistency**: No more mixing periods, dashes, or inconsistent spacing in bullet points.
*   **Standardized Abbreviations**: Automatically enforce firm-preferred conventions (because “MM” and “M” matter more than you think).
*   **Slide Master Compliance**: Ensures every slide adheres to firm-approved templates and layouts.
*   **Brand & Formatting Compliance**: Flag off-brand colors, rogue fonts, and misaligned shapes before they derail your deck’s credibility.

**Legal Fonts: Approved fonts, every time**
-------------------------------------------

With Legal Fonts in Deck Check, font compliance is assured from the start. This feature automatically identifies and corrects any fonts that deviate from your firm’s guidelines, eliminating manual checks and last-minute slide fixes. Because client-facing materials must always be polished and professional, Legal Fonts ensures every slide aligns with your firm’s strict branding and compliance standards.

**Underrated Features That Save Hours**
---------------------------------------

Beyond the headline checks, Deck Check includes other powerful tools designed to help finance & banking teams maintain accuracy and consistency across presentations.

*   **Reformat View**: Like find-and-replace, but for formatting—adjust font styles, spacing, text colors, and more in one place.
*   **Image Distortion Detection**: Ensures logos, charts, and visuals maintain proper scaling.
*   **Misaligned Shapes Detection**: Flags the slightest inconsistencies in shape alignment for a sharper, cleaner layout.

These capabilities are more than mere conveniences—they guarantee each deck is as refined as the insights behind it.
