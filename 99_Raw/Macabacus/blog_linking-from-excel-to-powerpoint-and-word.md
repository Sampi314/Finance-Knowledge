### **Method #1 - Paste as an Enhanced Metafile Picture**

![Linking from Excel to PowerPoint and Word - Method #1 - Paste as an Enhanced Metafile Picture](https://macabacus.com/assets/2023/04/linking-from-excel-to-powerpoint-and-word.png) Many analysts will Paste Special natively (Ctrl + Alt + V) as an Enhanced Metafile Picture. This method, while simple and readily available, does not create a dynamic link, and exposes the analyst to several key drawbacks.

1\. Every time an update is made to the financial model, the analyst will have to replace the image file with a fresh Copy-Paste. This can be incredibly time-consuming, as updates are often required, and exposes the deliverable to potential errors.

![Example of a pasted Enhanced Metafile Picture from Excel](https://macabacus.com/assets/2023/04/linking-from-excel-to-powerpoint-and-word-1.png)

2\. The above range of cells is an example of a pasted Enhanced Metafile Picture from Excel. We can see pretty clearly that the formatting is quite banged up:

*   The Excel comment indicator is showing.
*   The print-preview border is showing.
*   The fonts haven鈥檛 been recolored to black.
*   The gridlines are showing.

Overall, this is a poor option both due to its lack of dynamic linking, and poor formatting (which is likely to compromise brand compliance).

### **Method #2 - Paste as an Embedded WorkSheet Object**

![Method #2 - Paste as an Embedded WorkSheet Object](https://macabacus.com/assets/2023/04/linking-from-excel-to-powerpoint-and-word-2.png) This option is Microsoft鈥檚 attempt at linking your source Excel file to PowerPoint / Word. Unfortunately, those who have used these links before know that:

1\. These links are unreliable at best. For example, if you insert any rows or columns, or if the address of your cells (say B1: G9) changes in any way, your links are likely to be broken.聽

2\. Every time you make a paste in this manner, Excel actually embeds a full copy of your workbook into your destination document, which is how you end up with bloated file sizes in PowerPoint and Word.

![Example of a pasted Enhanced Metafile Picture from Excel](https://macabacus.com/assets/2023/04/linking-from-excel-to-powerpoint-and-word-3.png)
