**Understanding Radar Charts in Finance**
-----------------------------------------

A radar chart shows data with three or more variables starting from the same point. In the context of financial analysis, radar charts are used to compare the performance of different investment portfolios across multiple KPIs. Each KPI has an axis that goes from the chart’s center outward. The portfolios’ data points are plotted and connected to form shapes.

Benefits of using radar charts for portfolio comparison include:

1.  Visual comparison of multiple portfolios across several KPIs simultaneously
2.  Identification of strengths and weaknesses of each portfolio
3.  Facilitation of data-driven decision making
4.  Clear communication of complex data to stakeholders

**Preparing Your Financial Data**
---------------------------------

To demonstrate how to create a radar chart in Excel for investment analysis, we will use an example dataset that compares three investment portfolios (Portfolio A, B, and C) across six key performance indicators:

1.  **Return on Investment (ROI):** Measures the gain or loss generated on an investment relative to the amount of money invested.
2.  **Volatility:** Measures the degree of variation of trading prices series over time. 
3.  **Liquidity:** Indicates how quickly an investment can be sold in the market without affecting its price.
4.  **Diversification:** Reflects the level of variety in the investment holdings to reduce risk.
5.  **Tax Efficiency:** Indicates how much of the investment returns are kept after taxes.
6.  **Cost:** Represents the expenses associated with managing and operating the investment portfolio.

The dataset values range from 0 to 100, where 100 represents the highest or best performance in that category, and 0 represents the lowest.

| **Criteria** | **Portfolio A** | **Portfolio B** | **Portfolio C** |

| Volatility | 30  | 40  | 50  |
| Liquidity | 70  | 60  | 80  |
| Diversification | 90  | 60  | 70  |
| Tax Efficiency | 75  | 85  | 65  |
| Cost | 55  | 45  | 40  |

To prepare this data in Excel for creating a radar chart, organize it in a table format with the KPIs in the first column and the corresponding values for each portfolio in the subsequent columns.

**Step-by-Step Guide to Creating a Radar Chart**
------------------------------------------------

Here’s a step-by-step on how to use Excel to create a radar chart:

### **Step 1: Enter Your Data in Excel**

1\. Open Excel and pick a worksheet.

2\. Start in cell A2 and put the KPIs in the first column.

3\. Put portfolio names in the first row, starting at B1.

4\. Enter each portfolio’s values under their names.

![create radar chart excel](https://macabacus.com/assets/2024/03/create-radar-chart-excel-1.png)

### **Step 2: Selecting the Data for Your Radar Chart**

1\. Select your data and KPIs, like A1 to D7 here.

**Common Mistakes to Avoid in Financial Radar Charts**
------------------------------------------------------

When creating radar charts for financial data, be mindful of the following common mistakes:

1.  Overcrowding the chart with too many portfolios or KPIs, which can make it difficult to interpret
2.  Using an inappropriate scale that fails to capture the differences between portfolios effectively
3.  Neglecting to customize the chart title, axis labels, and legend, leading to confusion
4.  Choosing colors that are not colorblind-friendly or do not maintain a professional appearance
