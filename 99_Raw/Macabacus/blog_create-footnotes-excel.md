How to Create Footnotes in Excel
================================

![How to Create Footnotes in Excel](https://macabacus.com/assets/2024/04/create-footnotes-excel.jpg)

Footnotes are important tools for a financial analyst or an investment banker while making Excel spreadsheets. They serve as a link, adding references and making sure the data presented is precise and properly cited. Although Excel does not provide a footnote function, there are indeed a few helpful methods for including footnotes.

In this comprehensive guide, we will explore three different strategies for creating footnotes in Excel: using comments, adding a separate column, and including hyperlinks. By using different techniques, you can provide more clarity in your data in order to improve the decision-making and analysis process.

**Importance of Footnotes**
---------------------------

Footnotes are not only used to clarify any inconsistencies in data and calculations but also to provide helpful context or citations within your Excel spreadsheets. They can help to:

### **Clarify Assumptions**

Footnotes can help in describing the modeling assumptions and methodology used in the financial model. By explicitly demonstrating the rationale behind your calculations and forecasts, you can enable users to get a better insight into your assumptions and results, allowing them to corroborate or challenge your assumptions as it is deemed fit.

### **Provide Context**

Footnotes play an important role in emphasizing discussions regarding certain data points or mathematical calculations, which can help users understand the value of the information provided. For instance, a footnote may help explain an unusually high or low value in the data set. (The outliers may be a result of a one-time event or a change in accounting methodology).

### **Reference Sources**

Footnotes can point to the sources used for data or information in your Excel spreadsheet, and the data can be cross-checked to prove its accuracy and reliability. Through the proper citation of others, your work will demonstrate more authority, and thus, people will be able to use it and reproduce it reliably.

### **Enhance Communication**

Footnotes will give you an edge in modeling clarity and professionalism. They play a vital role in the precise interpretation of financial models by the team members, including stakeholders. You can clarify your instructions by providing further explanation and context. It will help prevent users from making mistakes, reducing the possibility of misinterpretation and confusion.

**Creating and Using Footnotes in Excel**
-----------------------------------------

Below are different ways to create and use footnotes in Excel:

### **Method 1: The Use of Footnotes as Comments**

An easy way to write your footnotes for your Excel spreadsheet is by using the comment tool. This way, any information can be added to a single cell or a cluster of cells, and the way the spreadsheet is organized will remain intact. Here’s a step-by-step guide on how to use comments as footnotes: Below is a simple procedure on how to incorporate comments within your footnotes: You may also extend your sentence to include a brief explanation:

**Step 1**: Click the cell you want to add a footnote.

![create footnotes excel](https://macabacus.com/assets/2024/04/create-footnotes-excel-1.png)

**Step 2**: Right-click the cell and choose ‘New Note’ from the context menu. As an alternative, you can utilize the keyboard combination Shift+F2 or simply press the keys on your own.

![create footnotes excel](https://macabacus.com/assets/2024/04/create-footnotes-excel-2.png)

**Step 3**: Selecting a cell will immediately display a comment field to the right of the cell. Type your footnote text into this box.

![create footnotes excel](https://macabacus.com/assets/2024/04/create-footnotes-excel-3.png)

**Step 4**: To confirm that a cell has a footnote, you may represent it by a small superscript number or symbol next to the column. For example, a key press would result in displaying superscript ‘1’ if you type ‘^1’ (without the quotes). Then, note the same number or symbol in the introduction of your document to associate it with your footnote.

![create footnotes excel](https://macabacus.com/assets/2024/04/create-footnotes-excel-6.png)

Here’s how you can add superscript text to your footnote:

Select the word or letter you want to create a superscript, then right-click and select ‘Format Comment’.

![create footnotes excel](https://macabacus.com/assets/2024/04/create-footnotes-excel-4.png)

A dialog box for ‘Format Comment’ will show up. Select ‘Superscript’ under ‘Effects’, then click ‘OK’.

![create footnotes excel](https://macabacus.com/assets/2024/04/create-footnotes-excel-5.png)

Commenting as footnotes provides a number of benefits. First, the footnote is linked directly to the appropriate cell, which allows you to find and read it easily. Second, comments do not affect the structure of your spreadsheet or the calculations that you already made. Another point is that the comments can be easily viewed or hidden on the Review tab using the ‘Show All Comments; button to help you regulate the visibility of your footnotes.

On the one hand, there are some drawbacks to using comments as footnotes. When dealing with a high volume of footnotes, the comment boxes of your spreadsheet can pack up and make navigation a little harder. Additionally, if you need to print your spreadsheet, the comments won’t get printed unless you check the Print/Send comments option. You will need to edit print settings to include comments.

### **Method 2: Adding a Separate Column for Footnotes**

An alternate and feasible technique that you can apply in order to add a footnote in your Excel spreadsheet is to use the column at one side as text to store the footnote. Such a technique offers you information in a concise layout. The footnotes will be easy to read, without resorting to comment boxes. Here’s how you can create footnotes using a separate column:

**Step 1**: Add a new column next to the cell in which you want to insert footnotes. You can do so by right-clicking anywhere at the appropriate location to the right of the header column and selecting ‘Insert’.

![create footnotes excel](https://macabacus.com/assets/2024/04/create-footnotes-excel-7.png)

**Step 2**: You can simply type the footnote text in the cells of the column. A footnote must be added in the same row of the item it should point to from the original column.

![create footnotes excel](https://macabacus.com/assets/2024/04/create-footnotes-excel-8.png)

**Step 3**: To help you easily identify the footnote column from the rest of the data, you can use a different color. To provide more contrast, you can also try variations in font style or background color.

![create footnotes excel](https://macabacus.com/assets/2024/04/create-footnotes-excel-9.jpg)

**Step 4**: To cross-reference the footnotes easily, include the native row numbers in the footnote text. For example, if you type a footnote in cell B3 that corresponds to the data in cell A3, you can include “A3:” at the beginning of your footnote text.

![create footnotes excel](https://macabacus.com/assets/2024/04/create-footnotes-excel-10.png)

Footnotes saved in a different column can be beneficial in a number of ways. It prevents you from messing up your legends and notes. Also, notes set up in a separate column will show up after printing the worksheet, meaning that there is no need to omit important details.

On the one hand, there can be drawbacks. For long footnotes, you would be compelled to make your columns wider in order to fit large volumes of text. Also, if you have too many footnotes, the footnote column can become longer.

### **Method 3: Using Hyperlinks**

Another approach to inserting footnotes in Excel is to use hyperlinks. Through hyperlinks, you can link a cell to another sheet or cell in your worksheet. Here’s how you can use hyperlinks to create footnotes:

**Step 1**: Choose the cell where you want to add a footnote.

![create footnotes excel](https://macabacus.com/assets/2024/04/create-footnotes-excel-11.jpg)

**Step 2**: Right-click on the cell and choose ‘Link’ from the contextual menu. You can also use the ‘Alt+Ctrl+K’.

![create footnotes excel](https://macabacus.com/assets/2024/04/create-footnotes-excel-12.jpg)

**Step 3**: In the ‘Insert Hyperlink’ dialog box, select ‘Place in This Document’ from the options on the left side.

![create footnotes excel](https://macabacus.com/assets/2024/04/create-footnotes-excel-13.jpg)

**Step 4**: In the ‘Type the cell reference’ box, enter the cell or an array of cells containing the footnote text. For instance, if the text of footnotes is in cell A1, then you would type ‘A1’. Click ‘OK’ to create the hyperlink.

![create footnotes excel](https://macabacus.com/assets/2024/04/create-footnotes-excel-14.jpg)

**Step 5**: You will now see that the cell with the footnote has a hyperlink.

![create footnotes excel](https://macabacus.com/assets/2024/04/create-footnotes-excel-15.jpg)

Making hyperlinks for footnotes provides a number of benefits. It keeps your spreadsheet clutter-free, as footnotes are stored in a separate spreadsheet. It can be very useful if your worksheet includes several footnotes or the footnote text is very long. Furthermore, hyperlinks can simplify the search and lead straight to your footnote text after you click on them.

While utilizing hyperlinks offers some advantages in the case of footnotes, it also comes with some limitations. If you shift or delete the cells containing your footnote text, the hyperlinks will break and you will need to update all the hyperlinks manually.

**Best Practices for Using Footnotes in Excel**
-----------------------------------------------

Regardless of the method you choose for creating footnotes in your Excel spreadsheets, there are several best practices to keep in mind:

*   **Be Consistent:** Use only one method to show notes using footnotes and be as consistent as possible. It will provide users with an intuitive way to navigate through and understand your footnotes.
*   **Keep Footnotes Concise:** Footnotes should provide context or explanation, but they should not be too long and detailed. Stick to the most significant information by making your footnotes brief and to the point.
*   **Use Clear Identifiers:** Make sure clear references, such as row numbers or cell names, are used to mark the related data with footnotes or comment cells. It will allow users to readily find the data that they need.
*   **Maintain Formatting:** To maintain consistent formatting, if you use a separate column or sheet for your footnotes, make sure to use a font size, color, and style consistently. It will be useful to set the footnotes apart from the main data.
*   **Update Regularly:** Be mindful to update your footnotes in your spreadsheet when making any necessary changes. It is an important consideration when using hyperlinks since incorrect cell references in the linked cells may cause broken links.
