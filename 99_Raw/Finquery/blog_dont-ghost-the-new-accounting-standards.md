Don’t Ghost The New Accounting Standards
========================================

by [FinQuery](https://finquery.com/author/leasequery/ "Posts by FinQuery")
 | Oct 17, 2022

Are you afraid of the looming accounting standards? [ASC 842](https://finquery.com/blog/asc-842-summary-new-lease-accounting-standards/)
, [GASB 87](https://finquery.com/blog/gasb-87-explained-example-new-lease-accounting/)
, and [GASB 96](https://finquery.com/blog/gasb-96-sbita-accounting-explained/)
 may be frightening, but ghosting them isn’t the right answer. At some point, you have to approach compliance head on. While you should beware of the repercussions of noncompliance, there is no need to panic!

With the right resources, you won’t feel the need to run away from your transition. Use the tips below to help face your compliance fears.

Compliance deadlines
--------------------

Feeling [spooked by the new guidance](https://finquery.com/blog/dont-let-new-lease-accounting-standards-spook-you/)
 is a common reaction. LeaseQuery will walk you through the dark and into the light. The first step is making sure you are aware of the correct compliance date for the standard you’re adopting. Pick your poison below.

### ASC 842

[ASC 842](https://finquery.com/blog/asc-842-summary-new-lease-accounting-standards/)
 is the new [lease accounting](https://finquery.com/blog/lease-accounting-explained/)
 standard for private and public companies reporting under US GAAP. The standard is effective for public and private entities with fiscal years beginning after December 15, 2018, and December15, 2021, respectively. Prior guidance was [ASC 840](https://finquery.com/blog/asc-840-vs-asc-842-old-lease-accounting-standard-vs-new/)
.

### GASB 87

[GASB 87](https://finquery.com/blog/gasb-87-explained-example-new-lease-accounting/)
 is the new [lease accounting standard for state and local governmental organizations](https://finquery.com/blog/gasb-government-accounting-standards-explained/)
. The standard is effective for all fiscal years beginning subsequent to June 15, 2021. Prior guidance includes GASB 13 and [GASB 62](https://finquery.com/blog/gasb-62-leases-to-gasb-87-eight-tips-to-ease-the-transition/)
.

### GASB 96

[GASB 96](https://finquery.com/blog/gasb-96-sbita-accounting-explained/)
 addresses the accounting treatment and financial reporting for [subscription-based information technology arrangements (SBITAs)](https://finquery.com/blog/what-is-a-sbita/)
. The standard is effective for fiscal years beginning after June 15, 2022. There is no prior guidance for these types of contracts.
