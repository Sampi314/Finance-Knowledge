### What is a lease liability?

Before we get into the calculations, it’s important to understand the term lease liability which is defined as the [present value of remaining lease payments](https://finquery.com/blog/how-to-calculate-present-value-of-future-lease-payments-in-excel/)
. Calculating the lease liability is the first step in accounting for a lease under ASC 842 and IFRS 16. The [ROU (right-of-use) asset, is then measured from the lease liability](https://finquery.com/blog/right-of-use-asset-lease-liability-asc-842-ifrs-16-gasb-87/)
, and represents the [lessee’s](https://finquery.com/blog/lessee-vs-lessor-differences-accounting-and-more-explained/)
 right to use the leased asset over the [lease term](https://finquery.com/blog/determining-correct-dates-lease-term-asc-842/)
.
