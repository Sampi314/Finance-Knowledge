SMB Investment Glossary
=======================

Essential terms and definitions for small business acquisition, search funds, and entrepreneurship through acquisition (ETA).

Legal and Regulatory
--------------------

[### Accredited Investor\
\
An individual or entity that meets SEC-defined financial thresholds ($1M+ net worth or $200K+ income) and is legally permitted to invest in unregistered securities like private equity, hedge funds, and search funds.\
\
Also known as: Qualified Investor, Sophisticated Investor, High Net Worth Individual](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/accredited-investor)

Strategy
--------

[### Acquisition Thesis\
\
A strategic framework defining what types of businesses to acquire, including industry focus, size criteria, and value creation plan.](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/acquisition-thesis)

Financial Metrics
-----------------

[### Add-Back\
\
An expense added back to reported earnings to calculate adjusted EBITDA, typically representing one-time costs or owner-related expenses that won't continue post-acquisition.](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/add-back)
[### EBITDA\
\
Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization - the primary profitability metric used to value small businesses, typically resulting in 3-5x EBITDA purchase price multiples.\
\
Also known as: Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization, Operating Cash Flow, Seller's Discretionary Earnings](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/ebitda)
[### Seller's Discretionary Earnings (SDE)\
\
A cash flow metric used to value small businesses, calculated as net income plus owner compensation, benefits, and discretionary expenses.](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/sde)
[### Working Capital\
\
The operating liquidity of a business, calculated as current assets minus current liabilities. In M&A, working capital targets determine post-closing adjustments.](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/working-capital)

Deal Structures
---------------

[### Asset Purchase vs Stock Purchase\
\
Two fundamental M&A structures: asset purchases buy specific business assets/liabilities while stock purchases acquire entire corporate entities, each with distinct legal, tax, and risk implications.\
\
Also known as: Asset Deal vs Stock Deal, Asset Acquisition vs Equity Acquisition](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/asset-purchase)
[### Earnout\
\
A contingent payment structure where sellers receive additional consideration based on future business performance, typically used to bridge valuation gaps and share risk between buyer and seller.\
\
Also known as: Contingent Consideration, Performance-Based Payment, Earnout Provision](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/earnout)

Investment Structures
---------------------

[### Bolt-on Acquisition\
\
A smaller, complementary acquisition added to an existing platform company to expand capabilities, geography, or product offerings.](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/bolt-on)
[### Co-Investment\
\
Direct investment alongside a search fund or private equity manager in a specific portfolio company, typically offering lower fees and higher alignment than traditional fund structures.\
\
Also known as: Side-by-Side Investment, Direct Co-Investment, Parallel Investment](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/co-investment)
[### Platform Company\
\
An initial acquisition that serves as the foundation for a buy-and-build strategy, with bolt-on acquisitions added to create scale and value.](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/platform-company)
[### Search Fund\
\
An investment vehicle where a searcher raises capital to find, acquire, and operate a single small business, typically generating 3-5x returns over 5-7 years.\
\
Also known as: Traditional Search Fund, Funded Search](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/search-fund)
[### Self-Funded Search\
\
A search model where entrepreneurs fund their own search for an acquisition target, often using personal savings or debt, rather than raising capital from investors.](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/self-funded-search)

People & Roles
--------------

[### Business Broker\
\
A professional intermediary who facilitates the sale of small businesses, typically earning 8-12% commission on completed transactions.](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/business-broker)
[### Searcher\
\
An entrepreneur who systematically searches for, acquires, and operates a small business, typically through a search fund or self-funded search model.](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/searcher)

Fund Operations
---------------

[### Capital Call\
\
A formal request from a fund manager for investors to contribute a portion of their committed capital, typically issued when investments are made.](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/capital-call)
[### Dry Powder\
\
Uncommitted capital available for investment, representing the funds that private equity firms have raised but not yet deployed.](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/dry-powder)
[### Vintage Year\
\
The year a private equity fund makes its first investment or closes on capital, used to benchmark fund performance against peers from the same time period.](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/vintage-year)

Fund Economics
--------------

[### Carried Interest (Carry)\
\
The share of investment profits paid to fund managers as performance compensation, typically 20% of profits above a preferred return hurdle.](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/carried-interest)
[### Illiquidity Premium\
\
The additional return investors expect for holding assets that cannot be easily sold or converted to cash, typically 2-4% above comparable liquid investments.](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/illiquidity-premium)
[### Management Fee\
\
An annual fee charged by fund managers to cover operational expenses, typically 2% of committed capital during the investment period and 2% of invested capital thereafter.](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/management-fee)
[### Preferred Return (Hurdle Rate)\
\
The minimum annual return LPs must receive before the GP earns carried interest, typically set at 8% in private equity funds.](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/preferred-return)

Metrics
-------

[### Churn Rate\
\
The rate at which customers stop doing business with a company, a critical metric for recurring revenue businesses and a key due diligence focus area.](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/churn)

Deal Process
------------

[### Confidential Information Memorandum (CIM)\
\
A detailed document prepared by sellers or brokers providing comprehensive information about a business for sale, including financials, operations, and growth opportunities.](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/cim)
[### Deal Flow\
\
The pipeline of investment opportunities available to a buyer or fund, sourced through brokers, direct outreach, networks, and proprietary channels.](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/deal-flow)
[### Due Diligence\
\
The comprehensive investigation process where buyers verify business information, assess risks, and validate assumptions before completing an acquisition, typically taking 30-60 days.\
\
Also known as: Due Diligence Review, Business Investigation, Buyer Verification](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/due-diligence)
[### Escrow\
\
A neutral third-party arrangement where funds or documents are held until specific conditions are met, commonly used in M&A to hold purchase price deposits and holdbacks.](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/escrow)
[### Exclusivity Period\
\
A contractual period (typically 60-90 days) during which the seller agrees to negotiate only with one buyer, allowing focused due diligence without competition.](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/exclusivity)
[### Indication of Interest (IOI)\
\
A preliminary, non-binding proposal from a buyer expressing interest and approximate valuation range, typically submitted before receiving detailed financials.](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/ioi)
[### LOI (Letter of Intent)\
\
A preliminary agreement between buyer and seller outlining key deal terms including price, structure, timeline, and conditions before formal due diligence and definitive purchase agreement.\
\
Also known as: Letter of Interest, Term Sheet, Indication of Interest](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/loi)
[### Proprietary Deal\
\
An investment opportunity sourced directly without broker involvement, typically resulting in less competition and better pricing for the buyer.](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/proprietary-deal)

Risk Assessment
---------------

[### Customer Concentration\
\
A risk metric measuring revenue dependency on top customers. High concentration (>20% from one customer) is a red flag in acquisition due diligence.](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/customer-concentration)

Due Diligence
-------------

[### Data Room\
\
A secure repository for sharing confidential documents during M&A due diligence, typically organized by category with controlled access and activity tracking.](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/data-room)
[### Quality of Earnings (QoE)\
\
A comprehensive financial analysis that verifies the accuracy, sustainability, and cash generation ability of a target company's reported earnings, identifying adjustments that impact valuation and deal structure.\
\
Also known as: QoE, QofE Study, Financial Due Diligence, Earnings Quality Analysis](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/quality-of-earnings)

Financing
---------

[### Debt Service Coverage Ratio (DSCR)\
\
A lending metric measuring a business's ability to pay its debt obligations, calculated as cash flow divided by total debt service. SBA loans typically require 1.25x minimum.](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/dscr)
[### Personal Guarantee\
\
A legal commitment where a business owner agrees to be personally liable for business debt if the company cannot pay, commonly required for SBA loans.](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/personal-guarantee)
[### SBA Loan\
\
Government-backed financing that covers 70-90% of small business acquisition costs at below-market rates, requiring 10-15% buyer equity and personal guarantees from borrowers.\
\
Also known as: SBA 7(a) Loan, Small Business Administration Loan](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/sba-loan)
[### Seller Financing\
\
A deal structure where the business seller provides a loan to the buyer for 10-30% of the purchase price, typically offering better terms than traditional financing while ensuring seller confidence in the business.\
\
Also known as: Owner Financing, Seller Note, Seller Carryback](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/seller-financing)

Valuation Metrics
-----------------

[### EBITDA Multiple\
\
A valuation metric expressing enterprise value as a multiple of Earnings Before Interest, Taxes, Depreciation, and Amortization, commonly used to price small and medium-sized business acquisitions.\
\
Also known as: EBITDA Multiple, Earnings Multiple, Cash Flow Multiple, EV/EBITDA](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/ebitda-multiple)

Valuation
---------

[### Enterprise Value\
\
The total value of a business including both equity and debt, representing the price a buyer would pay to acquire 100% of the company's operations.](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/enterprise-value)
[### Multiple\
\
A valuation metric expressing business value as a multiplier of earnings (EBITDA or SDE), commonly ranging from 2-6x for SMB acquisitions.](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/multiple)

Business Strategy
-----------------

[### Entrepreneurship Through Acquisition (ETA)\
\
Business strategy where MBA-educated professionals acquire and operate existing profitable companies rather than starting new ventures, popularized by Harvard and Stanford business schools through search fund programs.\
\
Also known as: ETA, Buy-Then-Build, Acquisition Entrepreneurship, Search Fund Model](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/entrepreneurship-through-acquisition)

Investment Strategies
---------------------

[### ETA (Entrepreneurship Through Acquisition)\
\
A business strategy where entrepreneurs acquire existing companies rather than starting from scratch, reducing startup risk while gaining immediate cash flow and established operations.\
\
Also known as: Acquisition Entrepreneurship, Buy-Then-Build](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/eta)

Fund Structure
--------------

[### General Partner & Limited Partner (GP/LP)\
\
The two main parties in a PE fund structure: the GP manages investments and operations, while LPs provide capital and have limited liability and involvement.](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/gp-lp)

Deal Structure
--------------

[### Holdback\
\
A portion of the purchase price held in escrow after closing to cover potential post-closing adjustments, indemnification claims, or seller obligations.](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/holdback)
[### Holdco (Holding Company)\
\
A parent entity that owns controlling interests in one or more operating businesses, commonly used in search fund and SMB acquisition structures to separate ownership from operations.](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/holdco)
[### Rollover Equity\
\
When a seller reinvests a portion of sale proceeds into the acquiring entity, maintaining ownership stake and alignment with the buyer's success.](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/rollover-equity)
[### Stock Purchase\
\
An acquisition structure where the buyer purchases ownership shares directly from shareholders, acquiring all assets and liabilities of the entity.](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/stock-purchase)
[### Transition Period\
\
The time after closing when the seller assists the buyer in transitioning the business, typically 30-90 days but can extend to 12+ months for complex businesses.](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/transition-period)

Legal
-----

[### Indemnification\
\
Contractual protection where the seller agrees to compensate the buyer for losses arising from breaches of representations, warranties, or specific known issues.](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/indemnification)
[### Non-Compete Agreement\
\
A contractual restriction preventing the seller from competing with the acquired business for a specified time and geographic area, typically 3-5 years.](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/non-compete)
[### Non-Disclosure Agreement (NDA)\
\
A legal agreement protecting confidential business information shared during M&A discussions, required before accessing detailed company data.](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/nda)

Performance Metrics
-------------------

[### Internal Rate of Return (IRR)\
\
A metric measuring investment performance as an annualized rate of return, accounting for the timing and size of all cash flows. Search funds target 25-35% IRR.](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/irr)
[### J-Curve\
\
The typical return pattern of PE funds showing initial negative returns as fees exceed gains, followed by positive returns as investments mature and exit.](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/j-curve)
[### Multiple on Invested Capital (MOIC)\
\
A performance metric showing total return as a multiple of original investment, calculated as total value divided by invested capital. Search funds target 3-5x MOIC.](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/moic)

Legal and Documentation
-----------------------

[### Letter of Intent (LOI)\
\
A non-binding preliminary agreement outlining the key terms of a proposed acquisition, including price, structure, timeline, and due diligence requirements before executing a definitive purchase agreement.\
\
Also known as: LOI, Term Sheet, Memorandum of Understanding, Intent to Purchase](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/letter-of-intent)

Market Segments
---------------

[### Lower Middle Market\
\
Companies with $1-10 million in annual EBITDA, representing the sweet spot for search funds and smaller private equity investments with less institutional competition and higher growth potential.\
\
Also known as: Small-Mid Market, SMB Market, Lower Mid-Market, Smaller Middle Market](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/lower-middle-market)

Risk Management
---------------

[### Representations and Warranties Insurance (RWI)\
\
Insurance that covers losses from breaches of seller representations in M&A transactions, increasingly common in deals over $20M.](https://resources.smbinvestornetwork.com/glossary/rep-warranty-insurance)
