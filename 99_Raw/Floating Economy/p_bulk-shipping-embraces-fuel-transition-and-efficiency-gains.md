Bulk shipping embraces fuel transition and efficiency gains
--------------------------------------------------------------------------

Carriers modernize fleets as floating economy markets shift with global demand.

![](https://media.beehiiv.com/cdn-cgi/image/fit=scale-down,quality=80,format=auto,onerror=redirect/uploads/asset/file/a1a9d756-64cb-448a-9af0-4144893ffc18/IMG_3901_5ae402c9837860cd6e4f65e304b3a5fa.jpg)

Leopold retrofit. Photo by Oldendorff.

Vessel operators are under pressure to adapt. With trade lanes shifting and emission rules tightening, bulk shipping companies are testing new fuels, wind-assist, and fleet retrofits. In 2025, the world’s largest carriers are showing how modernization looks in practice.

*   **Oldendorff invests in fuel R&D.** In June 2025, Oldendorff launched a research center in Scotland to compare [alternative e-fuels](https://www.tradewindsnews.com/bulkers/oldendorff-carriers-shipping-research-centre-kicks-off-with-project-to-compare-e-fuels/2-1-1834573)
     for bulk shipping. The move reflects the company’s long-term decarbonization strategy.  
    
*   **Oldendorff maintains fleet with upgrades.** Oldendorff scheduled more than 60 vessels for [dry docking and retrofits](https://www.oldendorff.com/news/maintaining-fleet-performance-dry-docking-and-newbuilding-activity-in-2024-and-2025)
     in 2024–2025. The program includes upgrades tied to environmental compliance and operational efficiency.  
    
*   **Star Bulk posts 2024 results.** In February 2025, Star Bulk published its Q4 [2024 financial report](https://www.starbulk.com/media/uploads_file/2025/02/18/p1ikdf37bhc356es1jj91s8s1gte4.pdf)
    . The results highlight cost discipline, dry docking schedules, and efficiency measures across its large bulker fleet.  
    
*   **Diana Shipping adds methanol dual-fuel bulkers.** Diana Shipping confirmed two newbuild Kamsarmax bulk carriers with [methanol dual-fuel capability](https://www.bairdmaritime.com/shipping/dry-cargo/bulkers/diana-shipping-charters-out-post-panamax-bulker-to-oldendorff)
    , scheduled for delivery in 2027–2028. The deal signals momentum toward cleaner bulker designs.
    

**That’s not all:** analysts expect dry bulk to balance volatile trade flows with rapid technology shifts. Methanol-ready designs, fuel R&D, and fleet retrofits show how carriers are adapting to stricter emission rules.
