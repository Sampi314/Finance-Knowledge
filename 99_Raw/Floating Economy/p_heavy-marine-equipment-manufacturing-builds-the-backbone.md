Heavy marine equipment manufacturing builds the backbone
----------------------------------------------------------------------------

Shipyards and OEMs drive power, lifting, and propulsion in floating infrastructure.

![](https://media.beehiiv.com/cdn-cgi/image/fit=scale-down,quality=80,format=auto,onerror=redirect/uploads/asset/file/c4aa3d70-6949-444f-a6de-f321d908348a/Konecranes-Savannah-Georia.b0ee87.jpg)

Konecrane. Photo by The Maritime Executive.

Floating infrastructure—from platforms to ports—needs serious heavy machinery: engines, propulsion systems, cranes, and lifting gear. These manufacturers are the muscle behind The Floating Economy, enabling scale, power, and durability.

*   **MAN Energy / Everllence - Marine Propulsion & Engine Systems:** They produce [large two-stroke and four-stroke marine engines](https://man-es.com/docs/default-source/document-sync-archive/marine-engine-programme-2024-eng.pdf?sfvrsn=5a369d23_2)
    , hybrid propulsion solutions, exhaust after-treatment, and systems optimized for emissions compliance. Their product portfolio includes LNG systems and marine engines designed to reduce fuel consumption.  
    
*   **HD Hyundai Marine Engine / HHI - Global Leader in Engine Manufacturing:** HD Hyundai is among the top marine engine manufacturers, delivering large engines and components (crankshafts, turbochargers) with over [15 million horsepower](https://www.hyundai-ec.com/en/)
     produced in recent years. They’re moving into greener power solutions and expanding capacity.  
    
*   **Konecranes - Port & Shipyard Heavy Lifting Systems:** Konecranes builds shipyard cranes, container handling systems, ship-to-shore cranes, mobile harbor cranes, rubber-tyred gantry cranes. They are [expanding manufacturing partners](https://maritime-executive.com/article/konecranes-to-build-ports-cranes-in-the-u-s-to-loosen-china-s-monopoly)
     and building cranes locally in various markets.
    

**Heavy marine equipment may look old-school**, but it’s evolving. From cleaner fuel engines to high-precision cranes, these manufacturers determine how fast, how clean, and how well The Floating Economy can scale.
