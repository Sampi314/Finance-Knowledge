The Three Levels of Excel Skills
--------------------------------

[Excel skills](https://trumpexcel.com/excel-skills/)
 can be classified into three categories:

*   Basic
*   Intermediate
*   Advanced

It’s a good idea to assess your Excel skills and put yourself in the right bucket.

In this section, we will discuss these three levels of Excel skills and some things you should know if you consider yourself a part of that level.

### Basic Excel Skills

If you are new to Microsoft Excel, you will begin with basic skills.

With a basic knowledge of Excel, you’ll be able to manage raw data, create simple charts, and manipulate data tables.

These skills are crucial for data collection, organization, and analysis, as well as for carrying out daily tasks efficiently

They include:

*   Creating and saving worksheets
*   Entering and formatting data
*   Sorting and filtering data
*   Simple calculations, such as SUM and AVERAGE
*   Formatting cells (e.g., font, colors, borders)
*   Basic [keyboard shortcuts](https://trumpexcel.com/excel-keyboard-shortcuts/)
     such as Control C and Control V

Mastering basic Excel skills allows you to build a strong foundation, preparing you for more advanced knowledge in MS Excel.

By starting with the essentials, you’ll be better equipped to handle more complex tasks and challenges in the workplace, eventually becoming an expert in managing spreadsheets and working with large datasets.

### Intermediate Excel Skills

Intermediate Excel users are familiar with a wider range of tools and functions that enhance their ability to work with data.

Some of the intermediate Excel skills include:

*   Applying [conditional formatting](https://trumpexcel.com/excel-conditional-formatting/)
     to highlight specific cells
*   Split Data in a cell into multiple cells using the Text-to-Column
*   Creating and managing [drop-down lists](https://trumpexcel.com/excel-drop-down-list/)
     (and other data validation rules)
*   [Removing duplicate](https://trumpexcel.com/find-and-remove-duplicates-in-excel/)
     cells or records
*   Advanced uses of filtering/sorting to quickly sift through large datasets
*   Building basic charts such as line charts or [pie chart](https://trumpexcel.com/pie-chart/)
    
*   Using text functions (such as LEFT, MID, RIGHT, PROPER, LOWER, TEXT) to manipulate text data
*   Using functions such as COUNT, COUNTIF, SUMIF
*   Knowing how to protect your worksheets or workbooks

With intermediate Excel skills, you can handle more complex tasks and improve your efficiency in processing and analyzing data.

Mastering intermediate Excel skills enhances your data management and analysis efficiency, which leads to improved business decisions. Your skillset will become more versatile, making you a valuable asset to your team and enhancing your professional resume.

**Note**: It’s not necessary for you to know everything I have mentioned here to be an intermediate Excel user. Even if you know some of these, you can still call yourself an intermediate user. However, I would recommend you learn about all these, if possible, before appearing for a job interview.

### Advanced Excel Skills

Advanced Excel users are experts who can harness the full potential of the software. These skills include:

*   Implementing advanced functions, such as [XLOOKUP](https://trumpexcel.com/xlookup-function/)
    , [VLOOKUP](https://trumpexcel.com/excel-vlookup-function/)
    , [INDEX/MATCH](https://trumpexcel.com/index-match/)
    , [SUMIFS](https://trumpexcel.com/excel-sumifs-function/)
    /[COUNTIFS](https://trumpexcel.com/excel-countifs-function/)
    , for precise data retrieval
*   [Utilizing Pivot Tables](https://trumpexcel.com/pivot-table/)
     to analyze and summarize large datasets
*   [Creating interactive dashboards](https://trumpexcel.com/creating-excel-dashboard/)
     using Power Query or Formulas
*   Automating tasks with [VBA macro codes](https://trumpexcel.com/excel-macro-examples/)
    
*   Extract and Transform data using [Power Query](https://trumpexcel.com/power-query-course/)
    

These are just some of the advanced skills. If you’re comfortable with some or all the things I have mentioned above, you can call yourself an Excel user with Advanced skills.

Having advanced Excel skills can lead to increased earning potential, with professionals skilled in Excel earning an average salary $42k – $86k per annum, according to [PayScale’s salary analysis](https://www.payscale.com/research/US/Job=Data_Analyst/Salary/2cbd1f5a/Microsoft-Excel)
.

Honing your advanced Excel skills equips you to handle complex data management tasks, thereby enhancing your career prospects and creating new growth opportunities.

**Note**: No one expects you to know everything in Excel. So don’t think that you can only call yourself an advanced Excel user if you know all of it. You never will.

When describing your Excel skills in your resume, be sure to mention the specific skill level and relevant tasks or functions you are proficient in.

This will help potential employers understand your capabilities and determine if your skills align with their needs.

Demonstrating Excel Skills on Your Resume (Examples)
----------------------------------------------------

In this section, I have some examples of how you can describe an Excel skill you have in your resume.

### Example 1 – Showing Formula/Function Expertise

When listing Excel skills on your resume, it’s important to be specific about what you can do. For instance, if you’re proficient in creating formulas and functions, you could say:

_Proficient in utilizing Excel formulas and functions to analyze and manipulate data for various purposes, such as budget tracking and sales forecasting._

In this example, you clearly state your ability to work with Excel formulas and showcase how you can use this skill to solve real-world problems like budget tracking and sales forecasting.

### Example 2 – Showing Expertise in Creating Charts

If you have worked with charts, that would be a good thing to mention, as it can help you stand out from other people.

_Experienced in creating visually appealing and informative charts and graphs in Excel to present data in an easily understandable format._

In this statement, you emphasize your ability to present data effectively using Excel’s charting and graphing features, making it clear that you can communicate important information to your team.

If you have created advanced charts such as combination charts, gauge charts, or bullet charts, you can consider mentioning them.

### **Example** 3 – Showing Expertise in Using Pivot Tables

Highlighting your proficiency in using Pivot Tables in Excel can set you apart from other candidates. For example:

_Adept at utilizing Excel PivotTables to summarize, analyze, explore, and present summary data. Proficient in using various features in Pivot Tables, including slicers and Pivot Charts._

This example demonstrates your ability to use PivotTables to analyze and interpret data, which can help in making strategic decisions in areas such as marketing and inventory.

### Example 4 – Showing Your VBA and Automation Expertise

Demonstrating your knowledge in Excel VBA (Visual Basic for Applications) can also be an asset. For example:

_Skilled in developing and implementing Excel VBA scripts to automate repetitive tasks and processes, significantly reducing the time and effort required in data analysis and reporting._

In this example, you’re emphasizing your ability to automate tasks using VBA, which is essential for increasing efficiency in managing and analyzing data.

If you have specific examples that can quantify the effort you saved because of your VBA scripts, that would be a good thing.

These are just some of the examples, and you can come up with similar sentences that you can use in your resume. Ensure that you tailor your Excel skills to the job role so that it feels that your skills can immediately be put to use.

What All Places Can You Show Your Excel Skills in Resume?
---------------------------------------------------------

Effectively showcasing your Excel skills in your resume involves incorporating them in various sections, such as the skills section, work experience, and certifications.

Strategically positioning your Excel skills throughout your resume helps potential employers quickly identify your expertise, thereby boosting your chances of scoring an interview.

We’ll examine various methods to incorporate your Excel skills into your professional resume.

### Skills Section

Include a dedicated skills section in your resume to highlight your Excel proficiency, listing specific functions and techniques you’re familiar with.

Being precise and providing details about your Excel skills gives potential employers a clear insight into your capabilities.

For example, instead of simply stating that you have “strong Excel skills,” you could mention that you’re proficient in creating Pivot Tables, using data validation, and automating tasks.

In addition to showcasing your Excel skills, the skills section is also an opportunity to include any Excel courses you’ve taken.

This demonstrates your commitment to learning and staying up-to-date with the latest software tools, making you an attractive candidate for potential employers.

### Work Experience Section

Use the work experience section to provide clear examples of how you’ve applied your Excel skills in previous roles, demonstrating their relevance to the position you’re applying for.

For instance, if you were responsible for creating spreadsheets, analyzing data, and creating charts and graphs in a previous role, be sure to include these tasks in your work experience section to showcase your expertise in Excel.

Offering concrete examples of your Excel skills application in the workplace not only validates your proficiency but also depicts your ability to employ these skills in real-world scenarios.

This can go a long way in convincing potential employers that you’re the right candidate for the job.

### Excel Certifications and Courses

Mention any Excel certifications or courses you’ve completed to showcase your commitment to learning and improving your skills.

Examples of certifications include Certifications for Microsoft Office Specialist (MOS) and Microsoft Excel Expert (MEE), while Excel courses can be offered by universities and online learning platforms.

Including these certifications and courses in your resume demonstrates your dedication to developing and honing your Excel skills, which can be beneficial to potential employers.

To include certifications and courses, showcase your skills on your resume by listing them in the skills section, accompanied by a brief description of the certification or course.

You can also showcase your Excel certifications on your LinkedIn profile, further enhancing your professional online presence.

Useful Tips When Describing Your Excel Skills in a Resume
---------------------------------------------------------

1.  **Create a Skills Section**: Include a dedicated “Skills” section near the top of your resume to highlight your Excel proficiency.
2.  **Be Hones**t: Always be honest about your Excel skills. Avoid exaggerating your abilities, as you may be tested on them during the interview.
3.  **Use Relevant Keywords**: Tailor your skills to match the specific job description. Use keywords from the job posting to make your resume more attractive to automated applicant tracking systems (ATS) and human recruiters.
4.  **Specify Your Level of Proficiency**: Mention your Excel proficiency level (e.g., beginner, intermediate, advanced) to give employers a clear idea of your abilities.
5.  **Highlight Relevant Excel Functions**: Mention specific Excel functions or features you are proficient in, such as PivotTables, VLOOKUP, HLOOKUP, SUMIF, IF statements, data analysis, data visualization, macros, or custom formulas.
6.  **Provide Real-life Examples**: If possible, provide examples of how you’ve used Excel in previous roles. Describe projects, reports, or tasks where you utilized Excel to solve problems or improve processes.
7.  **Certifications and Training**: If you have relevant certifications (e.g., Microsoft Excel Specialist) or have completed Excel training courses, include them in your resume. Mentioning these can boost your credibility.
8.  **Quantify Achievements**: Whenever possible, quantify your achievements related to Excel. For instance, you could mention that you improved a reporting process, reduced errors, or saved time by a specific percentage through your Excel skills.
9.  **Showcase Excel in Action**: If you have a personal website, blog, or online portfolio, consider creating Excel-related content or showcasing Excel projects to demonstrate your skills.

Things to Avoid When Describing Excel Skills on Resume
------------------------------------------------------

1.  **Being Vague:** Don’t be too general or vague; specify which functions and features you are proficient in, e.g., pivot tables, VLOOKUP, macros, etc.
2.  **Overstating Skills:** Don’t exaggerate your proficiency; be honest about your skill level to set appropriate expectations.
3.  **Using Buzzwords Without Context:** Avoid using buzzwords or jargon without providing context on how you’ve applied these skills in real-world situations. For example, don’t talk about using Python in Excel or Office Scripts in Excel if you have no idea how to do that
4.  **Listing Basic Skills when the Job Demands Advanced Skills:** If you are applying for a position that requires advanced Excel skills, listing only basic skills such as data entry or formatting may make your resume look weak.
5.  **Ignoring the Relevance:** Don’t list Excel skills if they are not relevant to the position you’re applying for, as they may appear as filler.

Frequently Asked Questions About Showing MS Excel Skills on Resume
------------------------------------------------------------------

Below are some common questions I get about using Excel skills in resumes.

### How can I showcase my Excel proficiency levels?

Rather than just listing “Excel” among your skills, differentiate them by adding the level of proficiency, such as basic, intermediate, or advanced.

Use action verbs and provide concrete examples to demonstrate your expertise in various Excel tasks.

For instance, mention how you’ve used Excel to analyze data, save time, or streamline processes.

### Which Excel functions should be mentioned on a resume?

The Excel functions you should mention depending on the job requirements and the level of your expertise.

At a minimum, include basic functions like SUM, AVERAGE, MIN, MAX, and IF.

For more advanced users, mention functions like XLOOKUP, VLOOKUP, HLOOKUP, INDEX, MATCH, TEXTSPLIT, FILTER and other specialized functions relevant to your industry.

If you mention a function’s name, make sure that you actually know about that function and have a few examples in mind that you can explain this function with. Nothing is more damning than the interviewer asking you about a. Excel function that you have mentioned and you not being able to explain.

### How can I highlight Excel skills relevant to specific job roles?

First, analyze the job description and identify the Excel skills required for the role. Use keywords from the job posting to tailor your resume.

For example, if a job requires data analysis, mention your ability to create Pivot tables and use Excel formulas to analyze large datasets and come up with insights. Similarly, if the job involves financial planning, emphasize your skills in budgeting, forecasting, and financial modeling using Excel.

### What are some ways to demonstrate Excel expertise in a resume?

To demonstrate your Excel expertise, use quantitative examples that showcase the impact of your Excel skills on company operations.

Highlight any improvements, time savings, or cost reductions resulting from your Excel work. For example, saying that you created VBA automation that saved at least 100 man-hours in a project is a lot more impactful than saying you created automation that saved time.

### How can I convey my Excel problem-solving abilities on a resume?

To convey your Excel problem-solving abilities, provide specific instances when you have used Excel to solve a problem, complete a project, or meet a deadline.

Emphasize your analytical skills, adaptability, and resourcefulness. Mention how you have used Excel to create custom solutions, integrate data from various sources, or automate routine tasks to save time and enhance productivity.

For example, if you are applying for a job in the human resource and recruiting field, you can mention that you created Excel templates that were able to improve the workflow and manage the candidates better.
