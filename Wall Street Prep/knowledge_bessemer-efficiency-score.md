What is the Bessemer Efficiency Score?
--------------------------------------

The **Bessemer Efficiency Score** is a measure of capital allocation and spending habits to determine the efficiency at which a SaaS company is growing.

![Bessemer Efficiency Score](https://media.wallstreetprep.com/uploads/2022/07/19012359/Bessemer-Efficiency-Score-Formula.jpg)

Bessemer Efficiency Score Framework
-----------------------------------

Coined by [Bessemer Venture Partners](https://www.bvp.com/)
, the BVP Efficiency Score is a framework for evaluating a company’s spending efficiency.

Unlike many early-stage metrics used in [venture capital](https://www.wallstreetprep.com/knowledge/venture-capital-diligence/)
, the efficiency score is unique in that it is more about tracking a company’s spending habits rather than solely its revenue [growth](https://www.wallstreetprep.com/knowledge/growth-rate/)
, i.e. the ”top line”.

While the growth at all costs mindset is common during bull markets — where [profitability](https://www.wallstreetprep.com/knowledge/profit-margin/)
 is not the near-term priority and is instead deferred to the future — issues emerge in bear markets when the capital markets dry up.

A single-minded focus on growth, in lieu of operating efficiency and discipline, eventually catches up with these growth-oriented companies.

Over the long run, efficient growth is more stable and reliable than growth that comes with [unsustainable spending](https://www.wallstreetprep.com/knowledge/cash-runway/)
.

If the economy were to undergo a contraction, companies with inefficient spending and poor [capital allocation](https://www.wallstreetprep.com/knowledge/capital-allocation/)
 would struggle to remain afloat (and would need to raise more outside capital).

BVP Efficiency Score
--------------------

The BVP scoring system is segmented into three primary classifications:

1.  Good → <0.5x
2.  Better → 0.5x to 1.5x
3.  Best → >1.5x

[![BVP Efficiency Score Framework](https://media.wallstreetprep.com/uploads/2022/07/19012402/BVP-Efficiency-Score-Framework.jpg)](https://media.wallstreetprep.com/uploads/2022/07/19012402/BVP-Efficiency-Score-Framework.jpg)

BVP Efficiency Score (Source: [State of the Cloud Report](https://www.bvp.com/atlas/state-of-the-cloud-2019)
)

Bessemer Efficiency Score Formula
---------------------------------

In order to calculate the efficiency score, the company’s net new annual [recurring revenue](https://www.wallstreetprep.com/knowledge/recurring-revenue/)
 ([ARR](https://www.wallstreetprep.com/knowledge/annual-recurring-revenue-arr/)
) is divided by its [net burn](https://www.wallstreetprep.com/knowledge/burn-rate/)
.

The formula for calculating the Bessemer Efficiency Score is as follows.

> ###### Bessemer Efficiency Score Formula
> 
> *   Bessemer Efficiency Score = Net New ARR ÷ Net Burn

Where:

*   Net New ARR = New ARR + Expansion ARR – Churned ARR
*   Net Burn = Revenue – Operating Expenses

Note that the calculation above is for early-stage startups generating less than $30 million in ARR.

Bessemer Efficiency Score Example Calculation
---------------------------------------------

Suppose a cloud computing company started 2021 with $12 million in beginning annual recurring revenue (ARR).

In the same period, the new ARR from new customers was $10 million, the expansion ARR from existing customers was $4 million, and the churned ARR was $2 million.

*   Beginning ARR = $12 million
*   New ARR = $10 million
*   Expansion ARR = $4 million
*   Churned ARR = –$2 million

The ending ARR for the period is, therefore, $24 million.

*   Ending ARR = $12 million + $10 million + $4 million – $2 million = $24 million

Our next step is to calculate the net new ARR, which is the sum of new ARR and expansion ARR, minus churned ARR.

*   Net New ARR = $10 million + $4 million – $2 million = $12 million

The final assumption needed is the net burn for the corresponding period, which we’ll assume to be $2 million.

With all our inputs ready, we can divide the net new ARR by the net burn to arrive at a BVP efficiency score of 6.0x.

*   BVP Efficiency Score = $12 million ÷ $2 million = 6.0x
*   Implied Growth Benchmark = Best
