Breaking into Portfolio Management
----------------------------------

Portfolio managers typically begin their careers as junior analysts, where they focus on researching new investment opportunities. They then move into senior analyst roles, covering securities on watch lists, generating investment ideas, and maintaining analysis of portfolio companies. After several years of experience, they progress to deputy or associate portfolio management positions, managing specific sections of a portfolio, before ultimately becoming portfolio managers with full responsibility.

Core Skills Covered in The Portfolio Manager Certification
----------------------------------------------------------

Financial Edge’s comprehensive portfolio management self-study course covers both the fundamentals of the buy-side and advanced portfolio construction. It is designed to develop theoretical and practical knowledge surrounding the investment management process and to give participants the skills needed to design investment portfolios for different client needs.

1.  ### The Asset Management Industry
    

*   Learn the mechanics of the investment management industry from both the perspective of clients investing money and the portfolio manager making investment decisions.
*   Learn the various objectives a client may have, the different types of fund structures, the implications of diversification, and liability-driven investing.
*   Differentiate between active and passive investment strategies.

2.  ### Fundamental Accounting
    

*   Develop a strong fundamental understanding of the components of a company’s financial statements.
*   Build a comprehensive understanding of the essential techniques required to accurately analyze the financial results of a company.

3.  ### Fundamental Modeling
    

*   Learn how to build models quickly and error-free, which are critical skills for making decisions and recommendations.
*   Develop the skills needed to understand the key drivers of a model’s outputs.

4.  ### Fundamental Valuation
    

*   Learn how and when to utilize key valuation methodologies.
*   Develop the skills needed to accurately and quickly interpret outputs from valuation models.

5.  ### Economic Analysis and Asset Allocation
    

*   Learn the macroeconomic data points a portfolio manager needs to consider when making investment decisions.
*   Understand how portfolio managers combine economic expectations with clients’ objectives and constraints to develop an asset allocation strategy.
*   Explore the impact of the economy, the business cycle, central banks, regulation, and general market trends on investment decisions.

6.  ### Security Selection
    

*   Learn about the process a portfolio manager uses to make investment decisions.
*   Learn how portfolio managers decide which individual securities to hold within their portfolios, including specific considerations across equities, fixed income, alternatives, and foreign exchange.
*   Learn about technical analysis approaches to making investment decisions.

7.  ### Financial Markets for Portfolio Management
    

*   Learn how modern financial markets work and how key investment instruments are priced and traded.
*   Explore equities, bonds, derivatives, and the full trade lifecycle to understand how market structure, pricing, and execution shape portfolio decisions.

8.  ### Performance Measurement
    

*   Learn how the performance of portfolio managers can be assessed.
*   Investigate the benchmarks against which portfolio performance can be judged, and the various approaches to assessing risk-adjusted performance.
*   Determine how returns generated by a portfolio can be broken down into different sources.
