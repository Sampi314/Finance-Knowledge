Covers the mid-year convention in DCF modeling, explaining how it adjusts discount periods (e.g., from 1.0, 2.0 to 0.5, 1.5) to reflect that cash flows typically arrive evenly throughout the year rather than entirely at year-end. It also covers how this interacts with stub periods and how to correctly apply the mid-year convention when calculating terminal value using the perpetuity growth method vs the multiples method.
